## üìö Courses, Curriculum & Teaching Style

The Shizuoka Japanese Language Institute primarily offers specialized, long-term preparatory courses designed explicitly for students intending to matriculate into Japanese universities, graduate schools, or specialized vocational colleges. The main offerings include the **2-Year University Preparation Course** (starting in April) and the **1.5-Year University Preparation Course** (starting in October). These tracks are structured to take students from foundational Japanese skills (or basic proficiency upon entry) up to the advanced level required for competitive admissions (JLPT N1 equivalent).

### Specialized Curricular Tracks

What sets the curriculum apart is its granular focus on specialized academic streams. After achieving a foundational competence in general Japanese (typically within the first 6 to 9 months), students are streamed into intensive pathways:

1.  **EJU Liberal Arts Track:** Focuses on Japanese reading comprehension, critical writing, Social Sciences (History, Geography, Politics, Economics), and EJU Mathematics Course 1. This track is designed for students targeting faculties like Law, Economics, Literature, and Humanities.
2.  **EJU Science (STEM) Track (Rikei):** This is a highly demanding stream requiring specialized faculty. Students focus intensively on EJU Mathematics Course 2, and advanced sciences, typically selecting two out of Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. The school provides targeted instruction in the scientific terminology and methodology necessary to compete for admission to engineering, medical, or science faculties at prestigious national and public universities.
3.  **Art and Design Preparation Track:** Recognizing the unique admission requirements of art universities, this stream combines high-level Japanese language training with portfolio development support. The curriculum includes specialized Japanese vocabulary related to aesthetics, materials, and art history, while also providing guidance on the practical steps required for submitting portfolios and preparing for specialized entrance examinations or interviews common in Japanese art institutions.

### Teaching Methodology and Integration

Teaching methods blend traditional, highly structured grammar and kanji acquisition with modern, communicative strategies. Classes are intensive, typically running five days a week with multiple sessions dedicated to different skill areas: reading, writing, listening, speaking (conversation), and specialized academic subjects. The curriculum is meticulously paced to align with JLPT and EJU testing dates.

*   **Integrated EJU Preparation:** Subject courses (Math, Science, Social Studies) are taught in Japanese, maximizing the student‚Äôs exposure to academic Japanese within the context of the subjects they will be tested on. Frequent mock exams and individualized feedback sessions are integral to this preparation.
*   **Conversation (Kaiwa) Focus:** Recognizing that EJU does not test oral ability, dedicated conversation classes run parallel to the academic stream. The school integrates Business Japanese modules early on for students considering vocational schools or seeking part-time employment, covering essential Japanese workplace etiquette, formal language (Keigo), and presentation skills.
*   **Faculty Expertise:** The institute ensures that the faculty includes teachers specialized not just in Japanese pedagogy, but also in the specific academic subjects (STEM, Social Sciences) required for EJU preparation, guaranteeing the quality and relevance of the preparatory education provided. The commitment to specialized tracks (STEM, Art) requires highly diversified teaching expertise, which the Shizuoka Japanese Language Institute prioritizes to maintain its reputation for successful student placement.
