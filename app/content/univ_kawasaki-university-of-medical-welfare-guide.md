## 🏫 University Overview

Kawasaki University of Medical Welfare (KUMW), located in the scenic and historic city of Kurashiki, Okayama Prefecture, is a prestigious private institution dedicated to the fields of medicine, social welfare, and health sciences. Founded in 1991 as part of the renowned Kawasaki Gakuen educational group, it was the first university in Japan to carry the term "Medical Welfare" in its name. The university operates under the founding philosophy of "Humanity and Service," aiming to cultivate professionals who possess not only advanced technical skills but also a deep sense of compassion and ethical responsibility.

For international students, KUMW represents a unique opportunity to enter the specialized world of Japanese healthcare and welfare systems. As Japan continues to lead the world in managing an aging population, the expertise developed at this university—ranging from advanced nursing and physical therapy to social work and medical informatics—is increasingly relevant on a global scale. The university is physically and educationally integrated with the Kawasaki Medical School and its affiliated hospitals, providing a clinical training environment that is virtually unmatched in the Chugoku and Shikoku regions of Japan.

## 🎓 Academic Faculties and Specialized Departments

The university is structured into five primary faculties, each offering a range of departments that cater to the diverse needs of the modern healthcare industry.

### 1. Faculty of Medical Welfare
This is the cornerstone of the university. It focuses on the complex intersection of healthcare and social services. The **Department of Medical Welfare** trains students to become social workers and psychiatric social workers, emphasizing the 'Total Care' approach. There is also a strong focus on **Child Management and Education**, preparing students to support the developmental needs of children in both clinical and community settings. For international students, this faculty offers deep insights into the Japanese 'Kaigo' and welfare infrastructure.

### 2. Faculty of Health Sciences
This faculty is dedicated to the technical and clinical aspects of healthcare. It includes the **Department of Nursing**, which is highly competitive and boasts state-of-the-art simulation labs. The **Department of Medical Technology** and **Department of Clinical Engineering** train students to handle the sophisticated diagnostic and life-support equipment found in modern hospitals. International students interested in the high-tech side of Japanese medicine will find these programs particularly rewarding.

### 3. Faculty of Rehabilitation
With the global demand for rehabilitation services skyrocketing, this faculty provides intensive training in **Physical Therapy (PT)**, **Occupational Therapy (OT)**, and **Speech-Language-Hearing Therapy (ST)**. The curriculum is heavily weighted toward practical clinical placement, ensuring that students are ready for the workforce immediately upon graduation. The pass rates for the Japanese National Exams in these fields at KUMW consistently exceed the national average.

### 4. Faculty of Health and Sports Management
Recognizing that prevention is as important as cure, this faculty combines sports science with health management. It prepares health fitness programmers and sports instructors who can work in community health centers, corporate wellness programs, or professional sports organizations. It is an excellent choice for students looking to bridge the gap between athletic performance and clinical health.

### 5. Faculty of Medical IT
In an era of Digital Transformation (DX), the **Department of Medical Informatics** and **Department of Medical Management** are crucial. These programs teach students how to manage electronic health records, analyze medical big data, and optimize hospital administration. This faculty is ideal for students with an interest in technology who want to apply their skills to improve healthcare efficiency.

## 🏥 The Kawasaki Gakuen Advantage: Clinical Practice

One of the most significant advantages of studying at Kawasaki University of Medical Welfare is its membership in the Kawasaki Gakuen group. This group includes the **Kawasaki Medical School Hospital** and the **Kawasaki Hospital**, both of which serve as the primary training grounds for students. 

Unlike many other universities where students must find their own clinical placements, KUMW students have guaranteed access to high-level clinical practice within the same campus or nearby group facilities. This 'Interprofessional Education' (IPE) model allows nursing, therapy, and welfare students to collaborate in real-world medical teams, mirroring the 'Team Medical Care' environment used in professional Japanese hospitals. For a foreign student, this immersion provides invaluable networking opportunities and a direct look at the high standards of Japanese patient care.

## 🌏 Support for International Students

While the university is primarily Japanese-speaking, it has shown a growing commitment to internationalization. Support services for international students include:

- **Japanese Language Support:** While degree programs are conducted in Japanese, the university provides assistance for students to improve their technical medical Japanese, which is essential for passing national licensure exams.
- **Scholarship Opportunities:** Students may apply for the Japan Student Services Organization (JASSO) scholarships, as well as local government and private foundation grants. The university also has merit-based tuition reduction schemes for high-achieving students.
- **Housing Assistance:** The university helps international students find suitable accommodation in Kurashiki, ranging from student dormitories to affordable private apartments. The cost of living in Okayama is significantly lower than in Tokyo or Osaka, making it an attractive destination for self-funded students.
- **Career Guidance:** The Career Support Center provides intensive training for job hunting in Japan, including resume writing workshops and mock interviews specifically tailored for the healthcare sector.

## 🏘️ Life in Kurashiki, Okayama

Kurashiki is one of Japan's most beautiful and livable cities. The university is located near the Nakasho Station area, providing easy access to both Kurashiki City center and Okayama City. 

- **Cultural Richness:** The famous 'Bikan Historical Quarter' with its white-walled storehouses and willow-lined canals is just a short train ride away. It offers a peaceful environment for students to relax and soak in Japanese culture.
- **Climate:** Known as the 'Land of Sunshine,' Okayama Prefecture enjoys a mild climate with very little rainfall, making it a comfortable place for students from various parts of the world.
- **Safe Environment:** As a smaller city compared to the major metropolises, Kurashiki is exceptionally safe and has a friendly, community-oriented atmosphere that is perfect for focused academic study.

## 📝 Admission Process for International Applicants

Admission for international students typically requires a high level of Japanese proficiency, usually N2 or N1 on the JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test), as the courses are rigorous and involve complex medical terminology. The process generally involves:

1.  **EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission):** Testing Japanese language skills and basic academic knowledge.
2.  **University Entrance Exam:** Specific subject tests depending on the chosen faculty (e.g., Biology or Chemistry for Health Sciences).
3.  **Interview:** A crucial part of the process where the university assesses the applicant's motivation, communication skills, and suitability for a career in the medical and welfare fields.

International students are encouraged to contact the admissions office at least a year in advance to understand the specific requirements for their country of origin and to ensure all documentation for the student visa (COE) is prepared correctly.

## 🎯 Why Choose Kawasaki University of Medical Welfare?

Choosing KUMW means choosing a path of professional stability and social impact. Japan's healthcare sector is one of the most advanced in the world, and there is a critical shortage of qualified professionals in nursing, rehabilitation, and social welfare. Graduating from this university not only provides a high-quality education but also grants eligibility for the Japanese National Examinations. 

For an international student, obtaining a Japanese national license in a field like Physical Therapy or Nursing is a 'golden ticket' to long-term employment and residency in Japan. The university’s nearly 100% employment rate for its graduates is a testament to its reputation among Japanese employers. If you are dedicated to helping others and want to master the art of Japanese medical welfare, Kawasaki University of Medical Welfare is an outstanding choice that will challenge you and reward you with a lifelong career.
