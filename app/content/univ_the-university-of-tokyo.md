## üè´ University Overview

The University of Tokyo (Êù±‰∫¨Â§ßÂ≠¶, T≈çky≈ç Daigaku), often shortened to Todai (Êù±Â§ß, T≈çdai), is a prestigious public research university located in Tokyo, Japan. Established in 1877 as the first imperial university, it is widely regarded as the most selective and respected university in Japan and one of the most prominent research universities in the world. The university has a rich history of producing leaders in various fields, including politics, business, science, and academia. Its alumni include numerous prime ministers, Nobel laureates, and prominent business figures, solidifying its reputation as a breeding ground for future leaders and innovators.

Todai's foundation lies in the integration of several older institutions of higher learning, including the Shogunate's Shoheizaka Gakumonsho, the Kaisei School, and the Tokyo Medical School. This historical foundation has allowed the university to maintain a diverse range of academic disciplines from its inception.

The university emphasizes research and innovation, consistently ranking among the top universities globally for research output and impact. It actively promotes international collaboration and attracts talented students and researchers from around the world. The University of Tokyo is a member of the Association of Pacific Rim Universities (APRU) and the IARU (International Alliance of Research Universities).

Todai's main campus is located in Hongo, Tokyo, a historic and vibrant area that contributes to the university's unique atmosphere. The campus features a blend of traditional and modern architecture, providing a stimulating environment for learning and research. Beyond Hongo, the university maintains several other campuses throughout Tokyo, including Komaba, Kashiwa, and Shirokanedai, each housing specific faculties and research centers.

## üåç International Student Support

The University of Tokyo is committed to providing a supportive environment for international students. A range of services are available to assist international students with their academic, personal, and professional development.

*   **Dormitory:** The university offers dormitory accommodation for international students, although availability can be limited. Priority is often given to newly arrived students. Information on available dormitories and application procedures can be found on the university's international student support website.
*   **English Programs:** While many courses are taught in Japanese, the University of Tokyo offers a growing number of programs taught entirely in English, particularly at the graduate level. These programs cater to international students who may not have advanced Japanese language proficiency.
*   **Career Support:** The university's career services provide guidance and resources to help international students find internships and employment opportunities in Japan and abroad. They offer workshops on resume writing, interview skills, and job searching, as well as networking events with potential employers.
*   **Scholarships:** A variety of scholarships are available to international students, including scholarships offered by the Japanese government (MEXT), private foundations, and the University of Tokyo itself. Information on scholarship eligibility and application procedures can be found on the university's website.
*   **Tutoring Services:** Tutoring services are offered in both Japanese and English, providing academic support to students who need help with specific subjects.
*   **Visa Support:** The International Support Office assists international students with visa applications and renewals.
