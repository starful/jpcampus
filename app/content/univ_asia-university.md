## üåç International Student Support

Asia University is deeply committed to supporting its international student community, recognizing the unique challenges and opportunities they face. The university provides a comprehensive suite of services designed to ensure a smooth transition and a fulfilling academic and social life in Japan.

**Housing:** The university operates dedicated dormitories for international students, offering a secure, comfortable, and affordable living environment. These dormitories facilitate cultural exchange among students from various backgrounds and are often conveniently located near campus or with easy access via public transport. Information on application procedures and fees is typically available on the international admissions website.

**English-Taught Programs:** For students who prefer to pursue their degrees primarily in English, Asia University offers specific degree programs. These include the Department of Business Strategy in the Faculty of Business Administration and the Department of Global Communication in the Faculty of International Relations. These programs cater to a global audience, eliminating the immediate need for advanced Japanese proficiency at the outset of studies, while also encouraging Japanese language acquisition during the program.

**Career Support:** A dedicated career center provides comprehensive assistance to international students seeking employment in Japan or abroad. Services include resume writing workshops, interview coaching, job fair organization, and guidance on navigating the Japanese job market. The university leverages its strong industry connections to help students secure internships and post-graduation employment opportunities.

**Japanese Language & Cultural Support:** While English-taught programs are available, the university encourages and provides resources for international students to learn Japanese. This includes Japanese language classes at various proficiency levels, as well as cultural orientation programs and events designed to help students adapt to Japanese society and build a strong sense of community. Mentorship programs and student advisors are also available to provide academic and personal support.
