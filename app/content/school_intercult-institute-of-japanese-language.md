## üìö Courses, Curriculum & Teaching Style

The core offering at Intercultural Institute of Japanese Language is the **Comprehensive University Progression Course (Á∑èÂêàÈÄ≤Â≠¶Ë™≤Á®ã)**. This track is specialized and intensive, running in various lengths to accommodate different intake periods and pre-existing Japanese language levels, ranging from the standard two-year course (April intake) down to 1 year and 3 months (January intake). The curriculum is meticulously structured, moving systematically from foundational grammar and communication skills to advanced academic language and specialized examination preparation.

The curriculum is rigorously divided into six distinct levels, ensuring students are placed accurately and progress logically. The initial stages (beginner to intermediate) focus heavily on building a solid foundation in all four core skills: listening, speaking, reading, and writing. However, as students transition into the upper-intermediate and advanced levels (N3 to N1 equivalent), the focus rapidly shifts toward academic application. Lessons at this stage frequently incorporate complex materials, including excerpts from university lectures, newspaper editorials, and academic texts, preparing students for the intellectual environment of tertiary education.

A key feature of the curriculum is the integrated **EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission)** preparation. Intercull offers dedicated elective classes and supplementary materials specifically targeting the three main components of the EJU: Japanese Language (Nihongo), Mathematics (Course 1 or 2), and General Studies (for humanities/social sciences). The EJU Nihongo section is taught not just for communicative fluency but for the specific demands of the test, including rapid reading comprehension and precise nuanced understanding. Similarly, the Mathematics and General Studies classes are taught in Japanese, enabling students to master the required content while simultaneously expanding their academic Japanese vocabulary‚Äîa dual benefit essential for success.

Intercull employs a communicative and task-based teaching methodology (TBI), even within its academic structure. While the end goal is progression, the process emphasizes active learning. Classrooms are often lively environments where students engage in debates, prepare and deliver presentations, and write complex reports and essays under instructor guidance. This active style ensures that students do not just passively absorb grammar but can actively deploy complex Japanese in analytical and argumentative contexts, a skill highly valued in university entrance interviews and seminar classes.

The pedagogical approach includes frequent mock examinations, not only for the JLPT and EJU but also internal university-style placement tests. Detailed feedback and performance analysis are standard, allowing instructors to identify specific weaknesses and tailor corrective measures. For instance, a student struggling with the abstract concepts in EJU General Studies will receive specific reading assignments and discussion opportunities to reinforce understanding, while essay writing practice includes multiple revision cycles with personalized critiques focused on logical flow, structure, and academic formality.

Furthermore, the academic timetable is carefully balanced. While the core hours are dedicated to general Japanese instruction, specialized afternoon sessions are mandated for EJU preparation, essay writing workshops, or research methodology classes, depending on the student‚Äôs goal (undergraduate or graduate school). This ensures maximum efficiency in the limited time available before application deadlines, providing a holistic and intensive preparation platform that addresses both linguistic skill gaps and examination readiness.

## üéì University & Career Pathways

Intercull is explicitly designed as a pathway school, and its entire administrative and teaching staff is deeply versed in the complexities of the Japanese university admission system. Success is measured not just by a student‚Äôs JLPT or EJU score, but by their acceptance into their desired higher education institution. The progression support is exceptionally detailed and spans several critical areas.

**1. EJU and JLPT Strategy:** The curriculum is calibrated to ensure students are prepared to take the necessary standardized tests at optimal timing. Specialized small-group EJU classes target specific weak points, utilizing past papers and sophisticated analysis tools to predict performance. Furthermore, mock interviews are conducted with faculty members who have deep experience in university admissions, often simulating the high-pressure environment of prestigious university entrance interviews.

**2. Personalized Counseling:** Every student aiming for higher education is assigned a dedicated academic counselor. This relationship is continuous, involving monthly check-ins to monitor academic progress, review university requirements, and refine application strategies. Counselors assist with the daunting task of selecting suitable universities based on the student's academic profile and career goals, ensuring the choices are realistic yet ambitious. **3. Application Documentation Support:** A major hurdle for international students is preparing flawless application materials. Intercull provides extensive assistance with writing research proposals, Statement of Purpose essays, and personal essays required by universities. Instructors provide multiple rounds of critical feedback, focusing on not just linguistic accuracy but also adherence to formal Japanese academic writing conventions, which differ significantly from Western styles. This support significantly reduces the risk of administrative errors that could jeopardize an application.

**4. This dedicated support system ensures that students leave Intercull not just with language certification, but with a fully packaged, highly competitive university application portfolio ready for submission.

**Dormitory Options:** Intercull typically partners with external management companies to offer shared and private room options located within a reasonable commuting distance to the school in Taito-ku. **Initial Settlement Assistance:** For all students, whether in a dormitory or private housing, the school provides crucial initial settlement support. This includes mandatory orientation sessions covering Japanese daily life etiquette, emergency procedures, and essential administrative tasks such as registering at the local ward office, opening a bank account, and securing national health insurance. This systematic guidance is vital for navigating the complex bureaucracy of settling in Japan.

**Ongoing Welfare and Emergency Support:** Given the high-pressure academic environment, student welfare is prioritized. The school administration maintains open communication channels for students to report difficulties, whether academic, financial, or personal. Emergency support is available for health issues or unexpected crises. Furthermore, administrative staff are experienced in guiding students through visa renewal procedures, a complex but mandatory process that requires meticulous timing and documentation, ensuring their status in Japan remains secure throughout their studies.
