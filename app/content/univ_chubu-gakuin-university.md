## 🏫 University Overview

Chubu Gakuin University (CGU) is a prestigious private institution located in the heart of Japan—Gifu Prefecture. Established with a deep-rooted Christian philosophy, the university operates under the guiding principle of "To Serve." This motto isn't just a plaque on the wall; it is the heartbeat of the institution, influencing its curriculum, student life, and community engagement. CGU is part of the Gifu Seiryo Gakuen educational corporation, which has a long history of providing quality education in central Japan. 

For international students, Chubu Gakuin University offers a unique blend of high-level professional training and a nurturing, community-oriented environment. Unlike massive urban universities in Tokyo or Osaka, CGU provides a setting where faculty members know students by name. This personalized approach is particularly beneficial for those navigating a new culture and language. The university is split across two main campuses: the Seki Campus, located in the historic 'City of Blades' (Seki), and the Gifu Campus, located in the vibrant prefectural capital. 

## 🎓 Academic Excellence and Faculties

Chubu Gakuin University is renowned primarily for its contributions to the fields of human welfare and health. It is consistently ranked as one of the top schools in the Tokai region for students aiming to pass the National Examination for Social Workers and Care Workers. 

### 1. Faculty of Social Welfare
This is the flagship faculty of the university. It focuses on developing compassionate professionals who can handle the complexities of Japan’s aging society. Students learn about policy, psychology, and practical care techniques. For international students, this faculty offers a deep dive into the 'Japanese model' of welfare, which is highly respected globally.

### 2. Faculty of Business Administration
This faculty is the most popular choice for international students. The curriculum is designed to bridge the gap between theoretical management and the practical needs of the Japanese business world. Courses cover marketing, accounting, information technology, and global business. The faculty emphasizes 'Regional Revitalization,' teaching students how businesses can support and grow local economies.

### 3. Faculty of Nursing and Faculty of Rehabilitation
Located primarily at the Seki Campus, these faculties are equipped with state-of-the-art simulation labs. The Faculty of Rehabilitation offers specialized tracks in Physical Therapy and Occupational Therapy. These programs are rigorous and require a high level of Japanese proficiency, but they boast nearly 100% employment rates for graduates.

### 4. Faculty of Education
Focusing on early childhood education and primary school teaching, this faculty prepares the next generation of educators. It emphasizes 'heart-to-heart' education, ensuring that teachers are not just transmitters of knowledge but mentors to their pupils.

## 🌏 International Student Support and the 'Bekka' Program

Chubu Gakuin University is exceptionally welcoming to students from overseas. One of its standout features is the Japanese Language Course for Overseas Students (Bekka). This one-year intensive program is designed to bring students up to the N2 or N1 level of the Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT), which is required for undergraduate or graduate study. 

Once enrolled in a degree program, international students benefit from:
- **Academic Advising:** Dedicated staff help students choose classes that match their language ability.
- **Language Support:** Advanced Japanese business language classes are available to help students prepare for job hunting in Japan.
- **Cultural Integration:** The university organizes 'International Exchange Salons' where Japanese and international students can interact, share meals, and learn about each other's cultures.

## 💰 Tuition, Fees, and Financial Aid

Studying at Chubu Gakuin University is significantly more affordable than many Western institutions or even private universities in Tokyo. 

- **Admission Fee:** Approximately 200,000 JPY (Paid only in the first year).
- **Annual Tuition:** Ranges from 700,000 to 900,000 JPY depending on the faculty (Nursing and Rehabilitation are higher due to lab fees).
- **Scholarships:** The university offers an 'International Student Special Scholarship' which can provide a 30% to 50% reduction in tuition fees based on academic performance and financial need. Additionally, students are eligible to apply for the MEXT Honors Scholarship and various private foundation grants.

## 🏘️ Life in Gifu: The Student Experience

Gifu Prefecture offers a high quality of life with a much lower cost of living than Japan's mega-cities. 

### Accommodation
The university assists students in finding affordable housing. Many international students live in private apartments near the campus, where rent typically ranges from 25,000 to 45,000 JPY per month. This is a fraction of what one would pay in Tokyo.

### Part-time Work
Gifu is an industrial hub, particularly famous for manufacturing and logistics. Many students find part-time work in local convenience stores, restaurants, or factories. The university’s career center provides guidance on how to find jobs that comply with student visa regulations (up to 28 hours per week).

### Culture and Leisure
Seki City is world-famous for its cutlery and samurai sword-making tradition. Students can visit museums or even try their hand at traditional crafts. Gifu City, on the other hand, offers the scenic Nagara River, famous for 'Ukai' (cormorant fishing). For those craving the big city light, Nagoya—Japan’s fourth-largest city—is only a 20-to-30-minute train ride away.

## 🚀 Career Prospects and Employment Support

Chubu Gakuin University maintains an impressive employment rate, often exceeding 95% for its graduates. The Career Support Center is highly proactive. For international students, they offer:
- **Job Hunting Seminars:** Teaching the unique 'Shukatsu' (job hunting) etiquette of Japan.
- **Mock Interviews:** Practicing how to answer difficult questions in Japanese.
- **Company Matching:** The university has strong ties with local companies in the Gifu and Aichi (Nagoya) areas that are looking to globalize their workforce.

Graduates from the Faculty of Business Administration often find roles in international trade, logistics, and hospitality, while those from the welfare and health faculties are highly sought after by medical corporations and social welfare organizations across Japan.

## 📝 Admission Process for International Students

Applying to Chubu Gakuin University involves a few key steps:
1. **Eligibility:** You must have completed 12 years of formal education.
2. **Language Requirement:** For direct entry into a faculty, JLPT N2 is generally required. If you do not have N2, you should apply for the 'Bekka' Japanese program first.
3. **Examination:** The entrance exam for international students usually includes a Japanese language test, a short essay (Sakubun), and an interview.
4. **Interview:** This is the most crucial part. The university looks for students who are motivated, understand the 'To Serve' spirit, and have a clear plan for their future in Japan.

## 🌟 Why Choose Chubu Gakuin University?

Choosing a university is about finding the right 'fit.' If you are looking for a massive, anonymous campus in a concrete jungle, this might not be the place for you. However, if you want a supportive environment where you can truly master the Japanese language, gain professional skills in business or welfare, and experience the 'real' Japan, Chubu Gakuin University is an excellent choice. 

It provides a bridge between your home country and a successful career in Japan. The combination of affordable tuition, a low cost of living, and a curriculum that is deeply integrated with the local community makes CGU a hidden gem for international students. By the time you graduate, you will not only have a degree but also a second home in Gifu and a professional network that will support you for years to come.
