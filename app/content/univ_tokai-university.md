## University Overview

Tokai University, established in 1942 by Dr. Shigeyoshi Matsumae, is recognized as one of Japan's largest and most comprehensive private universities. Its core philosophy, "Light of Hope for Humanity," focuses on developing individuals who contribute to global peace and human welfare. Originally founded as a private school, it achieved full university status in 1946. Over the years, Tokai University has grown substantially, now operating a network of campuses throughout Japan, each specializing in various academic fields. This multi-campus structure provides a diverse and enriching educational environment, catering to a wide range of interests and career aspirations.

The university is highly regarded for its extensive array of academic programs, ranging from humanities and social sciences to cutting-edge natural sciences, engineering, and medical fields. It has established a particularly strong reputation in areas such as engineering, marine science and technology, and physical education, consistently producing graduates who excel in their respective fields. Tokai University emphasizes practical education, encouraging hands-on experience, research, and interdisciplinary collaboration. It also promotes a global perspective, actively participating in international exchange programs and fostering an inclusive environment for students from around the world. With its large student body and diverse academic programs, Tokai University plays a significant role in Japanese higher education, equipping students not only with specialized knowledge but also with a holistic understanding of societal challenges and a dedication to lifelong learning.

## Faculties & Departments

Tokai University offers a wide range of academic programs across numerous faculties and departments, reflecting its commitment to comprehensive education. Here's a list of its key faculties:

*   **School of Letters:** Focuses on Japanese and foreign literatures, history, and cultural studies.
*   **School of Culture and Studies:** Explores diverse cultural phenomena, linguistics, and cross-cultural understanding.
*   **School of Law:** Provides comprehensive legal education, covering civil, criminal, and international law.
*   **School of Political Science and Economics:** Combines political theory, public policy, and economic principles.
*   **School of Business Administration:** Offers specialized studies in management, marketing, accounting, and international business.
*   **School of International Studies:** Dedicated to global affairs, international relations, and regional studies.
*   **School of Science:** Encompasses mathematics, physics, chemistry, and biological sciences.
*   **School of Information Science and Technology:** Focuses on computer science, IT, and related technological advancements.
*   **School of Engineering:** A flagship faculty, covering civil, mechanical, electrical, and aerospace engineering.
*   **School of Marine Science and Technology:** Unique programs in oceanography, fisheries, and marine engineering.
*   **School of Medical Technology:** Trains professionals in clinical laboratory science, radiological technology, etc.
*   **School of Health Sciences:** Offers studies in nursing, welfare, and public health.
*   **School of Sport and Health Science:** Specializes in sports science, physical education, and health promotion.
*   **School of Industrial Administration:** Addresses industrial management, production systems, and design.
*   **School of Art and Design:** Provides education in visual arts, design, and creative expression.
*   **School of Global Studies:** Offers programs with a strong international focus, often with English-medium options.
*   **School of Tourism:** Explores the business and cultural aspects of the tourism industry.

## International Student Support

Tokai University is dedicated to providing a supportive environment for its diverse international student body, which currently includes around 1,700 students from over 70 countries. The university offers a comprehensive range of services designed to help international students adjust to life in Japan and succeed academically.

*   **Dormitory Accommodation:** Tokai University provides various dormitory options for international students, including dedicated international student dormitories and regular student housing. These facilities are generally well-equipped and offer a convenient and safe living environment, fostering a sense of community among residents. Early application is highly recommended due to limited availability.
*   **English-Taught Programs:** While the majority of undergraduate programs are taught in Japanese and require proficiency, Tokai University offers specific English-taught programs, particularly within the School of Global Studies and some graduate-level courses. These programs are designed for students who wish to pursue their studies without full Japanese language proficiency at the outset.
*   **Japanese Language Support:** For students enrolled in Japanese-taught programs or those wishing to improve their language skills, the university provides Japanese language classes and support services. These range from beginner to advanced levels, helping students navigate daily life and academic pursuits in Japan.
*   **Career Support:** The university's career services office offers tailored support for international students seeking employment in Japan or elsewhere. This includes career counseling, workshops on Japanese job-hunting practices, resume review, interview preparation, and opportunities to connect with potential employers through job fairs and recruitment events. The strong alumni network also provides valuable connections.
*   **Advisory Services:** International student advisors are available to assist with various matters, including visa procedures, daily life concerns, academic advising, and cultural adjustment. This personalized support ensures that international students feel well-supported throughout their academic journey at Tokai University.

## Campus Location & Access

Tokai University operates multiple campuses across Japan, each with unique characteristics and academic focuses. The primary and largest campus is the Shonan Campus in Hiratsuka, Kanagawa Prefecture, while several urban campuses cater to specific faculties and graduate programs. Here's an overview of key campuses and their access:

*   **Shonan Campus:**
    *   **Location:** Hiratsuka, Kanagawa
    *   **Key Features/Faculties:** Main campus; majority of undergraduate faculties, research.
    *   **Access:** Odakyu Line: Tokaidaigaku-mae Station (then walk or bus)
*   **Yoyogi Campus:**
    *   **Location:** Shibuya, Tokyo
    *   **Key Features/Faculties:** Administrative functions, some graduate programs.
    *   **Access:** Odakyu Line: Sangubashi Station (walk); JR Shinjuku Station (bus/walk)
*   **Takanawa Campus:**
    *   **Location:** Minato, Tokyo
    *   **Key Features/Faculties:** School of Tourism, School of Cross-Cultural and Business.
    *   **Access:** JR/Keikyu Line: Shinagawa Station (walk/bus)
*   **Numazu Campus:**
    *   **Location:** Numazu, Shizuoka
    *   **Key Features/Faculties:** School of Marine Science and Technology.
    *   **Access:** JR Tokaido Line: Numazu Station (then bus)
*   **Kumamoto Campus:**
    *   **Location:** Kumamoto, Kumamoto
    *   **Key Features/Faculties:** Regional campus with faculties like Industrial Management.
    *   **Access:** JR Hohi Main Line: Kumamoto Daigaku Mae Station (then walk)
*   **Sapporo Campus:**
    *   **Location:** Sapporo, Hokkaido
    *   **Key Features/Faculties:** Focus on environmental and regional studies.
    *   **Access:** JR Hakodate Line: Teine Station (then bus)

Each campus offers a distinct academic and social environment, ranging from the spacious and green Shonan campus to the urban settings of the Tokyo campuses, providing students with varied options depending on their field of study and lifestyle preferences.
