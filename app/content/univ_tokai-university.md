## üè´ University Overview

Tokai University, founded in 1942 by Dr. Shigeyoshi Matsumae, stands as one of Japan's largest and most comprehensive private universities. Its foundational philosophy, encapsulated by the phrase 'Light of Hope for Humanity,' emphasizes fostering individuals who contribute to global peace and human welfare. Originally established as a private school, it gained full university status in 1946. Over the decades, Tokai University has expanded significantly, now operating a network of campuses across Japan, each specializing in various academic disciplines. This multi-campus structure allows for a diverse and rich educational environment, catering to a wide array of interests and career paths.

The university is highly regarded for its extensive range of academic offerings, spanning from humanities and social sciences to cutting-edge natural sciences, engineering, and medical fields. It has built a particularly strong reputation in areas such as engineering, marine science and technology, and physical education, consistently producing graduates who excel in their respective fields. Tokai University places a strong emphasis on practical education, encouraging hands-on experience, research, and interdisciplinary collaboration. It also promotes a global perspective, actively engaging in international exchange programs and fostering an inclusive environment for students from around the world. With its large student body and diverse academic programs, Tokai University plays a significant role in Japanese higher education, preparing students not just with specialized knowledge but also with a holistic understanding of societal challenges and a commitment to lifelong learning.

## üåç International Student Support

Tokai University is committed to providing a supportive environment for its diverse international student body, which currently numbers around 1,700 students from over 70 countries. The university offers a comprehensive range of services designed to help international students adapt to life in Japan and succeed academically.

**Dormitory Accommodation:** Tokai University provides various dormitory options for international students, including dedicated international student dormitories and regular student housing. These facilities are generally well-equipped and offer a convenient and safe living environment, fostering a sense of community among residents. Early application is highly recommended due to limited availability.

**English-Taught Programs:** While the majority of undergraduate programs are taught in Japanese and require proficiency, Tokai University offers specific English-taught programs, particularly within the School of Global Studies and some graduate-level courses. These programs are designed for students who wish to pursue their studies without full Japanese language proficiency at the outset.

**Japanese Language Support:** For students enrolled in Japanese-taught programs or those wishing to improve their language skills, the university provides Japanese language classes and support services. These range from beginner to advanced levels, helping students navigate daily life and academic pursuits in Japan.

**Career Support:** The university's career services office offers tailored support for international students seeking employment in Japan or elsewhere. This includes career counseling, workshops on Japanese job-hunting practices, resume review, interview preparation, and opportunities to connect with potential employers through job fairs and recruitment events. The strong alumni network also provides valuable connections.

**Advisory Services:** International student advisors are available to assist with various matters, including visa procedures, daily life concerns, academic advising, and cultural adjustment. This personalized support ensures that international students feel well-supported throughout their academic journey at Tokai University.
