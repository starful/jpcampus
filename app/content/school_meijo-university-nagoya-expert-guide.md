## üè´ School Overview & Philosophy

Meijo University, situated in the dynamic metropolitan center of Nagoya, Aichi Prefecture, stands as one of the most prestigious comprehensive private universities in the Chubu region. Founded on the principle of practical learning and contribution to society, Meijo University distinguishes itself through its exceptional focus on Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM), alongside highly respected professional schools such as Pharmacy and Law. Its institutional philosophy, rooted in 'Shih≈ç' (the pursuit of ultimate truth) and 'Jichi' (self-governance and independence), cultivates graduates who are not only academically proficient but also possess the critical thinking and ethical foundations necessary to become leaders in complex, modern society. The university's strategic location in Nagoya‚Äîa global hub for manufacturing, automotive technology, and advanced robotics‚Äîprovides unparalleled opportunities for research collaboration and practical internship experiences, making it an extremely attractive destination for international students seeking careers in high-tech or industrial sectors. The international standing of Meijo University was significantly bolstered by its connection to Nobel Prize-winning research, particularly in the field of blue LED technology, symbolizing its commitment to cutting-edge scientific inquiry and innovation.

While this institution is not a Japanese language school (JLS) but a full-fledged degree-granting university, international applicants must possess superior Japanese language proficiency, typically evidenced by high scores on the N1 level of the Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) and, critically, outstanding performance on the Examination for Japanese University Admission (EJU). The university mandates the EJU for most undergraduate admissions, signaling its rigorous entrance standards comparable to top national universities. Students considering Meijo University must recognize that their preparatory phase‚Äîwhether at a JLS or an international high school‚Äîneeds to be meticulously geared towards achieving high marks in EJU‚Äôs specialized subjects (Mathematics, Science, Japan and the World) in addition to the Japanese language component. The competitive nature of its popular faculties, particularly Science and Technology and Pharmacy, means that only the most dedicated and academically prepared international students are likely to secure admission, making preparation for the entrance examination procedures an extended and resource-intensive endeavor. The institution operates across several campuses, including the main Tenpaku Campus, known for its extensive science and engineering facilities, the Yagoto Campus focusing on Pharmacy, and the smaller campuses dedicated to law and commerce, each offering a specialized learning environment that caters to the distinct needs of its respective faculties.

Meijo University‚Äôs proactive engagement with the local community and industry partnerships defines its educational approach. The university‚Äôs vast alumni network extends deep into corporate Japan, particularly within the central industrial corridor. This robust connectivity ensures that the curriculum is consistently updated to reflect current industrial demands and technological advancements, providing graduates with highly marketable skills immediately upon graduation. For international students, this means that the transition from academic study to professional employment in Japan is significantly smoother, leveraging the university‚Äôs reputation and established career services infrastructure. Furthermore, the commitment to globalizing the campus environment is evident in the increasing diversity of its student body and the proactive establishment of exchange programs with leading universities worldwide. The institutional budget heavily prioritizes research infrastructure, offering state-of-the-art laboratories and specialized equipment, which is a major draw for postgraduate applicants in specialized fields like engineering and pharmaceutical sciences. The foundation of its educational strategy is building a bridge between academic theory and real-world implementation, preparing students to be innovators rather than mere observers in their chosen fields.

## üìö Courses, Curriculum & Teaching Style

Meijo University boasts a remarkably diverse range of faculties that strongly emphasize professional and applied sciences. The core academic offerings are partitioned across ten major faculties: Law, Business Administration, Economics, Policy Studies, Foreign Studies, Education, Science and Technology, Agricultural Sciences, Pharmacy, and Urban Science. For international students, the faculties of Science and Technology, Pharmacy, and Urban Science represent the pinnacle of the university‚Äôs competitive advantage. The Science and Technology faculty, encompassing disciplines from electrical and electronic engineering to civil engineering and architecture, features intensive, project-based learning. Students are expected to engage in advanced research from an early stage, often participating in projects directly funded or supported by nearby industrial giants.

Instructional methods at Meijo are characterized by a blend of large-format lectures, crucial small-group seminars (zemi), and extensive laboratory work. In the science faculties, the curriculum is incredibly demanding, requiring mastery of highly technical Japanese terminology alongside advanced mathematical and scientific concepts. The teaching style encourages critical inquiry and problem-solving, moving beyond rote memorization typical of some institutions. The Zemi system, particularly prominent in social sciences but also utilized in technical fields, pairs a small group of students with a supervising professor, allowing for deep dives into specialized research topics and providing personalized mentorship‚Äîa critical resource for international students navigating complex academic material and cultural integration.

Furthermore, the curriculum for professional studies, such as Pharmacy and Law, is exceptionally structured and geared toward national licensing examinations. The six-year Pharmacy program is highly competitive and is acknowledged nationwide for producing successful licensed pharmacists. Similarly, the newer faculty of Urban Science addresses the complex challenges of modern infrastructure, sustainable development, and smart city planning‚Äîa specialization that is gaining global importance and is directly relevant to Japan's urban landscape. This faculty often incorporates fieldwork and collaboration with municipal authorities in Nagoya, ensuring theoretical knowledge is grounded in practical governmental and urban planning realities. The requirement for high EJU scores ensures that students entering these rigorous programs already possess the fundamental knowledge base necessary to handle the intensity of the specialized curriculum.

For those interested in the humanities and social sciences, faculties like Business Administration and Economics offer programs tailored to the specifics of the East Asian economic model, often integrating case studies drawn from the successful business strategies of local Japanese companies. The Faculty of Foreign Studies focuses not only on language acquisition but also on cross-cultural competency and international relations, essential skills for future professionals working in multinational environments. Across all departments, there is an institutional push towards digital literacy and data analysis, ensuring graduates are prepared for the rapidly evolving job market. Students are continually assessed through a combination of written examinations, research papers, presentations, and, in science disciplines, laboratory reports and practical skills evaluations. Maintaining high academic standing is crucial, particularly as Meijo University often links scholarship renewals and certain career opportunities to grade point average (GPA) performance.
