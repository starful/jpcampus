## üìö Courses, Curriculum & Teaching Style

Kowa Japanese Language Institute offers dedicated 'Shin-gaku' (Higher Education Preparation) courses, tailored to meet the varying arrival times of international students while ensuring full preparation for university entrance cycles. The curriculum is rigidly structured to align with the progressive demands of the JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test) and, critically, the EJU. The core courses run across durations of 2 years, 1 year and 9 months, and 1 year and 6 months, starting in April, July, and October respectively. This tiered entry system allows students flexibility while maintaining the required instructional hours necessary to progress from beginner (N5 equivalent) to advanced academic proficiency (N1/N2 equivalent) before graduation.

**Curriculum Progression:**

The academic journey at Kowa is sharply divided into foundational, intermediate, and advanced stages. The initial phase concentrates heavily on grammar fundamentals, foundational vocabulary, and communicative ability, aiming quickly toward JLPT N3/N4 proficiency. Once this foundation is established, typically within the first nine months to a year, the curriculum shifts decisively towards examination strategy. The intermediate stage focuses on bridging the gap between conversational Japanese and academic Japanese, introducing complex sentence structures, formal writing styles, and technical vocabulary required for university lectures.

**EJU Specialization:**

Kowa's expertise lies in its comprehensive EJU training. Unlike schools where EJU preparation is optional or an extracurricular activity, at Kowa, it is integrated into the timetable. Specialized elective classes are offered focusing on the non-language components of the EJU: Japan and the World (Social Studies/Liberal Arts), Mathematics (Course 1 or 2), and Science (Physics, Chemistry, Biology). These classes are taught by experienced instructors, often specialists in those subjects, using materials specifically tailored to the EJU format and delivered in clear, yet rigorous, Japanese. The school utilizes frequent mock exams, analyzing student performance data to pinpoint individual weaknesses and provide targeted supplementary instruction. This strategic approach maximizes students' scores, which are crucial for entrance to competitive national and private universities.

**Teaching Methodology and Academic Support:**

The teaching style at Kowa blends high-intensity, structured classroom learning with personalized academic counseling. While grammar and vocabulary acquisition are taught through traditional methods augmented by modern instructional technology, the EJU and interview preparation modules are highly interactive. Students engage in frequent role-playing exercises for university interviews, receive detailed feedback on essay and application writing, and participate in comprehensive discussion classes designed to improve logical reasoning and presentation skills in Japanese. Teachers act as mentors, not just instructors, providing guidance on university selection, crafting statement of purpose essays, and navigating the often-complex application submission process. The relatively small school size ensures that every student receives this bespoke level of attention and support, maintaining a high standard of academic accountability and maximizing success rates in university placements.
