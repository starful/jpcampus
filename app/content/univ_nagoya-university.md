Founded in 1871 as a temporary medical school, it officially became Nagoya University in 1939. The university's philosophy emphasizes fostering independent thinking, cultivating a spirit of inquiry, and contributing to global society through research and education. The campus atmosphere is vibrant and intellectually stimulating, attracting students from diverse backgrounds and cultures. The main campus, located in Chikusa-ku, Nagoya, offers a blend of modern and traditional architecture, creating a welcoming and inspiring environment for learning and collaboration.

## ğŸ“ Curriculum & Courses

Nagoya University offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs across various faculties, including Literature, Education, Law, Economics, Informatics, Science, Medicine, Engineering, and Agriculture. For international students, several programs are offered in English, making it possible to pursue a degree without proficiency in Japanese, although learning Japanese is highly recommended for daily life and cultural integration.

The university also requires the Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU) for admission to most undergraduate programs taught in Japanese. However, some English-taught programs may have alternative admission requirements.

The curriculum is designed to provide students with a strong foundation in their chosen field, as well as opportunities for interdisciplinary study and research. Students can choose from a variety of elective courses, including those related to Japanese language and culture, business, and other areas of interest.
