## üè´ University Overview

Nagoya University (ÂêçÂè§Â±ãÂ§ßÂ≠¶, Nagoya Daigaku), often abbreviated to Meidai (ÂêçÂ§ß), is a Japanese national university located in Nagoya, Aichi Prefecture. It is the last Imperial University to be established, tracing its roots back to 1871 with the establishment of a temporary medical school and hospital. Officially chartered as an Imperial University in 1939, Nagoya University has a long-standing reputation as one of Japan's leading research institutions. It consistently ranks among the top universities in Japan and Asia, and is renowned for its contributions to various fields, especially science and technology. Six Nobel laureates are affiliated with Nagoya University, highlighting the university's commitment to academic excellence and innovative research.

Nagoya University is a comprehensive university offering a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs. It places a strong emphasis on fostering independent thinking and encourages students to pursue their intellectual curiosity. The university is actively involved in global collaborations and aims to cultivate global leaders who can contribute to solving complex global challenges. Nagoya University is also a designated National University Corporation, which grants it a degree of autonomy in its operations and strategic planning.

The university is part of the Japanese government's Top Global University Project (SGU), aiming to enhance its global competitiveness. The SGU initiative provides significant funding and support for internationalization efforts, including the expansion of English-taught programs, increased international student enrollment, and the establishment of partnerships with leading universities worldwide.

## üåç International Student Support

Nagoya University provides comprehensive support services for international students to ensure a smooth transition and a fulfilling academic experience. The International Student Support Office offers assistance with visa applications, housing arrangements, and daily life matters. The university also provides Japanese language courses at various proficiency levels, enabling international students to improve their communication skills.

**Dormitory:**
Nagoya University offers several options for student accommodation, including international student dormitories. These dormitories provide affordable and convenient housing, fostering a multicultural living environment. The availability of dormitory spaces is competitive, and application procedures vary depending on the type of dormitory.

**English Programs:**
Nagoya University offers a growing number of degree programs taught entirely in English, particularly at the graduate level. These programs attract international students from diverse backgrounds and provide opportunities for cross-cultural learning. Detailed information on English-taught programs can be found on the university's official website.

**Career Support:**
Nagoya University's Career Support Office provides guidance and resources to help international students explore career options and prepare for job hunting in Japan and abroad. The office organizes career fairs, workshops, and individual consultations to assist students with resume writing, interview skills, and networking.
