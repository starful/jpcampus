## üè´ University Overview

Mie University (Mie Daigaku), often abbreviated as 'Miedai', is a prestigious national university located in Tsu City, Mie Prefecture. Established in 1949 through the merger of several regional institutions, it has grown into a comprehensive research university that prides itself on its 'Sansui' (Three Spirits) philosophy: local focus, international outlook, and humanitarian values. 

What sets Mie University apart is its unique geographical location and its commitment to environmental sustainability. Situated on the shores of Ise Bay, it is one of the few national universities in Japan that enjoys a coastal setting. Furthermore, Mie University is globally recognized for its 'Green Campus' initiative; it was the first university in the world to receive ISO 14001 certification for its entire campus environmental management system. For international students, this represents a learning environment that is not only academically rigorous but also ecologically conscious and physically serene.

## üéì Academic Excellence and Faculties

Mie University is composed of five distinct faculties, each offering specialized tracks that cater to the evolving needs of the global job market.

### 1. Faculty of Humanities, Law and Economics
This faculty is the intellectual heart of the university for social sciences. It offers two major departments: Humanities and Social Sciences. International students often find the Department of Social Sciences particularly attractive, as it explores the intersection of law, economics, and sociology within the context of Japan‚Äôs unique regional development. It provides a solid foundation for those looking to enter the Japanese corporate world or public administration.

### 2. Faculty of Education
With a long history of training elite educators, this faculty focuses on modern pedagogical techniques and psychological development. While primarily targeted at domestic students, the research conducted here regarding bilingual education and international student integration is world-class.

### 3. Faculty of Medicine
Including the prestigious School of Medicine and the School of Nursing, this faculty is attached to the Mie University Hospital‚Äîa central hub for advanced medical care in the Tokai region. It is known for its research in regenerative medicine and cardiovascular health. International collaboration in medical research is a significant focus, with numerous partnerships across Asia and Europe.

### 4. Faculty of Engineering
Engineering is one of the most popular choices for international students. The faculty covers Mechanical, Electrical, Molecular, and Civil Engineering. Given Mie's proximity to Aichi (the home of Toyota), the engineering programs have strong industrial ties, focusing on 'Monozukuri' (the art of making things), robotics, and sustainable energy solutions.

### 5. Faculty of Bioresources
This is perhaps Mie University's most distinctive faculty. Located near the sea and mountains, research here focuses on agriculture, fisheries, and forest science. The faculty is a leader in marine biology and sustainable food production. It also houses the famous **International Ninja Research Center**, which approaches the history and techniques of the Ninja through scientific and historical lenses, attracting scholars from around the globe.

## üåø The 'Green Campus' Philosophy

Mie University‚Äôs commitment to the environment is not just a slogan; it is embedded in the curriculum. The university aims to produce 'Environmental Human Resources'‚Äîgraduates who, regardless of their major, understand the importance of sustainability. Students are encouraged to participate in energy-saving programs, waste reduction initiatives, and regional conservation projects. This forward-thinking approach makes Miedai an ideal choice for students interested in environmental policy or green technology.

## üóæ Living in Tsu City, Mie

For an international student, the location is a perfect balance between urban convenience and natural beauty. Tsu City is the capital of Mie Prefecture, offering a significantly lower cost of living compared to Tokyo or Osaka. 

- **Housing:** The university provides international student dormitories (Foreign Student House) at very affordable rates. Rent typically ranges from 15,000 to 25,000 JPY per month, which is a fraction of what one would pay in the Kanto region.
- **Accessibility:** While Tsu is a quiet city, it is excellently connected. The Kintetsu and JR lines provide easy access to Nagoya (under 60 minutes) and Osaka (about 90 minutes). This allows students to enjoy a peaceful study environment while remaining close to Japan's major metropolitan hubs.
- **Leisure:** From the historic Ise Grand Shrine (Ise Jingu) to the high-octane excitement of the Suzuka Circuit, Mie Prefecture offers a rich cultural and recreational landscape. The local seafood, particularly Ise-ebi (spiny lobster) and Matsusaka beef, is legendary across Japan.

## üåê International Student Support System

Mie University‚Äôs Center for International Education and Research (CIER) provides comprehensive support to ensure students transition smoothly into Japanese life. 

- **Japanese Language Programs:** The university offers intensive Japanese language courses ranging from beginner to advanced levels. These are designed to help students cope with their academic requirements and daily life.
- **Tutor System:** New international students are paired with a Japanese student tutor. These tutors assist with everything from opening a bank account and registering at the city hall to understanding the nuances of Japanese social etiquette.
- **Scholarships:** In addition to the MEXT scholarship, Mie University students are eligible to apply for various local government and private foundation scholarships, such as the JASSO honors scholarship and the Mie International Exchange Foundation grants.

## üìà Admission Guide for International Students

Admission to Mie University is competitive but accessible for dedicated students. The primary pathway for privately-funded students is the **Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU)**.

### Requirements:
1. **EJU Scores:** Most faculties require scores in Japanese as a Foreign Language, Science/Mathematics, or Japan and the World, depending on the major. A high score in the Japanese section (usually 280+ excluding the writing section) is typically necessary.
2. **English Proficiency:** For Engineering and Medicine, TOEFL or TOEIC scores are often required to demonstrate English competency.
3. **University-Specific Exam:** Shortlisted candidates are usually invited for an interview (often in Japanese) and sometimes a secondary written essay test at the Tsu campus.

Applications generally open in December, with exams held in February. It is crucial for prospective students to check the specific guidelines for their chosen faculty, as requirements can vary significantly between Humanities and Bioresources.

## üíº Career Prospects and Regional Industry

Mie University graduates are highly sought after by regional employers. The prefecture is a manufacturing powerhouse, particularly in the automotive and electronics sectors. Companies like Sharp, Honda, and various Toyota subsidiaries have a strong presence in the area. The university‚Äôs Career Center offers specialized job-hunting workshops for international students, focusing on Japanese business manners, resume writing (Rirekisho), and interview techniques. Many international graduates find employment in logistics, international trade, and engineering firms within the Tokai region.
