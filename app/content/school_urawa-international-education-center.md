## School Overview & Philosophy

The Urawa International Education Center is a Japanese language school located in Urawa-ku, Saitama City, Saitama Prefecture, established with the goal of helping students successfully enter Japanese universities and adapt to Japanese society. More than just a language education institution, the school focuses on providing customized education programs that maximize each student's individual potential. With a systematic curriculum, experienced faculty, and a pleasant learning environment, students can move forward towards their dreams. The school respects the diverse backgrounds and learning objectives of students and is committed to supporting them as they grow into global talents.

The Urawa International Education Center is based on a student-centered educational philosophy, creating a classroom atmosphere that encourages active participation and interaction. Instructors go beyond simply conveying knowledge, motivating students to learn and helping them develop self-learning abilities. In addition, customized feedback is provided, taking into account students' individual learning styles and needs, helping them identify and improve their strengths and weaknesses. The school also helps students deepen their understanding of Japanese society and culture and foster an international sensibility through Japanese cultural experience activities and various extracurricular activities.

The Urawa International Education Center is an educational institution dedicated to the success of its students' futures. The school supports students not only in improving their Japanese language skills, but also in acquiring core competencies required in the 21st-century global society, such as critical thinking, problem-solving, and communication skills. In addition, the school provides information necessary for students to enter Japanese universities, supports the entrance examination preparation process, and also provides career counseling and employment support services after graduation. The Urawa International Education Center will be a solid foundation for students to achieve their dreams and grow into talents who contribute to society.

The school can accommodate 206 students, and students of various nationalities can study together and experience international exchange. In addition, it operates a dormitory to help students live safely and conveniently. In particular, it provides single rooms in the dormitory so that students can relax and concentrate on their studies in a private space. The Urawa International Education Center is dedicated to providing the optimal environment for students to focus solely on their studies.

## Courses, Curriculum & Teaching Style

The Urawa International Education Center offers a variety of courses tailored to students' Japanese language proficiency levels and learning goals. In particular, the university preparation course for students aiming to enter university helps students improve their Japanese language skills and prepare for the EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students) through a systematic curriculum and professional faculty. In addition, it offers a variety of specialized courses, such as courses for improving conversation skills, business Japanese courses, and courses for entering art and design fields, meeting the diverse needs of students. Below is brief information about the courses offered:

*   **University Preparatory Course (2 Years):** A systematic two-year university preparation course. Provides intensive support for improving Japanese language skills and preparing for the EJU exam. Helps students succeed in entering university by providing comprehensive information and counseling necessary for university entrance.
*   **University Preparatory Course (1 Year, 9 Months):** A one-year, nine-month university preparation course. You can prepare for university entrance through intensive study in a relatively short period of time. In addition to improving Japanese language skills, it also focuses on increasing understanding of Japanese culture and society.
*   **University Preparatory Course (1 Year, 6 Months):** A one-year, six-month university preparation course. Suitable for students who want to study intensively in a short period of time by entering in October. In addition to preparing for the EJU exam, it also supports practical preparation necessary for university entrance, such as interviews and writing self-introductions.
*   **University Preparatory Course (1 Year, 3 Months):** A one-year, three-month university preparation course. Suitable for students who aim to enter a Japanese university in a relatively short period of time with January enrollment. Systematically supports all processes necessary for university entrance, from improving basic Japanese language skills to preparing for the EJU exam.
