## 🏫 University Overview
Tokoha University (常葉大学) stands as the largest and most prominent private higher education institution in Shizuoka Prefecture. Established with a mission centered on 'Tokoha' (meaning 'Evergreen,' symbolizing eternal vitality and growth), the university has grown from a specialized teacher training college into a comprehensive university system with ten faculties and nineteen departments. For international students, Tokoha offers a unique blend of high-quality Japanese education, a supportive local environment, and a gateway to the industrial heart of Japan. The university is spread across four major campuses—Kusanagi, Mizuochi, Sena, and Hamamatsu—each strategically designed to cater to specific academic disciplines.

## 🗾 The Shizuoka Advantage
Choosing Tokoha University means living in Shizuoka, a region often called the 'Best of Japan.' Located halfway between Tokyo and Nagoya, Shizuoka offers a mild climate, breathtaking views of Mt. Fuji, and a lower cost of living compared to the metropolitan giants. For international students, this means less financial pressure and a safer, more manageable lifestyle while remaining well-connected to the Shinkansen line. Shizuoka is also a manufacturing and agricultural powerhouse, providing ample part-time work opportunities and post-graduation career paths in industries ranging from green tea production to automotive giants like Suzuki and Yamaha.

## 🎓 Academic Excellence and Faculties
Tokoha University is structurally divided to maximize student potential in various fields:

### 1. Faculty of Education
As the foundation of the university, this faculty is renowned throughout central Japan. It consistently produces a high number of certified teachers. For international students interested in pedagogy or the Japanese education system, this is a top-tier choice.

### 2. Faculty of Foreign Studies
This faculty is the hub for international exchange. It offers majors in English, Spanish, and Global Communication. International students often find themselves acting as cultural ambassadors here, engaging in vibrant language exchanges with Japanese peers.

### 3. Faculty of Management and Economics
Located in the urban centers of Shizuoka, these faculties focus on the practical application of business theory. They maintain strong ties with local Shizuoka businesses, offering students internships and real-world project-based learning opportunities.

### 4. Faculty of Health Sciences, Nursing, and Social Welfare
These programs are centered mostly at the Hamamatsu campus. They are equipped with state-of-the-art medical simulation equipment. With Japan's aging society, these fields are critical, and Tokoha’s graduates are highly sought after by regional hospitals and care facilities.

## 🌏 International Student Support and Admissions
Tokoha University actively welcomes students from across Asia and the world. The admissions process for international applicants typically involves the Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU), a document review, and an interview. 

### Support Systems:
- **Tuition Reduction:** Tokoha offers a significant tuition reduction (often up to 30%) for self-financed international students who meet academic and financial criteria.
- **Japanese Language Support:** The university provides supplementary Japanese language courses to help students transition from basic proficiency to the academic Japanese required for senior-level research.
- **Housing Assistance:** While Tokoha may not have traditional dormitories on every campus, the Student Affairs office provides extensive help in finding affordable 'Student Mansions' or apartments near campus, often acting as a guarantor for the lease.

## 🏢 Campus Life and Infrastructure
- **Kusanagi Campus:** This is the flagship campus, opened recently with modern, eco-friendly architecture. It features vast open-access libraries, high-tech computer labs, and diverse dining halls that serve as social hubs.
- **Mizuochi Campus:** Situated in the heart of Shizuoka City, this campus is ideal for Law and Economics students who want to be close to the local government offices and corporate headquarters.
- **Hamamatsu Campus:** Located in Japan’s ‘City of Music and Innovation,’ this campus has a strong focus on health, rehabilitation, and physical education, offering vast sports facilities and clinical labs.

## 💰 Financial Overview (Annual Estimate)
While costs vary by faculty, a general breakdown for an international student in a social science track is as follows:
- **Admission Fee:** 240,000 JPY (First year only)
- **Tuition:** Approximately 700,000 to 900,000 JPY per year (after reductions)
- **Facility Fees:** 150,000 to 200,000 JPY
- **Living Expenses:** Shizuoka is affordable; students can live comfortably on 80,000 to 120,000 JPY per month, including rent, food, and utilities.

## 📈 Career Prospects
Tokoha University boasts an exceptionally high employment rate, often exceeding 95% for its graduates. The 'Career Support Center' provides specialized guidance for international students, including workshops on Japanese business etiquette, resume writing (Rirekisho), and mock interviews. Many international graduates find roles in Shizuoka’s international trade companies, tourism sectors, or stay within the university’s extensive network of partner schools and clinics.

## 🌟 Why Choose Tokoha University?
1. **Community Integration:** Unlike universities in Tokyo where international students can become isolated in 'foreigner bubbles,' Tokoha encourages deep immersion into Japanese society.
2. **Practical Focus:** The curriculum is designed to make you 'shakai-jin' (a productive member of society) from day one.
3. **Natural Beauty:** Being able to see Mt. Fuji from your classroom window provides a unique, serene atmosphere that fosters focused study and mental well-being.
4. **Balanced Pace:** You get the benefits of a modern education system without the overwhelming stress of Japan's largest megacities.

## 📝 Application Tips for International Students
1. **Master the EJU:** Focus heavily on the 'Japanese as a Foreign Language' section, as Tokoha values students who can communicate effectively with their Japanese professors and classmates.
2. **Identify Your 'Why':** During the interview, clearly articulate why you want to study in Shizuoka specifically. Showing knowledge of the local region and industries is a major plus.
3. **Financial Planning:** Have your financial documents ready early. While the university offers scholarships, proving you have the initial funds for the first year is vital for the 'Certificate of Eligibility' (COE) process.
4. **Connect Early:** Reach out to the Admissions Office (Nyushi-ka) or visit the campus during an 'Open Campus' day if you are already in Japan. Personal connections go a long way in the Japanese admission context.

## 🏁 Conclusion
Tokoha University represents the best of regional Japanese education. It offers a prestigious name within Shizuoka, modern facilities, and a warm, welcoming community for those coming from abroad. Whether your goal is to become a teacher, a business leader, or a healthcare professional, Tokoha provides the 'evergreen' foundation needed to grow your career in Japan and beyond. By choosing Tokoha, you are not just choosing a university; you are choosing a lifestyle that balances academic rigor with the peaceful, scenic charm of the Japanese countryside.
