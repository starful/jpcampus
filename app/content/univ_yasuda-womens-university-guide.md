## 🏫 University Overview

Yasuda Women's University (安田女子大学, Yasuda Joshi Daigaku) is a prestigious private women's institution located in the heart of Hiroshima City. With a history spanning over 100 years—dating back to the establishment of the Yasuda Academy in 1915—the university has evolved into one of the most respected educational centers for women in the Chugoku and Shikoku regions of Japan. The university is famous for its educational philosophy of 'Softness and Strength' (柔しく剛く, Yasashiku Tsuyoku), which aims to cultivate women who are compassionate and graceful yet possess the inner strength and professional skills to thrive in a modern, globalized society. For an international student, Yasuda offers a unique blend of traditional Japanese values and cutting-edge academic programs.

The campus is situated in Asaminami-ku, Hiroshima, providing a serene learning environment that is well-connected to the city center via the Astram Line. Known for its high-quality facilities and a cohesive 'Garden Campus' design, Yasuda creates an atmosphere where students can focus deeply on their studies while developing lifelong friendships. The institution is not just a place for academic learning; it is a finishing school for character, where students are trained in the nuances of Japanese social and professional etiquette, making them highly sought after by employers across Japan.

## 📚 Academic Excellence & Faculty Breakdown

Yasuda Women's University offers a diverse range of undergraduate and graduate programs. Each faculty is designed to respond to the specific needs of the job market while fostering intellectual curiosity.

### 1. Faculty of Letters
This is the cornerstone of the university, focusing on Japanese Language and Literature, English Language and Culture, and History and Archaeology. International students often find a home here, particularly in the Japanese department, where the study of 'Bungaku' (literature) provides deep insights into the Japanese psyche.

### 2. Faculty of Digital Business (Newly Established)
Recognizing the digital transformation (DX) of global industries, this faculty is one of the most progressive in Japan. It combines data science, digital marketing, and management. It is an excellent choice for international students looking to work in Japan's tech-forward business sector.

### 3. Faculty of Pharmacy
A rigorous six-year program that prepares students for the national pharmacist examination. The facilities are top-tier, featuring advanced research labs and simulation pharmacies. The focus here is on 'human-centered' pharmacy, emphasizing the patient-pharmacist relationship.

### 4. Faculty of Contemporary Business
This faculty covers Public Management and International Business. It focuses on practical skills, including accounting, finance, and logistics. It is designed to produce leaders who can navigate the complexities of international trade and local governance.

### 5. Faculty of Psychology
Yasuda boasts one of the strongest psychology programs in Western Japan. With clinical psychology tracks and developmental studies, it provides students with the tools to support mental health in schools, hospitals, and corporate environments.

### 6. Faculty of Nursing & Health Sciences
These faculties address the growing demand for healthcare professionals in Japan's aging society. Programs include Nursing, Physical Therapy, and Medical Technology. The training is hands-on, utilizing the university's strong network of affiliated hospitals in Hiroshima.

## 🌸 Student Life and the 'Yasuda Brand'

What sets Yasuda apart is the 'Yasuda Brand.' In Hiroshima and the surrounding prefectures, a graduate of Yasuda is synonymous with 'reliability, politeness, and high competence.' This reputation is built through the university's unique 'Manners and Etiquette' curriculum. All students learn formal Japanese tea ceremony (Sado), flower arrangement (Kado), or traditional social protocols, which builds a level of poise and confidence that is highly visible during job interviews.

### Campus Facilities
The campus is often described as a 'park-like' setting. The 'No. 9 Building' and the library are architectural highlights, providing massive open spaces for collaborative learning. The Learning Commons is a hub for international exchange and group projects, equipped with the latest technology for presentations and digital research.

### Extracurricular Activities
From traditional Japanese calligraphy and dance clubs to modern sports and music circles, there are over 50 student organizations. For international students, joining a 'Circle' is the fastest way to master the Japanese language and understand the 'Kouhai-Senpai' (junior-senior) relationship dynamics essential for working in Japan.

## 🌏 International Student Support & Admissions

Yasuda Women's University is actively expanding its international footprint. While the international student body is currently small, this offers a significant advantage: personalized support. Unlike massive urban universities where students can feel like a number, at Yasuda, the International Exchange Center provides one-on-one assistance with visas, housing, and academic tutoring.

### Admission Requirements
For undergraduate programs, international applicants generally need to provide:
- Evidence of Japanese Language Proficiency (JLPT N2 or N1 is highly recommended).
- Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU) scores.
- An interview and a short essay (Shoronbun).
The university looks for students who align with their 'Softness and Strength' philosophy—individuals who are eager to learn about Japanese culture while contributing their own perspectives.

### Scholarships and Tuition Reduction
Yasuda offers tuition reduction schemes for self-financed international students, often ranging from 30% to 50% of the tuition fee depending on academic performance. There are also local Hiroshima-based scholarships like the Hiroshima Peace Culture Foundation scholarship available to residents.

## 💼 Career Prospects: The 99% Success Rate

The most compelling reason for international students to choose Yasuda is the career support. The university consistently reports an employment rate of nearly 100% for its graduates. The Career Center starts working with students from their first year, offering workshops on resume writing (RIREKISHO), mock interviews, and industry analysis.

Major employers include regional banks (Hiroshima Bank), airlines (JAL/ANA), international hotel chains, and major retail corporations. For international students, the university leverages its connections with local Hiroshima companies looking to expand overseas, making it an ideal springboard for a career in Japan.

## 📍 Living in Hiroshima: A City of Peace and Culture

Hiroshima is an ideal city for international students. It is large enough to offer all the conveniences of a metropolis—shopping, nightlife, and culture—but small enough to be safe, affordable, and friendly. 

- **Cost of Living:** Significantly lower than Tokyo or Osaka. Rent for a student apartment near the university can range from 30,000 to 50,000 JPY per month.
- **Culture:** As a 'City of Peace,' Hiroshima is globally recognized. Students can visit the Peace Memorial Park, Miyajima Island (a UNESCO World Heritage site), and enjoy the local soul food, Okonomiyaki.
- **Accessibility:** The university is connected via the Astram Line, a modern transit system that makes commuting from the city center quick and easy.

## 🏁 Conclusion: Is Yasuda Right for You?

Yasuda Women's University is perfect for the international student who wants more than just a degree. It is for the woman who wants to immerse herself in Japanese culture, master the language, and acquire the professional grace that will open doors in the Japanese corporate world. If you are looking for a supportive, female-focused environment that prioritizes character development alongside high-level academic training, Yasuda Women's University is an exceptional choice in Western Japan. By choosing Yasuda, you are not just attending a university; you are joining a lifelong network of empowered women who carry the 'Softness and Strength' required to lead in the 21st century.
