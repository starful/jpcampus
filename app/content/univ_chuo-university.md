## üè´ University Overview

Chuo University, or Chuo Daigaku, is a prestigious private research university located in Tokyo, Japan. Founded in 1885 as Igirisu Horitsu Gakko (English Law School), it has a long and distinguished history of producing prominent figures in law, business, and politics. Chuo University is widely recognized as one of Japan's top universities, consistently ranking high in national and international rankings. It is particularly well-regarded for its Faculty of Law, which is one of the oldest and most respected in the country. The university is known for its rigorous academic environment, commitment to research, and strong alumni network.

Chuo University maintains four main campuses: the Surugadai Campus in central Tokyo, the Korakuen Campus also in Tokyo, the Tama Campus located in the western suburbs of Tokyo, and the Myogadani Campus in Tokyo. The Tama Campus, the largest, houses most of the undergraduate faculties. The Surugadai Campus primarily hosts graduate programs and the Law School.

Chuo University places strong emphasis on fostering global perspectives and promoting international exchange. It has partnerships with numerous universities around the world, offering students opportunities to study abroad and participate in collaborative research projects. The university is committed to providing a supportive environment for international students, with comprehensive support services including housing, language assistance, and career counseling.

## üåç International Student Support

Chuo University provides extensive support for international students to ensure a smooth and enriching academic experience. Key support services include:

- **Dormitory:** The university offers on-campus and off-campus housing options for international students. Dormitory facilities are modern and well-equipped, providing a comfortable and safe living environment.
- **English Programs:** Several faculties offer degree programs taught entirely in English, particularly at the graduate level. The university also provides Japanese language courses for international students to enhance their communication skills and cultural understanding.
- **Career Support:** The Career Center provides guidance and resources to help international students explore career options, prepare for job interviews, and find employment opportunities in Japan and abroad. They offer workshops, individual counseling, and networking events.
- **Visa Support:** The university assists international students with the visa application process and provides guidance on immigration regulations.
- **Counseling Services:** Mental health support services are available to all students, including international students, to address any personal or academic challenges they may face.
- **International Student Association:** Connects international students and organizes events.
