## University Overview

Chuo University, also known as Chuo Daigaku, is a prestigious private research university located in Tokyo, Japan. Established in 1885 as Igirisu Horitsu Gakko (English Law School), it boasts a long and distinguished history of producing prominent figures in law, business, and politics. Chuo University is widely recognized as one of Japan's top universities, consistently achieving high rankings both nationally and internationally. It is particularly renowned for its Faculty of Law, one of the oldest and most respected in the country. The university is known for its rigorous academic environment, commitment to research, and a strong alumni network.

Chuo University operates four main campuses: the Surugadai Campus in central Tokyo, the Korakuen Campus also in Tokyo, the Tama Campus situated in the western suburbs of Tokyo, and the Myogadani Campus in Tokyo. The Tama Campus, being the largest, hosts the majority of the undergraduate faculties. The Surugadai Campus primarily accommodates graduate programs and the Law School.

Chuo University emphasizes cultivating global perspectives and promoting international exchange. It has established partnerships with numerous universities worldwide, offering students opportunities to study abroad and engage in collaborative research projects. The university is dedicated to providing a supportive environment for international students, offering comprehensive support services that include housing, language assistance, and career counseling.

## International Student Support

Chuo University offers extensive support services for international students to ensure a seamless and enriching academic experience. Key support services include:

*   **Dormitory:** The university provides both on-campus and off-campus housing options for international students. Dormitory facilities are modern and well-equipped, offering a comfortable and secure living environment.
*   **English Programs:** Several faculties offer degree programs taught entirely in English, especially at the graduate level. The university also provides Japanese language courses for international students to improve their communication skills and cultural understanding.
*   **Career Support:** The Career Center offers guidance and resources to assist international students in exploring career options, preparing for job interviews, and identifying employment opportunities both in Japan and abroad. They provide workshops, individual counseling, and networking events.
*   **Visa Support:** The university assists international students with the visa application process and offers guidance on immigration regulations.
*   **Counseling Services:** Mental health support services are available to all students, including international students, to address any personal or academic challenges they may encounter.
*   **International Student Association:** This association connects international students and organizes various events.
