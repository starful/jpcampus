## University Overview & Reputation

Shinshu University, located in the heart of the Japanese Alps, is a well-known national university recognized for its dedication to regional contributions and groundbreaking research. While it is not part of the 'Soukeisen' group of top private universities, Shinshu University holds a significant position among national universities in Japan. It is known for its diverse faculties and commitment to developing innovative solutions to both local and global challenges. Its location in Nagano Prefecture offers a unique learning environment for students looking for a combination of academic excellence and natural beauty.

Founded in 1949 through the merger of several institutions, including Matsumoto Higher School, Nagano Normal School, and Nagano Technical College, Shinshu University has a strong history of academic achievement and practical training. The university's core values emphasize community involvement, interdisciplinary collaboration, and the pursuit of knowledge that benefits society. These values are reflected in the university's broad range of programs and its dedication to addressing regional issues like environmental sustainability, healthcare, and economic development.

Shinshu University has a solid academic reputation both in Japan and internationally. It is especially respected for its research in fields such as engineering, agriculture, and medicine. The university is actively involved in collaborative research projects with institutions around the world, contributing to advancements in areas like renewable energy, materials science, and medical technology. Socially, Shinshu University provides a vibrant and inclusive campus environment where students from diverse backgrounds can thrive. The university's location in Nagano provides numerous opportunities for outdoor recreation, contributing to a healthy and balanced lifestyle for students.

**Signature Programs:**

One of Shinshu University's signature programs is the **Faculty of Engineering's program in Mechanical Engineering**. This program is notable for its emphasis on practical training and industry collaboration. Students can participate in internships with leading manufacturing companies in Japan, gaining hands-on experience in design, manufacturing, and automation. The program also features state-of-the-art research facilities, allowing students to conduct advanced research in areas such as robotics, mechatronics, and sustainable energy. This program is particularly appealing to international students because of its global perspective, with opportunities to participate in exchange programs with partner universities worldwide and collaborate on international research projects. Many graduates go on to work for leading companies in the automotive, aerospace, and electronics industries.

Another notable program is the **Faculty of Agriculture's program in Crop Science**. This program is highly regarded for its focus on sustainable agriculture and its contributions to addressing global food security challenges. Students learn about the latest techniques in crop breeding, cultivation, and pest management, with a strong emphasis on environmentally friendly practices. The program also benefits from its location in Nagano Prefecture, a region known for its diverse agricultural production. Students have opportunities to conduct field research on local farms and collaborate with farmers to develop innovative solutions to agricultural challenges. This program is particularly appealing to international students interested in sustainable agriculture, food science, and rural development. The program also focuses on rice production, which is important to Japanese culture and global food security. Graduates are well-prepared to pursue careers in agricultural research, development, and extension.

## International Student Admissions & Life

**Admissions Process:**

The application process for international students at Shinshu University typically involves these steps:

*   Submit an online application, including academic transcripts, letters of recommendation, and a personal statement outlining academic goals and reasons for choosing Shinshu University.
*   Most faculties require the Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU), which assesses Japanese language proficiency and fundamental academic skills.
*   Applicants whose native language is not English must submit scores from recognized English proficiency tests such as TOEFL or IELTS. Specific score requirements vary depending on the faculty and program.
*   Some departments may also require an interview, conducted in person or via video conference, to assess communication skills and suitability for the program.
*   It's essential to check the specific requirements for each faculty and program on the university's official website.

**English-Taught Programs:**

Shinshu University offers several degree programs taught entirely in English, making it accessible for international students who may not have advanced Japanese language proficiency. Some notable English-taught programs include:

*   **Global Aqua Innovation Center (GAIC) Ph.D. Program:** An interdisciplinary program focusing on water-related issues, encompassing environmental science, engineering, and social sciences, designed to train future leaders in water management and innovation.
*   **Graduate School of Science and Technology (various programs):** Certain departments within the Graduate School of Science and Technology offer courses and research opportunities in English, particularly in fields such as engineering, materials science, and computer science. Contact the relevant department to confirm the availability of English-taught courses.
*   **International Program in Agriculture:** Offered by the Faculty of Agriculture, this program allows international students to study various aspects of agriculture in English, focusing on sustainable agriculture and food security.

**Student Support:**

Shinshu University provides a comprehensive range of support services for international students to ensure a smooth transition and a successful academic experience.

*   The International Student Center offers assistance with visa applications, housing arrangements, and other practical matters.
*   Academic advising is available from faculty members and dedicated advisors who can help students choose courses, plan their academic progress, and access research opportunities.
*   Counseling services are also available to provide emotional support and guidance.
*   The university organizes orientation programs and social events to help international students connect with each other and integrate into the campus community.
*   Visa support is provided by the International Student Center, assisting students with the renewal and extension of their visas.

**Campus Life & Diversity:**

Shinshu University has a vibrant and diverse campus community, with a growing number of international students from various countries.

*   The university has many student clubs and organizations catering to a wide range of interests, including sports, arts, culture, and academic pursuits.
*   These clubs provide opportunities for international students to interact with Japanese students and share their cultures.
*   The overall campus atmosphere is welcoming and inclusive, with a strong emphasis on intercultural understanding and exchange.
*   International students are actively encouraged to participate in campus events and contribute to the university's diverse cultural landscape.
*   The university promotes diversity through various initiatives, such as language exchange programs, cultural festivals, and international student support groups.

**Scholarship Opportunities:**

Shinshu University offers various scholarship opportunities for international students. Some of the major scholarships include:

*   **Japanese Government (MEXT) Scholarship:** A prestigious scholarship offered by the Japanese Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology (MEXT) that covers tuition fees, provides a monthly stipend, and covers travel expenses to and from Japan. Apply through the Japanese embassy or consulate in your home country.
*   **Shinshu University Scholarship for International Students:** Offered by the university, this scholarship provides financial assistance to outstanding international students. The amount and duration of the scholarship vary depending on academic performance and financial need.
*   **Private Foundation Scholarships:** Several private foundations in Japan offer scholarships to international students studying at Japanese universities. These scholarships often have specific eligibility criteria, such as academic field of study or nationality.

**Cost of Living:**

The cost of living in Matsumoto, where the main campus is located, is relatively affordable compared to major metropolitan areas like Tokyo or Osaka.

*   Rent for a single room apartment typically ranges from 30,000 to 50,000 JPY per month.
*   Food expenses can be estimated at around 30,000 JPY per month, depending on dietary habits.
*   Transportation costs vary depending on the distance traveled, but a monthly budget of 5,000 to 10,000 JPY should be sufficient.
*   Other expenses, such as utilities, internet, and personal items, can be estimated at around 10,000 to 20,000 JPY per month.
*   Overall, a reasonable monthly budget for living expenses in Matsumoto would be around 75,000 to 130,000 JPY.
