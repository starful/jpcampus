## üè´ University Overview

Keio University, officially known as Keio Gijuku University, stands as one of Japan's most prestigious and oldest private institutions of higher learning. Founded in 1858 by Yukichi Fukuzawa, a prominent intellectual during the Meiji Restoration, Keio was initially established as a school for Western studies. Fukuzawa's vision was to introduce Western knowledge and ideas to Japan, fostering modernization and progress. This commitment to innovation and academic excellence has been a hallmark of Keio University throughout its history.

Keio is renowned for its comprehensive academic programs, rigorous curriculum, and strong ties to the business world. The university has consistently produced leaders in various fields, including politics, economics, science, and technology. Keio's alumni network is extensive and influential, providing valuable connections and opportunities for its graduates. The university's reputation extends internationally, attracting students and researchers from around the globe.

Keio University is also a member of the Top Global University Project (SGU), a Japanese government initiative aimed at enhancing the international competitiveness of selected universities. As part of this project, Keio is actively engaged in promoting international collaborations, attracting international faculty and students, and developing English-based degree programs. The university's commitment to globalization is evident in its diverse student body and its numerous partnerships with universities around the world.

The university prides itself on its tradition of fostering independent thinking and critical inquiry. Keio's educational philosophy emphasizes the importance of developing well-rounded individuals who are capable of contributing to society in meaningful ways. Through its diverse academic programs, research initiatives, and extracurricular activities, Keio strives to cultivate the intellectual curiosity, leadership skills, and social responsibility of its students.

## üåç International Student Support

Keio University provides a comprehensive range of support services for international students to ensure a smooth and enriching academic experience. These services include:

*   **Dormitory:** Keio offers several dormitories specifically for international students. These dormitories provide a safe and comfortable living environment and foster a sense of community among international students.
*   **English Programs:** Numerous degree programs are offered in English, particularly at the graduate level. These programs cater to international students who may not have a strong command of Japanese.
*   **Language Support:** Japanese language courses are available for international students at all levels, from beginner to advanced. These courses help students improve their Japanese language skills and integrate more easily into Japanese society.
*   **Career Support:** The university's career services offer guidance and support to international students seeking employment opportunities in Japan or abroad. This includes career counseling, resume workshops, and job placement assistance.
*   **Visa Support:** Keio provides assistance with visa applications and renewals for international students.
*   **Cultural Exchange Programs:** Various cultural exchange programs are organized throughout the year to promote cross-cultural understanding and interaction between international and Japanese students.
*   **Counseling Services:** The university offers counseling services to help international students address any personal or academic challenges they may face.
