## School Overview

Nagoya Futoku Japanese Language Academy offers a supportive and enriching environment for international students seeking to master the Japanese language and pursue higher education or career opportunities in Japan. Founded on the principles of personalized learning and cultural immersion, the academy fosters a close-knit community where students can thrive. With small class sizes and dedicated instructors, Nagoya Futoku aims to equip each student with the necessary linguistic proficiency and cultural understanding for success.

The academy prides itself on its welcoming atmosphere. Staff and faculty are readily available to assist students with academic, personal, and cultural adjustments. Regular events and activities are organized to promote interaction and friendship among students from diverse backgrounds.

## Location & Surroundings

Nagoya Futoku Japanese Language Academy is conveniently located in the heart of Nagoya, offering easy access to public transportation, shopping, and cultural attractions. The school's central location allows students to immerse themselves in the vibrant city life while benefiting from a safe and comfortable learning environment. Numerous convenience stores, restaurants, and cafes are within walking distance, making daily life convenient and enjoyable.

## Curriculum & Courses

Nagoya Futoku Japanese Language Academy offers a comprehensive curriculum designed to cater to students of all levels, from beginners to advanced learners. The curriculum focuses on developing well-rounded language skills, including reading, writing, listening, and speaking. Emphasis is placed on practical communication skills necessary for daily life, academic pursuits, and professional endeavors.

The school utilizes ability-based class placement to ensure that students are learning at an appropriate pace and level. Regular assessments are conducted to track student progress and make necessary adjustments to their learning plans.

Here's a look at the different levels and their focus:

*   **Beginner (N5 equivalent):**
    *   Focus: Basic grammar, vocabulary, and sentence structures. Introduction to Japanese culture.
    *   Description: Students learn fundamental communication skills for everyday situations.
    *   JLPT Target: N5
*   **Elementary (N4 equivalent):**
    *   Focus: Expanding vocabulary and grammar. Developing basic conversational skills.
    *   Description: Students are able to engage in simple conversations and read basic texts.
    *   JLPT Target: N4
*   **Intermediate (N3 equivalent):**
    *   Focus: Improving fluency and comprehension. Learning more complex grammar structures.
    *   Description: Students can understand and participate in more complex conversations.
    *   JLPT Target: N3
*   **Upper-Intermediate (N2 equivalent):**
    *   Focus: Advanced grammar and vocabulary. Developing sophisticated communication skills.
    *   Description: Students are prepared for academic studies and professional communication.
    *   JLPT Target: N2
*   **Advanced (N1 equivalent):**
    *   Focus: Mastering advanced language skills. Polishing communication for academic and professional purposes.
    *   Description: Students achieve a high level of fluency and comprehension.
    *   JLPT Target: N1

**Course Options:**

*   **University Preparatory Courses (2 years, 1.9 years, 1.6 years, 1.3 years):** These courses are designed for students who plan to enter Japanese universities or vocational schools. The curriculum includes intensive Japanese language training, preparation for the Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU), and guidance on university selection and application.
*   **Short-Term Courses:** These courses are suitable for students who wish to study Japanese for a shorter period, typically from a few weeks to a few months. The curriculum focuses on practical communication skills and cultural immersion.

**Elective Classes:**

In addition to the core curriculum, Nagoya Futoku offers a variety of elective classes to cater to students' specific interests and goals. These classes may include:

*   Business Japanese: Learn the language and etiquette necessary for working in a Japanese business environment.
*   Japanese Culture: Explore Japanese traditions, arts, and customs through hands-on activities and field trips.
*   EJU Preparation (Science): Targeted preparation for the science sections of the EJU exam.
*   Art & Design: Introduction to Japanese art and design principles, tailored for students interested in these fields.
