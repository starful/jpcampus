The university is dedicated to producing graduates with broad perspectives, adaptability, and a strong understanding of global and regional dynamics, making it an ideal choice for those interested in bridging cultures and economies.

## üåç International Student Support

Aichi University places significant emphasis on providing robust support systems for its diverse international student body, recognizing the unique challenges faced when adapting to a new country and academic environment. 

### Language and Academic Support

The university provides specialized Japanese Language Program (JLP) courses tailored to the needs of international students, aiming to rapidly improve academic Japanese skills necessary for coursework and daily life. Remedial classes are also available for specific subjects where language proficiency might initially impede understanding. Furthermore, the Academic Advising Center offers one-on-one guidance on study techniques, assignment submission, and cultural adaptation.

### Housing and Accommodation

While housing is competitive, Aichi University offers designated **International Student Dormitories**, particularly on the Toyohashi Campus, providing a cost-effective and socially integrated start to university life. These dorms are generally available for the first year of study, offering single rooms and shared facilities, facilitating integration with local and other international students. The International Center also assists students in searching for private apartments near the Nagoya Campus once they transition out of university housing, leveraging partnerships with local real estate agencies familiar with international student tenancy requirements.

### Scholarships and Financial Aid

Aichi University offers a highly attractive array of institutional scholarships specifically targeting privately funded international students (ÁßÅË≤ªÂ§ñÂõΩ‰∫∫ÁïôÂ≠¶Áîü). These scholarships significantly alleviate the financial burden:

*   **Aichi University International Student Scholarship:** A primary source of aid, often providing substantial tuition reductions or monthly stipends based on academic merit and financial need.
*   **Reduction/Exemption of Tuition Fees:** High-performing students are eligible for potential reductions in their annual tuition fees, sometimes covering 30% to 50% of the cost.
*   **MEXT (Monbukagakusho) Scholarship:** Although competitive, the university actively nominates outstanding candidates for the Japanese government's MEXT scholarship, which covers tuition and provides a generous monthly stipend.
*   **External Private Scholarships:** The International Center regularly circulates information and assists students in applying for various external scholarships provided by foundations and local organizations specific to the Aichi region.

### Daily Life and Career Support

The International Center acts as the primary liaison for all non-academic issues, assisting with essential procedures such as visa renewal, national health insurance enrollment, and banking.
