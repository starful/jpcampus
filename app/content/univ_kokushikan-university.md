## University Overview
Kokushikan University was established in 1917 as a private academy and achieved university status in 1949. Founded on the principles of "Bushido Spirit," "Respect for Humanity," and "Cultivation of Intellect," the university boasts a unique historical journey, blending traditional Japanese martial arts and values with modern academic pursuits. Initially focused on cultivating individuals with strong moral character and a dedication to public service, it has evolved into a comprehensive institution, providing a diverse range of academic disciplines across humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, and physical education.

The university operates on three distinct campuses in Tokyo: Setagaya, Machida, and Tama, each catering to specific academic and extracurricular needs. Over its century-long history, Kokushikan University has gained a reputation for its robust physical education programs, producing numerous Olympic athletes and sports professionals. Beyond sports, it's recognized for offering practical and career-oriented education in fields like law, political science, economics, engineering, and contemporary business. While its historical roots are deeply embedded in nationalistic ideals, the university today emphasizes a global perspective, promoting international exchange and fostering a diverse student body. It strives to cultivate individuals who are not only academically proficient but also possess a strong sense of ethics, social responsibility, and resilience, preparing them to contribute positively to a rapidly changing global society. The university's commitment to practical learning is evident through its various internship programs, community engagement projects, and a focus on developing problem-solving skills, ensuring graduates are well-prepared for their chosen careers. Kokushikan University continually updates its curriculum and facilities to meet the demands of contemporary society, embracing innovation while upholding its founding spirit. Its alumni network is extensive, with graduates making significant contributions in various sectors including government, business, education, and sports, both within Japan and internationally. This rich history and forward-looking approach define Kokushikan University's unique position within Japanese higher education.

It is always recommended to consult the official university website for the most current and specific fee structures.

*   Yearly Tuition (Average): 1,000,000 JPY (Varies by faculty, e.g., Science and Engineering might be higher, Letters slightly lower)

*   Other Fees (Approx.): 20,000 JPY (Student association fees, accident insurance, etc.)

## International Student Support
Kokushikan University is committed to providing a supportive and enriching environment for its international student community. The university offers a range of services and facilities designed to help international students adapt to life in Japan and succeed academically.

*   **Dormitory Accommodation:** The university offers several dormitories for both male and female students, providing convenient and affordable housing options. These dormitories often foster a sense of community among residents, including both Japanese and international students. Availability may vary, and early application is recommended.
*   **Japanese Language Education:** For students who need to improve their Japanese proficiency for daily life and academic studies, the university offers comprehensive Japanese language courses at various levels. These courses are crucial for integration and academic success.
*   **Academic and Life Counseling:** International students have access to dedicated counselors who can provide guidance on academic matters, visa procedures, cultural adjustment, and personal concerns. This support system helps students navigate potential challenges and make the most of their university experience.
*   **Career Support:** The university's career center offers specialized support for international students seeking employment in Japan. This includes workshops on Japanese job-hunting customs, resume writing, interview preparation, and connecting students with potential employers through job fairs and networking events.
*   **Limited English-Taught Courses:** While the majority of undergraduate programs are taught in Japanese, Kokushikan University does offer a limited number of courses and some specialized programs that incorporate English instruction, particularly within faculties like 21st Century Asia and some graduate programs. Students are encouraged to check specific program details for English-taught options.
*   **Scholarship Opportunities:** Various scholarship programs are available for international students, including those offered by the university itself, the Japanese government (MEXT), and private organizations. These scholarships can help alleviate financial burdens and are often merit-based or need-based. Information on how to apply and eligibility criteria is typically provided through the International Center.
*   **Cultural Exchange Programs:** The university actively promotes cultural exchange through various events and activities, allowing international students to share their cultures and learn about Japanese traditions, fostering a vibrant and multicultural campus environment.

## Campus Location & Access
Kokushikan University operates three distinct campuses, each with its unique focus and access. These campuses are strategically located in Tokyo, offering a mix of urban convenience and specialized facilities.

#### Setagaya Campus (Main Campus)
The Setagaya Campus is the primary academic hub, housing the main administrative offices and several key faculties. It's located in a residential area of Tokyo, providing a calm yet accessible environment for study.

*   Address: 4-28-1 Setagaya, Setagaya-ku, Tokyo 154-8515, Japan
*   Faculties: Political Science and Economics, Law, Letters, Business Administration, Contemporary Business, 21st Century Asia
*   Nearest Station: Gotokuji Station (Odakyu Line), Miyanosaka Station (Tokyu Setagaya Line)
*   Access: Approximately 10-15 minute walk from either Gotokuji Station or Miyanosaka Station. Frequent bus services are also available from nearby major stations like Shibuya.

#### Machida Campus
The Machida Campus is renowned for its extensive sports facilities and is home to the Faculty of Physical Education. It offers a spacious environment ideal for athletic training and research.

*   Address: 7-35-1 Higashitama, Machida-shi, Tokyo 195-8550, Japan
*   Faculties: Physical Education
*   Nearest Station: Tsurukawa Station (Odakyu Line)
*   Access: Approximately 10-15 minute bus ride from Tsurukawa Station. The university also operates shuttle bus services during peak hours.

#### Tama Campus
The Tama Campus is dedicated to science and engineering, featuring advanced laboratories and research facilities. It is located in a green, suburban area, conducive to focused academic and research activities.

*   Address: 9-7-1 Nagayama, Tama-shi, Tokyo 206-8515, Japan
*   Faculties: Science and Engineering
*   Nearest Station: Odakyu Nagayama Station (Odakyu Line), Keio Nagayama Station (Keio Sagamihara Line)
*   Access: Approximately 15-20 minute walk from either Odakyu Nagayama Station or Keio Nagayama Station. Local bus services also connect to the campus.

Students can easily commute between the campuses and other parts of Tokyo using Japan's efficient public transportation network, though travel times between campuses can vary.
