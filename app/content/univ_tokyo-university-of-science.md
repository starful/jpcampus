## üè´ University Overview & Reputation

The Tokyo University of Science (Êù±‰∫¨ÁêÜÁßëÂ§ßÂ≠¶, TUS), often abbreviated as *Rikaidai*, stands as a prestigious private institution deeply rooted in Japan's scientific and technological advancements. Founded in 1881 by forward-thinking physicists from the esteemed University of Tokyo, TUS holds the distinction of being one of the oldest private science universities in the country. Its foundation reflects a commitment to practical learning and a dedication to fostering talent in science and engineering.

TUS consistently ranks among the top private universities in Japan, particularly excelling in the STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) fields. While not a national university, its reputation often places it in a comparable tier to some national institutions, particularly for specialized areas. The university is known for its rigorous academic environment and emphasis on hands-on experience. Its alumni network is extensive and influential, encompassing leaders in industry, academia, and government, further solidifying TUS's reputation as a breeding ground for innovation and expertise.

Over its history, TUS has established itself as a significant contributor to Japan's technological prowess. It boasts a strong research focus, with numerous research centers dedicated to cutting-edge areas like nanotechnology, materials science, and robotics. This dedication to research translates into a curriculum that emphasizes critical thinking, problem-solving, and the application of theoretical knowledge to real-world challenges. Students at TUS are expected to engage actively in research projects, often collaborating with faculty and industry partners. Its commitment to excellence has made it a popular choice for domestic students and is increasingly attracting talented international students seeking a challenging and rewarding education in Japan.

One of the University's flagship programs is within the **Faculty of Engineering, specifically the Department of Electrical Engineering**. This department is renowned for its cutting-edge research in areas such as power electronics, communication systems, and robotics. Students benefit from state-of-the-art laboratories, experienced faculty members, and opportunities to participate in collaborative projects with leading companies. The program places a strong emphasis on practical application, preparing graduates for successful careers in various industries, including electronics, telecommunications, and energy. International students are attracted to this program due to its comprehensive curriculum, research opportunities, and the potential for global impact. While a fully English program isn't guaranteed at the undergraduate level, many graduate courses are offered in English.

Another highly regarded program is the **Faculty of Science's Department of Mathematics**. This department has a long history of excellence in mathematical research and education. Students benefit from a challenging curriculum that covers a wide range of topics, from pure mathematics to applied mathematics. The department fosters a strong sense of community, with numerous student-led seminars and workshops. Graduates are well-prepared for careers in academia, research, and industry. The program's rigorous approach and emphasis on problem-solving skills make it attractive to international students seeking a solid foundation in mathematics. The program is constantly pushing boundaries and delving into cutting edge mathematics with applications into other sciences such as physics and computer science.

## üåç International Student Admissions & Life

The application process for international students at Tokyo University of Science typically involves several key steps. Most undergraduate programs require applicants to take the Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU) to assess their Japanese language proficiency and basic academic skills. In addition, applicants must demonstrate English language proficiency through standardized tests such as TOEFL or IELTS. The specific minimum scores may vary depending on the department and program, but a TOEFL iBT score of 80 or an IELTS score of 6.0 is generally expected. Some departments may also conduct interviews as part of the admissions process, either in person or via video conferencing.

While many undergraduate courses are taught in Japanese, Tokyo University of Science offers an increasing number of graduate programs entirely in English. These programs are particularly attractive to international students who may not have a strong command of the Japanese language. For example, the Graduate School of Science and Technology offers specific English-taught courses and programs. To find the most up-to-date list of English-taught programs, it is essential to visit the university's official website and consult the specific department's information.

The International Student Office at Tokyo University of Science provides a wide range of support services to international students. This includes assistance with visa applications, housing arrangements, and academic advising. The office also organizes cultural events and activities to help international students integrate into the university community and Japanese society. Academic advising is available to help students choose courses, develop study plans, and navigate the academic requirements of their programs. Counseling services are also available to provide support for students' personal and emotional well-being. TUS boasts roughly 700 international students from various countries creating a diverse and welcoming campus community. There is a wide variety of student clubs and organizations, catering to diverse interests such as sports, music, culture, and academics. The overall campus atmosphere is generally considered to be supportive and inclusive, although the academic rigor can be demanding.

Several scholarships are available to help international students finance their studies at Tokyo University of Science. These include:

*   **University-Specific Scholarships:** TUS offers its own scholarships for international students based on academic merit and financial need. The details and eligibility criteria for these scholarships can be found on the university's website.
*   **MEXT Scholarships (Japanese Government Scholarships):** These prestigious scholarships are offered by the Japanese Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology (MEXT). They cover tuition fees, provide a monthly stipend, and offer round-trip airfare. Application procedures vary; some students apply through the Japanese embassy in their home country, while others apply through the university after enrolling.
*   **Private Foundation Scholarships:** Various private foundations in Japan offer scholarships to international students. These scholarships often have specific eligibility requirements, such as nationality, field of study, or academic performance. Some prominent foundations include the Rotary Yoneyama Memorial Foundation and the JASSO (Japan Student Services Organization) scholarship.

The cost of living in Tokyo can be relatively high, but it is possible to manage expenses with careful budgeting. Here's a rough monthly budget estimate for an international student:

*   **Accommodation:** 50,000 - 80,000 JPY (depending on the type of housing)
*   **Food:** 30,000 - 50,000 JPY
*   **Transportation:** 10,000 - 20,000 JPY
*   **Utilities (electricity, gas, water):** 10,000 - 15,000 JPY
*   **Other Expenses (books, entertainment, etc.):** 20,000 - 30,000 JPY

**Total Monthly Budget:** 120,000 - 195,000 JPY (approximately)

This is just an estimate, and actual expenses may vary depending on individual spending habits. Living in university dormitories can significantly reduce accommodation costs. Also, students should be aware that Tokyo, despite being a large city, can be managed affordably, and a lot of free to low-cost entertainment is always available.

The Tokyo University of Science has multiple campuses, each catering to different faculties and departments. However, the main campus is located in Kagurazaka, Shinjuku, Tokyo, a vibrant and historic neighborhood known for its charming atmosphere, traditional restaurants, and unique blend of Japanese and Western culture. The area is also home to several other universities and educational institutions, creating a lively and intellectual environment. Kagurazaka offers a mix of traditional shops, modern boutiques, and a diverse culinary scene, making it an appealing location for students.
