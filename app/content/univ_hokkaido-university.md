## üè´ University Overview

Hokkaido University, established in 1876 as Sapporo Agricultural College, is one of Japan's leading national universities. Located in Sapporo, Hokkaido, the university is renowned for its extensive research activities and strong emphasis on international collaboration. It is one of the former Imperial Universities and is consistently ranked among the top universities in Japan and Asia. Hokkaido University is a designated National University Corporation, offering a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs across various disciplines.

The university's history is deeply rooted in the development of Hokkaido, with a mission to contribute to the advancement of society through education, research, and social contribution. It has a strong tradition of fostering independent-minded and globally aware individuals.

Hokkaido University's sprawling campus provides a unique environment for both academic pursuits and extracurricular activities. The campus itself is a tourist attraction, known for its natural beauty and historical buildings.

## üåç International Student Support

Hokkaido University offers extensive support for international students, including:

- **Dormitory:** On-campus and off-campus housing options are available for international students.
- **English Programs:** Various undergraduate and graduate programs are offered in English, particularly in the sciences and engineering.
- **Japanese Language Support:** Japanese language courses are offered to help international students improve their language skills.
- **Career Support:** Career counseling and job search assistance are provided to help international students find employment after graduation. The university also has a strong alumni network that supports career development.
- **Scholarships**: Hokkaido University offers a variety of scholarships for international students, including scholarships from the Japanese government (MEXT), private foundations, and the university itself. Application procedures and eligibility criteria vary depending on the scholarship.
- **Buddy Program**: The university has a buddy program to pair international students with local students to help them adjust to life in Japan and navigate the campus.
