## üè´ School Overview & Philosophy

Yoshida Japanese Language Academy, located in Nishi-Waseda, Shinjuku, Tokyo, is a Japanese language institution for students aiming to enter university. With a long history and tradition, it excels in tailored educational programs, particularly for science-related university admissions. The academy aims to provide comprehensive support for the overall preparation needed for Japanese university entrance exams, not just improving language skills. It assists students in succeeding in their university aspirations through EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission) preparation, personalized interview and essay guidance for each university, and academic advising. It also strives for small-group classes to provide careful guidance tailored to each student's learning progress and needs, creating an active classroom atmosphere that encourages students' active participation.

Yoshida Japanese Language Academy strives to enhance students' understanding of Japanese culture along with their Japanese language skills. Through various cultural activities and exchange programs, the academy supports students in adapting well to Japanese society and developing an international perspective. Furthermore, the experienced faculty serve as mentors, supporting and encouraging students' dreams, going beyond simply teaching Japanese. Students can freely discuss not only academic difficulties but also concerns about their overall lives, and the school is committed to students' successful study abroad experiences. In particular, for students aiming to enter science-related universities, the academy provides in-depth information and entrance exam strategies for science-related educational programs at Japanese universities, helping students choose the best university for them.

Yoshida Japanese Language Academy is not just a place to learn Japanese, but a place to build a foundation for pursuing dreams. Through thorough guidance, the academy helps students enter prestigious Japanese universities and supports their careers through a continuous network even after graduation. To meet the diverse needs of students, the school offers various courses, including a general Japanese course, a 2-year university preparation course, a 1-year and 9-month university preparation course, a 1-year and 6-month university preparation course, and a 1-year and 3-month university preparation course. Each course can be selected based on students' Japanese language abilities and learning goals, and all courses offer systematic curricula and guidance from experienced instructors. Students can efficiently learn by selecting a course that suits them and progress towards acceptance into their target university.

Yoshida Japanese Language Academy's teaching style aims for student-centered, participatory classes. Rather than instructors unilaterally delivering knowledge, they encourage active student participation and enhance learning through questions and discussions. Additionally, students are helped to learn Japanese enjoyably by utilizing various activities such as audio-visual materials and games. In particular, in EJU preparation classes, mock exams are conducted in an environment similar to the actual test to increase students' adaptability to the exam and to guide them in compensating for their weaknesses. In interview and essay classes, customized guidance is provided by identifying the strengths and weaknesses of individual students, helping them approach the exam with confidence.

## üéì University & Career Pathways

Yoshida Japanese Language Academy aims not only to teach Japanese but also to help students achieve their dreams. Therefore, the university admission support system is very systematic and comprehensive. The school provides customized academic advising, taking into account students' academic performance, aptitudes, and aspirations, and helps them choose the best university for them. In addition, all processes necessary for university entrance, such as EJU preparation, interview guidance, and essay guidance, are systematically supported. In particular, for students aiming to enter science-related universities, the academy provides in-depth information and entrance exam strategies for science-related educational programs at Japanese universities, helping students choose the best university for them.

Specifically, the school provides the following university admission support system: individual academic advising, EJU preparation courses, interview preparation courses, essay preparation courses, provision of university-specific entrance exam information, university visits, and meetings with graduates. Individual academic advising is conducted one-on-one with the homeroom teacher, and a customized university admission plan is established by understanding the student's academic performance, aptitude, and aspirations. EJU preparation courses analyze the characteristics of each subject of the EJU exam and present effective learning methods, and interview preparation courses conduct mock interviews in an environment similar to the actual interview situation to help students approach the interview with confidence. Essay preparation courses analyze the characteristics of each type of essay exam and present effective essay writing methods, and university-specific entrance exam information provides information on each university's entrance exam guidelines, competition rates, and average scores of successful applicants.

## üè° Accommodation & Living Support

Yoshida Japanese Language Academy does not operate its own dormitory, but provides students with various accommodation options through real estate agencies affiliated with the school. Generally, students can choose from share houses, studio apartments, and homestays. Share houses are a form of living where several students live together, and have the advantage of being able to find accommodation at a relatively low cost. Studio apartments have the advantage of being able to secure personal space, but are more expensive than share houses. Homestays have the advantage of being able to experience Japanese culture by living in a Japanese home, but are more expensive than other accommodation options and have the burden of having to adapt to home life.

The school provides information necessary for students to find accommodation and provides accommodation-related support, such as introducing real estate agencies. In addition, the school provides various living support services to help students adapt well to life in Japan. For example, it supports administrative procedures necessary for life, such as opening a bank account, setting up a mobile phone, and extending a visa, and provides various information and services such as medical consultations and information on part-time jobs. In particular, it operates a consultation service for international students to help students comfortably discuss their difficulties with life in Japan. In addition, the school operates a 24-hour emergency contact network for the safety of students and has established a system to be able to respond immediately in the event of an emergency.
