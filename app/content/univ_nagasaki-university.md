## üè´ University Overview & Reputation

Nagasaki University, located in the historically significant city of Nagasaki, is a prominent national university in Japan. While not part of the most elite 'Imperial Universities' group, it holds a strong regional presence and is recognized nationally for its contributions to research, particularly in fields related to medicine, engineering, and marine sciences. It is a respected institution with a commitment to internationalization and fostering global perspectives.

Founded in 1949 through the amalgamation of several pre-existing institutions, including the Nagasaki Medical College and the Nagasaki College of Economics, Nagasaki University carries a legacy deeply intertwined with the city's history, especially the atomic bombing of 1945. This historical context has instilled in the university a strong sense of social responsibility and a dedication to peace and international cooperation. The university's founding principles emphasize academic freedom, respect for humanity, and contribution to regional and global development.

Nagasaki University maintains a solid academic reputation, consistently ranking among the top 30-50 universities in Japan. It is particularly well-regarded for its medical programs, fisheries science, and engineering disciplines. Socially, the university is known for its welcoming atmosphere and strong connections to the local community. The university's graduates are actively sought after by both domestic and international employers, reflecting the quality of education and research conducted at the institution.

**Signature Programs:**

*   **Tropical Medicine and Global Health:** The Institute of Tropical Medicine (NEKKEN) at Nagasaki University is a globally recognized research center focusing on infectious diseases prevalent in tropical regions. This program offers advanced research opportunities and training in areas such as virology, parasitology, and immunology. International students can participate in research projects and contribute to the development of new diagnostics, treatments, and prevention strategies for diseases like malaria, dengue fever, and Zika virus. NEKKEN actively collaborates with international organizations and research institutions worldwide, providing students with valuable global networking opportunities.

*   **Fisheries Science:** Given Nagasaki's coastal location and history, the Faculty of Fisheries at Nagasaki University is a standout program. It provides comprehensive education and research opportunities in areas such as marine resource management, aquaculture, and seafood processing. The program emphasizes sustainable fisheries practices and the conservation of marine ecosystems. International students can benefit from hands-on training through research vessels, experimental fish farms, and collaborations with local fishing communities. This program is particularly attractive for students interested in careers in marine biology, fisheries management, and marine conservation.

*   **Peace and Reconciliation Studies:** As a city deeply affected by the atomic bombing, Nagasaki University has a strong commitment to peace and reconciliation. The university offers various courses and research programs related to peace studies, international relations, and conflict resolution. These programs examine the historical and social consequences of war and explore strategies for promoting peace and understanding. International students have the opportunity to engage in dialogue with survivors of the atomic bombing, participate in peace education initiatives, and contribute to research on disarmament and non-proliferation. This program is ideal for students interested in careers in diplomacy, international organizations, and peace advocacy.

## üåç International Student Admissions & Life

**Admissions Process:** The application process for international students at Nagasaki University typically involves several key steps. First, applicants must meet the academic requirements for their chosen program, which usually includes a high school diploma or equivalent for undergraduate programs and a bachelor's degree for graduate programs. Second, applicants are generally required to submit scores from the Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU) to demonstrate their Japanese language proficiency and academic aptitude. The specific EJU subjects required vary depending on the program of study. In addition to the EJU, some programs may require applicants to submit scores from English proficiency tests such as TOEFL or IELTS. Finally, shortlisted candidates may be invited for an interview, which can be conducted in person or via video conference. The interview aims to assess the applicant's motivation, communication skills, and suitability for the program.

**English-Taught Programs:** While Nagasaki University offers a range of programs, the number of degree programs taught entirely in English is relatively limited, particularly at the undergraduate level. However, there are several graduate programs offered in English, especially within the fields of Tropical Medicine and Global Health, Engineering, and Fisheries Science. The Graduate School of Biomedical Sciences offers a Master's and Doctoral program in Tropical Medicine. In the Faculty of Engineering, certain graduate-level courses are available in English. Detailed program information and specific requirements are available on the university's website, and potential applicants are strongly encouraged to consult the program details carefully.

**Student Support:** Nagasaki University provides a range of support services for international students. The International Student Support Office assists students with various aspects of their life in Japan, including visa applications, housing arrangements, and cultural adaptation. Academic advisors are available to provide guidance on course selection, research opportunities, and career planning. The university also offers counseling services to help students cope with stress and cultural adjustment challenges. Additionally, the university provides Japanese language courses for international students to improve their language skills and facilitate their integration into Japanese society.

**Campus Life & Diversity:** Nagasaki University has a diverse student body, with international students from various countries around the world. While the exact number fluctuates, typically there are several hundred international students enrolled at the university. The university has a variety of student clubs and organizations catering to different interests, including sports, culture, and academics. These clubs provide opportunities for international students to interact with Japanese students and participate in extracurricular activities. The campus atmosphere is generally welcoming and inclusive, with a strong emphasis on intercultural understanding and global citizenship.

**Cost of Living:** The cost of living in Nagasaki is generally lower compared to major cities like Tokyo or Osaka. Rent for a one-bedroom apartment near the university typically ranges from 40,000 to 60,000 JPY per month. Food expenses can be estimated at around 30,000 to 40,000 JPY per month, depending on dietary habits. Transportation costs are relatively low, with a monthly student pass costing around 5,000 to 8,000 JPY. Overall, a monthly budget of 80,000 to 120,000 JPY should be sufficient to cover basic living expenses in Nagasaki.
