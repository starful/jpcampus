## üìö Courses, Curriculum & Teaching Style

The curriculum at Kurashiki Foreign Language Academy is predominantly structured around its flagship University Progression Courses, designed to shepherd students from foundational Japanese ability to the level required for competitive university entrance. The core offerings include the standard two-year course (starting in April) and the intensive 1.5-year course (starting in October), both meticulously aligned with the academic calendar of Japanese universities. These courses are far more than general language instruction; they are integrated academic programs aimed at preparing students for the rigorous demands of higher education, including complex essay writing, advanced reading comprehension of academic texts, and specialized vocabulary essential for specific fields of study.

A key distinguishing feature of KFLA is the highly specialized curriculum tracks that move beyond general JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test) preparation. While JLPT N1 proficiency remains a goal, the academic focus is heavily placed on the Examination for Japanese University Admission (EJU). Students are divided into intensive classes focusing on specific EJU subjects: the Science Track (Rika, Mathematics Course 2) caters to engineering and natural science aspirants, providing necessary technical vocabulary and review of high school level physics, chemistry, and biology in Japanese. Simultaneously, the Arts/Design Track is specifically tailored for those entering disciplines such as fine art, architecture, product design, or manga/animation. This track includes specialized instruction in visual vocabulary, critical analysis, and crucial preparation for portfolio submissions and practical examinations required by prestigious art universities.

In addition to progression support, KFLA integrates practical communication skills throughout the curriculum. The 'Conversation' (ÌöåÌôî) component ensures that students develop fluid, natural spoken Japanese necessary for daily life and campus interactions, moving beyond textbook grammar into real-world communication strategies. Teaching methodologies at KFLA emphasize interactive learning, critical thinking, and a balanced approach to the four core skills: reading, writing, listening, and speaking.
