## üè´ University Overview & Reputation

Chukyo University, established in 1956 but tracing its roots back to the establishment of the Chukyo Commercial School in 1923, stands as a prominent private institution located in the vibrant city of Nagoya, the industrial and commercial heart of Japan's Tokai region. While it may not share the national recognition of the Kanto-based mega-universities often cited as the "Soukeisen" rivals, Chukyo is highly esteemed regionally, often grouped within the 'Meichuunan' consortium (alongside Meijo and Nanzan Universities) as one of the most prestigious private institutions in central Japan. This reputation is built upon a strong commitment to practical, career-focused education, fostering well-rounded individuals ready for professional life in industries concentrated in the Chubu area, particularly manufacturing, finance, and advanced technology. The university maintains a selective entry process, firmly positioning it in the top tier of private universities outside the immediate Tokyo-Osaka corridor.

The university‚Äôs founding philosophy centers on the motto "Pursue Truth and Cultivate Humanity." This dual focus emphasizes rigorous academic inquiry alongside the development of moral character, civic responsibility, and resilience. Chukyo particularly excels in the fields of Sports Science‚Äîwhere it frequently produces Olympic and international athletes‚ÄîPsychology, and Business Administration, frequently graduating influential business leaders who drive the regional economy. Its strong, long-standing ties to local industries ensure exceptionally high graduate employment rates, making it a reliable and desirable choice for students seeking stable, successful career paths immediately after graduation in one of Japan's most dynamic economic zones. Academically, Chukyo is known for its modern, interdisciplinary approach, constantly adapting its curricula to meet the evolving demands of the global workplace.

Chukyo is unique in its deliberate fusion of academic tradition and cutting-edge modernity, spread across two primary campuses. The main Yagoto campus in Nagoya is a vertically integrated, urban hub of commerce, humanities, and social sciences, contrasted slightly by the extensive Toyota Campus, which specializes in globally leading Sports Science and advanced Engineering facilities. The university has invested heavily in modernizing its infrastructure and expanding its global reach through academic partnerships. For international students, Chukyo represents an excellent opportunity to experience high-quality Japanese higher education outside the high-pressure, saturated environments of the Kanto or Kansai regions, offering a unique blend of regional industrial importance, cosmopolitan living in Nagoya, and a highly supportive academic environment.

### Signature Programs

**1. The Faculty of Sports Science (Toyota Campus):** Chukyo University is arguably most famous nationally and internationally for its Faculty of Sports Science, primarily located at the sprawling Toyota Campus. This faculty is far more than a simple athletic training ground; it is a serious academic hub dedicated to the comprehensive science behind human performance, physical health, and fitness culture. Its distinction lies in its holistic approach, covering highly specialized fields such as sports biomechanics, exercise physiology, mental training, coaching theory, and sports management. The university has successfully produced a disproportionately high number of world-class athletes, particularly in figure skating (with alumni like Shoma Uno and Mao Asada), swimming, and track. International students interested in high-level sports research, rehabilitation, physical education, or elite coaching will find unparalleled resources here, including advanced motion analysis laboratories and specialized conditioning centers. The curriculum is both scientifically rigorous and uniquely positioned due to its institutional history of elite athletic success.

The program maintains highly specialized tracks, allowing students to delve deeply into areas like child development psychology, neuroscience research, or mental health counseling, providing a clear path toward professional certification. For international students seeking postgraduate study or a career in the mental health field in Japan or globally, Chukyo offers a high-standard curriculum that meets demanding professional requirements and utilizes cutting-edge technology for experimental behavioral analysis and psychological testing.

The faculty prides itself on nurturing entrepreneurial spirit and equipping graduates with the skills demanded by multinational corporations. ## üåç International Student Admissions & Life

### Admissions Process

Admission for international undergraduate students at Chukyo University typically follows one of two main pathways: the General International Student Entrance Examination or a Specialized Examination for applicants residing overseas. The primary path requires candidates to have taken the **Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU)**. Required subjects usually concentrate on the Japanese language (with a high-score threshold), Mathematics (Course 1 or 2, depending on the faculty‚Äôs technical requirements), and either Japan and the World (for humanities) or two Science subjects (for STEM faculties). Achieving high proficiency in Japanese, often equivalent to JLPT N1 or a high N2 score, is mandatory for success in the EJU and subsequent coursework.

Following successful EJU results, applicants must submit an institutional application, which mandates academic transcripts, a detailed statement of purpose, and frequently, proof of financial stability. Most faculties, especially Law, Business, Psychology, and Global Studies, mandate an on-campus or online **interview**. This interview is critical, testing not only Japanese comprehension and fluency but also the applicant's motivation, critical thinking abilities, and specific interest in the chosen field. While standardized English proficiency tests like **TOEFL or IELTS** are not usually the primary entry requirement for Japanese-taught degrees, high scores are beneficial for demonstrating overall academic readiness and for placement in specialized English-medium courses within certain faculties. Admission cycles usually run in the fall (September/October) for April entry, demanding meticulous scheduling of the EJU examination.

### English-Taught Programs

Chukyo University currently offers very few full degree programs taught entirely in English at the undergraduate level. The majority of its highly reputed academic offerings are conducted exclusively in Japanese, requiring substantial competency in the language for full academic integration and success. The university operates primarily under the Japanese educational model, prioritizing deep linguistic immersion.

However, international students benefit from various **English Track Courses** and bilingual modules within key faculties, notably the **Faculty of Global Studies** and the **Faculty of Business Administration**. These are not full degrees in English but are specialized pathways where a significant portion (often between 30% and 50%) of specialized, core coursework is delivered in English, frequently utilizing international textbooks and sources. These tracks are designed for students who possess strong foundational Japanese skills but wish to specialize their professional academic focus using global frameworks and terminology. At the postgraduate level, certain specialized research laboratories and specific fields within Engineering and Information Sciences may conduct thesis supervision and research meetings entirely in English, contingent upon the supervising professor‚Äôs research area and international partnerships.

### Student Support

The **International Center** at Chukyo University serves as the vital hub for all non-academic and welfare support for foreign students. This dedicated office provides critical services, including comprehensive guidance on the complex student visa renewal process, mandatory registration with local Nagoya authorities, and securing suitable housing (including placement in university-affiliated dormitories and referral to local apartment agencies). Academically, every student is assigned a dedicated faculty advisor who assists with course registration, academic planning, and career guidance. The university also offers extensive, high-quality Japanese language courses tailored to improve academic Japanese proficiency beyond the initial entry level, ensuring long-term success. Furthermore, Chukyo operates robust and confidential counseling services, offering mental health support, adjustment counseling, and cross-cultural mentoring, which is essential for students navigating a new cultural and academic environment.

### Campus Life & Diversity

Chukyo University boasts a moderately diverse and highly dynamic student body, reflecting the international outlook of Nagoya city. While the overall number of international students is smaller compared to Japan's largest public universities, the cohort, numbering approximately 400-500 students, creates a vibrant and close-knit community. The university strongly encourages active participation in student life. There are hundreds of registered student clubs (circles), ranging widely from traditional Japanese arts (Calligraphy, Kyudo) to highly competitive sports, academic interest groups, and specialized international exchange and volunteer clubs. The atmosphere on the main Yagoto campus is energetic, sophisticated, and professional, situated directly in a bustling commercial and residential area of Nagoya. The university‚Äôs strong regional identity means that international students receive an authentic immersion experience, balancing academic rigor with exposure to the rich culture and powerful industrial landscape of the Chubu region.

### Scholarship Opportunities

Chukyo University offers several generous and targeted financial aid packages specifically for high-achieving international students.

1.  **Chukyo University International Student Scholarship:** This is the university‚Äôs primary internal support mechanism. Newly admitted students who demonstrate exceptional academic merit (based on EJU scores and institutional exam results) may be eligible for a significant tuition reduction, often ranging from 30% to 50% for the first year. Continuation of the scholarship into subsequent years is rigorously dependent upon maintaining a superior GPA (typically 3.0 or higher) and full-time enrollment status.
2.  **MEXT (Monbukagakusho) Scholarship:** While most applicants secure this government scholarship via pre-enrollment embassy recommendations, Chukyo University welcomes and supports MEXT scholars who enroll as either undergraduate or research students. This prestigious scholarship provides comprehensive financial coverage, including tuition waiver, a substantial monthly stipend (approx. ¬•117,000), and round-trip airfare, offering complete financial relief.
3.  **JASSO Honors Scholarship for Privately Financed International Students:** This highly sought-after stipend is available to currently enrolled international students who are privately financing their education and demonstrate outstanding academic performance. It provides a monthly allowance (approx. ¬•48,000) for a limited duration, contingent upon maintaining excellent grades and meeting EJU score criteria upon entry.
4.  **Private Foundation Scholarships:** The International Center actively liaises with various Japanese private foundations (e.g., Rotary Yoneyama, local Nagoya-based philanthropic groups) that offer supplemental monthly stipends (ranging from ¬•30,000 to ¬•100,000). Students are encouraged to apply for these external grants once enrolled, with the Center providing extensive guidance on the application process.

## üìç Campus Location & Access

### Main Campus Vibe

Chukyo University‚Äôs central hub, the **Yagoto Campus**, is the epitome of the modern, urban Japanese university. Strategically located in the desirable Tempaku Ward of Nagoya, the campus is surrounded by a sophisticated mix of upscale residential areas, bustling commercial shopping centers, and student-focused amenities. The campus itself is vertically organized and highly modern, reflecting Nagoya's status as a center of technological and economic advancement. The campus benefits from being directly integrated with the surrounding neighborhood, providing immediate access to a wealth of student-friendly services, restaurants, and study spots. The vibe is sophisticated, professional, and incredibly convenient, as the campus exit leads immediately to the subway station entrance. This prime location facilitates excellent part-time job opportunities and allows students effortless access to the cultural, historical, and recreational amenities of central Nagoya.

The secondary **Toyota Campus** is geographically distinct, located further east outside the immediate urban sprawl, offering extensive space for its specialized Sports Science and Engineering facilities. This campus is more focused and high-performance, featuring immense training grounds and state-of-the-art laboratories. Regular, reliable shuttle buses operate between the two campuses, ensuring seamless connection for students who need to attend classes or utilize resources at both locations.
