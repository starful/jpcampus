## 🏫 University Overview

St. Catherine University (聖カタリナ大学, *Sei Katarina Daigaku*) is a esteemed private, co-educational Catholic institution situated in the historic and scenic city of Matsuyama, Ehime Prefecture, on Japan's Shikoku Island. Established in 1966 by the Dominican Sisters of the Congregation of the Immaculate Conception of Montreal, Canada, the university is rooted in the spiritual legacy of Saint Catherine of Siena. Operating under the guiding Latin motto **"Amor et Servitium"** (Love and Service), St. Catherine University commits itself to nurturing compassionate professionals who are prepared to contribute deeply to human health, welfare, education, and community development.

Unlike large, impersonal mega-universities, St. Catherine prides itself on a close-knit, supportive learning community. With a low student-to-faculty ratio, the institution focuses heavily on individualized guidance, character building, and practical, hands-on training. It has built a formidable reputation in Western Japan as a premier training ground for social workers, registered dietitians, school teachers, nurse professionals, and sports instructors.

For international students, the university offers a warm, welcoming environment that provides a soft landing into Japanese society. It is an ideal destination for those who want to master the Japanese language, acquire specialized knowledge in human sciences or healthcare, and experience the rich cultural heritage and legendary hospitality of Ehime Prefecture.

## 🎓 Academic Programs & Faculty Structure

St. Catherine University offers undergraduate programs designed to meet the growing demands of modern health, welfare, and medical industries. The curriculum is split across two primary faculties, each offering specialized pathways:

### 1. Faculty of Human Health Sciences (人間健康学部)
This faculty focuses on the holistic preservation and enhancement of human life through social support, nutritional science, and physical health. It is divided into three highly specialized departments:
*   **Department of Social Welfare (社会福祉学科):** Prepares students to become licensed social workers, mental health social workers, and care professionals. The curriculum combines intensive theoretical coursework with extensive field placements in regional hospitals, nursing homes, and social services offices.
*   **Department of Health and Nutrition (健康栄養学科):** Dedicated to training registered dietitians and nutritionists. Students learn the science of food preparation, clinical nutrition, food safety, and public health nutrition, utilizing state-of-the-art kitchen and laboratory facilities.
*   **Department of Sport and Health Sciences (健康スポーツ学科):** Focuses on sports pedagogy, athletic training, health promotion, and community sports management. Graduates often pursue careers as health and physical education teachers, commercial gym trainers, athletic coaches, or corporate wellness advisors.

### 2. Faculty of Nursing (看護学部)
Located at the high-tech Matsuyama City Station Campus, this faculty is designed to train highly skilled, empathetic nursing professionals. The curriculum combines core scientific nursing principles with advanced clinical training. Students gain rich practical experience working directly with local hospitals and community clinics. The program prepares students for the Japanese National Nursing Examination and trains them to become leaders in local healthcare networks.

## 💴 Tuition, Fees & Financial Aid

St. Catherine University is committed to making high-quality private education accessible to international students. Compared to universities in Tokyo or Osaka, the cost of both education and living in Ehime is significantly more affordable.

### Standard Academic Fees (approximate in JPY):
*   **Admission Fee:** 200,000 JPY (Paid once upon enrollment)
*   **Yearly Tuition:** 
    *   *Faculty of Human Health Sciences:* ~780,000 to 820,000 JPY per year
    *   *Faculty of Nursing:* ~1,000,000 to 1,150,000 JPY per year (due to specialized clinical lab fees)
*   **Other Fees:** Textbook fees, insurance, and student association dues average around 50,000–80,000 JPY annually.

### Financial Support & Scholarships for International Students:
To ease the financial burden of studying abroad, St. Catherine University offers several robust support systems:
1.  **Tuition Fee Reductions:** Self-financed international students who demonstrate financial need and academic promise are eligible for a **30% to 50% reduction in annual tuition fees**.
2.  **MEXT & Local Scholarships:** The university actively assists qualified students in applying for the Japanese Government (MEXT) Honors Scholarship, as well as private foundation scholarships set up for international scholars residing in Shikoku.
3.  **Extremely Low Cost of Living:** Rent for a private studio apartment near the campus ranges between 25,000 to 45,000 JPY per month, which is less than half of what students typically pay in metropolitan Tokyo.

## 🗺️ Campus Life & Locations

St. Catherine University operates two distinct campuses, each offering a unique environment tailored to the student's field of study:

*   **Hojo Campus (Main Campus):** Nestled in a beautiful coastal setting overlooking the Seto Inland Sea, the Hojo Campus hosts the Faculty of Human Health Sciences. It features extensive green spaces, comprehensive athletic facilities (including a dedicated baseball field, soccer pitches, and gyms), a well-stocked library, and research laboratories. It provides a peaceful, safe, and academically focused atmosphere away from urban distractions.
*   **Matsuyama City Station Campus (Shiki Campus):** Situated right in the bustling heart of Matsuyama City, this campus houses the Faculty of Nursing. Highly modern and easily accessible by local tram, train, and bus networks, this urban campus allows students to balance rigorous clinical training with the vibrant lifestyle of downtown Matsuyama.

### Living in Matsuyama:
Matsuyama is famous for being one of Japan’s warmest, safest, and most culturally rich cities. It is home to **Dogo Onsen**, one of the oldest hot spring resorts in Japan, and the majestic Matsuyama Castle. International students enjoy a highly supportive local community, scenic coastal cycling paths, affordable local cuisine, and efficient public transportation.

## 🤝 International Student Support Systems

Transitioning to a new country can be challenging, but St. Catherine University provides structured, highly personalized support to ensure international students thrive academically and socially:

*   **The Peer Tutor System:** Every incoming international student is paired with a dedicated Japanese peer tutor. This student helper assists with registering for classes, purchasing textbooks, navigating local municipal offices, opening bank accounts, and practicing conversational Japanese.
*   **Specialized Japanese Language Support:** The university provides supplementary Japanese language classes designed to help students master the academic vocabulary needed for their majors and prepare for the **Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) N2 or N1 levels**.
*   **Housing Assistance:** While the university does not have a large-scale international dorm, the Student Affairs Office directly coordinates with trustworthy local real estate agencies to secure safe, furnished, and affordable apartments close to both campuses without requiring a traditional Japanese guarantor.
*   **Cultural Exchange Programs:** The international office regularly hosts cultural exchange events, cooking parties, local festival visits, and homestay opportunities, allowing students to integrate deeply into the Ehime community.

## 🚀 Career Prospects & Employment

One of the most compelling reasons to choose St. Catherine University is its consistently stellar graduate employment rate, which regularly exceeds **95%**. The university's strong focus on professional qualification fields (welfare, nursing, dietetics, and physical health) ensures that its students are highly sought after by regional employers.

### Employment Assistance Services:
*   **One-on-One Career Counseling:** Professional career advisors work individually with international students starting from their third year to help them write Japanese-style resumes (Rirekisho) and practice for job interviews.
*   **On-Campus Job Fairs:** The university regularly invites hospitals, welfare corporations, sports companies, and local businesses to campus for exclusive networking and recruitment sessions.
*   **Pathway to Working in Japan:** With Japan facing a severe demographic shortage in healthcare, nursing, and elder care, graduates from St. Catherine's social welfare and nursing programs find themselves in an exceptionally advantageous position to secure direct employment in Japan, complete with work visa sponsorship.

## 📝 Admission Process for International Applicants

St. Catherine University welcomes applicants from all over the world. The admission process is designed to evaluate both academic capability and a genuine passion for the chosen field of study.

### Key Requirements for Application:
1.  **Academic Eligibility:** Applicants must have completed 12 years of formal primary and secondary education outside of Japan.
2.  **Language Proficiency:** Since all undergraduate classes are conducted in Japanese, applicants must typically demonstrate a proficiency level equivalent to **JLPT N3 or higher** (or equivalent scores on the Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students - EJU).
3.  **Application Documents:** Submission of high school transcripts, a statement of purpose outlining the student's study plan and motivation, and financial sponsorship documentation.
4.  **Entrance Examination:** Eligible candidates are invited for an interview (often available online for overseas applicants) and a basic written exam or essay in Japanese to assess language competence and general aptitude.

*Prospective students are highly encouraged to contact the St. Catherine University Admissions Office early to receive the latest international student application booklet and verify specific application deadlines.*
