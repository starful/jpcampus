## 🏫 University Overview

Chiba University of Commerce (CUC), or Chiba Shoka Daigaku, is a prestigious private institution located in Ichikawa, Chiba Prefecture, just a stone's throw away from the heart of Tokyo. Founded in 1928 by Dr. Rikizo Kono, a visionary who believed in the harmony of ethics and economics, CUC has spent nearly a century cultivating "shido-jin"—leaders with a strong sense of social responsibility and practical business acumen. For international students, CUC represents a perfect blend of traditional academic rigor and modern, sustainable innovation. 

The university is not just a place for theory; it is a laboratory for real-world application. It stands out in the Japanese higher education landscape for its proactive stance on environmental issues and its deep-rooted connections with the Japanese corporate sector. With its motto of "Practical Learning," CUC ensures that every student graduates with the skills necessary to navigate the complexities of the global economy.

## 🎓 Academic Excellence and Faculties

CUC is organized into five primary faculties, each designed to address specific needs within the modern workforce. For an international student, choosing the right faculty is the first step toward a successful career in Japan or abroad.

### 1. Faculty of Commerce and Economics
As the founding faculty of the university, it remains the flagship program. It focuses on the mechanics of trade, accounting, and finance. Students learn how to manage businesses, understand market trends, and navigate the legal frameworks of commerce. This faculty is ideal for students aiming to work in Japanese trading companies (sogo shosha) or financial institutions.

### 2. Faculty of Economics
This faculty dives deeper into economic theory, social policy, and the analysis of data. In an era where big data drives decision-making, the Faculty of Economics at CUC emphasizes quantitative skills alongside traditional economic thought, preparing students for roles in research, policy analysis, and strategic planning.

### 3. Faculty of Policy Studies
Focusing on the intersection of law, politics, and society, this faculty prepares students to solve social issues through policy innovation. International students often find this faculty rewarding if they are interested in public administration, NGOs, or international organizations. The curriculum encourages critical thinking about how governance affects business and vice versa.

### 4. Faculty of Service Management
In a world where the service industry dominates the GDP of most developed nations, this faculty is highly relevant. It covers hospitality, tourism, and lifestyle services. It teaches students how to create value through customer experience, a core concept in Japanese "Omotenashi" (hospitality) culture.

### 5. Faculty of Human and Social Studies
This faculty explores the human element of business and society. It covers psychology, sociology, and sports management. Understanding human behavior is essential for modern marketing and management, making this faculty a popular choice for those interested in HR, counseling, or social entrepreneurship.

## 🌿 Japan's First RE100 University

One of CUC’s most remarkable achievements is its commitment to sustainability. CUC was the first university in Japan to join the RE100 initiative, a global corporate leadership initiative bringing together influential businesses committed to 100% renewable electricity. In 2019, the university achieved its goal of generating enough renewable energy (through its own solar power plants) to match the total electricity consumed across the campus. 

For international students, this means studying in an environment that practices what it preaches. The "CUC Student University Environmental Energy Group" allows students to participate directly in energy management and environmental policy. This unique focus on Green Transformation (GX) makes CUC graduates highly attractive to modern companies looking for ESG-conscious employees.

## 🌏 International Student Support and Life

CUC is renowned for its welcoming atmosphere. The International Center provides comprehensive support, from visa processing and housing assistance to Japanese language tutoring. 

### The Global Center
The Global Center acts as a hub for international exchange. It hosts events like the "Global Cafe," where students from different backgrounds can practice languages and share cultures. This integration helps international students overcome the initial "culture shock" and build a social network early on.

### Housing and Location
Located in Ichikawa, the campus is only about 20 minutes from Tokyo Station by train. This allows students to live in a relatively affordable, quiet residential area while having easy access to the job opportunities and cultural excitement of Tokyo. The university provides dormitory options and assists in finding private apartments, ensuring that students have a safe and comfortable home.

## 💼 Career Prospects and Job Placement

One of the strongest arguments for choosing CUC is its exceptional employment rate, which consistently exceeds 95%. The university’s Career Center is proactive, offering specialized programs for international students who wish to work in Japan. 

### Specialized Career Support:
- **Individual Counseling:** One-on-one sessions to help students identify their career goals and prepare for the Japanese job-hunting process (Shu-katsu).
- **Job Hunting Seminars:** Workshops on writing Japanese-style resumes (Rirekisho) and mastering the art of the Japanese interview.
- **Company Information Sessions:** CUC hosts numerous companies on campus specifically looking to hire international talent.
- **Internship Programs:** The university facilitates internships with local businesses, providing students with invaluable experience in a Japanese professional setting.

## 📝 Admission Guidance for International Students

Entering Chiba University of Commerce requires careful preparation. The university generally offers a special entrance examination for international students (Ryugakusei Nyushi).

### Key Requirements:
1. **Japanese Language Proficiency:** Most programs require a level equivalent to JLPT N2 or higher, as lectures are primarily conducted in Japanese. Some specialized tracks may accept N3 if the student shows high potential.
2. **EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students):** Applicants are typically required to submit EJU scores for "Japanese as a Foreign Language" and "General Subjects" (Japan and the World).
3. **Interview:** A personal interview is a crucial part of the process. The faculty looks for motivation, a clear career plan, and the ability to communicate ideas effectively in Japanese.
4. **Document Review:** Academic transcripts from your home country, a statement of purpose, and letters of recommendation are thoroughly evaluated.

## 💰 Tuition and Financial Aid

CUC understands the financial burden of studying abroad and offers several scholarship opportunities to alleviate this. 

- **Tuition Reduction:** International students with good academic standing and financial need can apply for a 30% reduction in tuition fees.
- **CUC Scholarships:** Internal scholarships based on merit and university contribution.
- **External Scholarships:** The university assists students in applying for JASSO (Japan Student Services Organization) scholarships and various private foundation grants.

While the first-year cost is approximately 1.25 million JPY (including admission fees), the subsequent years are lower. When combined with scholarships and part-time work (within the legal limit of 28 hours per week), a degree at CUC is a highly attainable goal for many.

## 🗼 Why Choose Chiba University of Commerce?

Choosing a university is not just about the ranking; it's about the "fit." CUC is the ideal choice for international students who:
- **Want to work in Japan:** The university's focus on practical business and its location near Tokyo make it a powerful engine for employment.
- **Value Sustainability:** Those interested in how business can solve environmental problems will find no better home in Japan than CUC.
- **Prefer a Supportive Environment:** Unlike massive national universities where you might be just a number, CUC offers a community-centric experience where professors and staff know you by name.
- **Seek Balance:** You get the peace of a Chiba suburb with the skyline of Tokyo visible on the horizon, offering the best of both worlds in terms of lifestyle.

In conclusion, Chiba University of Commerce is a forward-thinking, industry-aligned institution that prepares students for the challenges of the 21st century. It bridges the gap between academic theory and the practical needs of the global marketplace, all while fostering a deep respect for the environment and social ethics. For an international student looking to build a career in the world's third-largest economy, CUC is a gateway to a bright and sustainable future.
