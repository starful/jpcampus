## 🏫 School Overview & Philosophy

UNITAS Japanese Language School, Tokyo Campus (ユニタス日本語学校東京校) is a premier educational institution located in the pulsating heart of Tokyo's Shinjuku district. Operating from the 3rd floor of the 22 Sankyo Building at 2-2-9 Okubo, Shinjuku-ku, the school offers students an unparalleled base for both rigorous academic preparation and immersive urban living. With a substantial capacity of 840 students, UNITAS is a bustling, energetic environment designed to support high-achieving individuals aiming to make their mark in Japan.

At the core of the UNITAS philosophy is the belief that language learning is not merely an academic exercise, but a gateway to future opportunities. As part of a larger educational network, the Tokyo Campus places immense emphasis on fostering a global perspective. The school strives to bridge cultures by gathering a diverse array of students from all corners of the globe and training them to become fluent, culturally competent leaders. Whether your goal is to pass the rigorous entrance exams for a top-tier Japanese university, secure a position at a prestigious graduate school, or transition smoothly into the Japanese corporate sector, the school’s academic framework is built to help you reach those heights.

Choosing UNITAS Tokyo means positioning yourself in a supportive, high-energy environment. The administration balances its strict academic standards with a warm, caring approach, ensuring that every international student feels secure and motivated from the day they arrive.

## 📚 Courses, Curriculum & Teaching Style

The academic structure at UNITAS Tokyo Campus is highly organized, focusing intensely on practical language acquisition alongside targeted examination preparation. For students aiming for higher education, the school's intensive **Shingaku (Academic Preparation)** tracks are designed to build the specialized language skills needed to tackle the EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission) and the JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test) levels N2 and N1.

Classes are structured to accommodate students at all proficiency levels, from absolute beginners learning basic hiragana and katakana to advanced learners analyzing modern literature and economic articles. The teaching style is highly interactive, balancing grammar, vocabulary, and kanji drills with spoken communication and debate to ensure your spoken Japanese remains as sharp as your written test scores.

## 🌍 Student Body & Campus Life

With a capacity of 840 students, the UNITAS Tokyo Campus is a miniature global village. Unlike smaller schools that may be dominated by one or two student nationalities, UNITAS takes pride in cultivating a diverse international student body. This rich cultural mix encourages students to use Japanese as their primary medium of communication, even outside the classroom, accelerating their real-world fluency.

Located in Shin-Okubo (Shinjuku-ku), the campus sits inside Tokyo's most vibrant multicultural hub. Famous for its international grocery stores, diverse dining options, and close proximity to the ultra-modern business districts of Shinjuku, Shin-Okubo offers a fascinating backdrop for daily student life. Within walking distance of the school, you will find incredibly cheap and delicious food options from all over Asia, making it highly affordable for students on a budget.

Campus life at UNITAS goes far beyond vocabulary lists and exams. The school organizes regular cultural exchange events, seasonal festivals, and field trips throughout Tokyo. These activities allow you to practice your Japanese in natural settings, bond with classmates from different programs, and build lifelong friendships that span the globe.

## 🎓 University & Career Pathways

For students with academic ambitions, UNITAS Tokyo Campus is an exceptional launching pad. The school is highly regarded by Japanese colleges and vocational schools for producing disciplined, highly capable graduates. 

### Intensive EJU & JLPT Training
Understanding that high exam scores are the key to unlocking premium higher education opportunities in Japan, UNITAS provides intensive, structured preparation for the EJU and JLPT. Faculty members conduct targeted seminars on exam-taking strategies, speed-reading, and logical essay writing (Shoronbun)—a component that many international students find particularly challenging.

### Comprehensive University & Graduate School Counseling
Navigating the college application process in a foreign country can be overwhelming. To address this, UNITAS provides dedicated, one-on-one counseling. Advisors assist you in:
- Selecting the right universities or vocational schools (Senmon Gakko) based on your career goals and academic performance.
- Drafting compelling personal statements and research proposals for graduate school applicants.
- Conducting mock interviews to build your confidence and refine your formal Japanese communication style.
- Utilizing recommendation systems (Suisen) that give qualified UNITAS students preferential admission pathways to partner universities.

## 💡 Practical Tips for Applicants

To make your transition to UNITAS Japanese Language School, Tokyo Campus as smooth as possible, keep the following practical advice in mind:

1. **Choose the Right Intake Term:** The Japanese academic year begins in April. If you plan to enter a Japanese university or vocational school immediately after graduating from UNITAS, enrolling in the **April 2-Year Course** or the **October 1 Year 6 Months Course** aligns perfectly with academic transition cycles.
2. **Budget for Shinjuku Living Costs:** While the tuition at UNITAS is highly competitive, living in the heart of Tokyo requires careful budgeting. Rent in Shinjuku-ku and surrounding areas can be high. Consider looking for sharehouses or student dormitories recommended by the school to reduce monthly expenses. Utilizing local international supermarkets in Shin-Okubo is also a fantastic way to cook affordably.
3. **Prepare for Visa Screening Early:** High-capacity schools in Tokyo require meticulous documentation for visa approval. Gather your financial sponsorship records, academic diplomas, and proof of basic Japanese study early to avoid last-minute delays.
4. **Apply for Scholarships:** UNITAS offers generous scholarship opportunities and financial aid for outstanding students who demonstrate both academic excellence and high attendance. Make it a priority to maintain near-perfect attendance from day one to qualify for these valuable grants.
5. **Part-Time Work Considerations:** If you intend to work part-time (up to 28 hours per week allowed under the student visa), Shin-Okubo and Shinjuku offer an abundance of employment opportunities in retail, hospitality, and language services. However, ensure that your work hours never compromise your class attendance and study time, as UNITAS maintains strict academic standards.
