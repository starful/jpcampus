## üìö Courses, Curriculum & Teaching Style

Yokkaichi Japanese Language School‚Äôs curriculum is meticulously engineered for university progression, structured around two primary long-term courses, the **Progression I Course** and the **Progression II Course**, corresponding typically to 1.5-year (October start) and 2-year (April start) schedules, respectively. *   **EJU and Academic Track Specialization:**
    Students are streamed into dedicated classes focusing on the EJU subject material, which includes Mathematics, Japan and the World (Liberal Arts), and Science (Physics, Chemistry, Biology). *   **Science and Liberal Arts Pathways:**
    For students aiming for technical or medical fields, the **Science Track** provides intensive instruction in the required Science EJU subjects and specialized Japanese vocabulary required for engineering and scientific research. Conversely, the **Liberal Arts Track** focuses on the 'Japan and the World' EJU component, along with essay writing and critical reading skills essential for humanities, social sciences, and business majors. This early bifurcation ensures that instruction is relevant and maximizes study efficiency, preventing students from spending time on unnecessary subjects.
*   **Art and Design Progression Track:**
    Recognizing the growing demand for specialized preparation for art universities and vocational design schools, Yokkaichi Japanese Language School offers specific guidance for portfolio preparation and practical skill development. The school‚Äôs teaching style is characterized by interactive, student-centered learning, utilizing modern technology and engaging, project-based assignments to reinforce understanding and practical application, moving beyond traditional lecture formats to create proactive and confident Japanese speakers.
