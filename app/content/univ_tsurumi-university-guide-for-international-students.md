## 🏫 University Overview

Tsurumi University (鶴見大学, Tsurumi Daigaku) is a private institution located in the heart of Yokohama, Kanagawa Prefecture. Founded on the spiritual principles of the Soto Zen sect of Buddhism, it is uniquely positioned adjacent to the majestic Soji-ji Temple, one of the two head temples of the Soto school. This spiritual grounding provides a serene and disciplined atmosphere that permeates the campus, fostering not only academic excellence but also the moral development of its students. The university's philosophy is rooted in "Zen and the Heart," aiming to produce graduates who are compassionate, mindful, and technically proficient in their chosen fields.

While Tsurumi University is relatively specialized compared to the massive multidisciplinary universities in Tokyo, it holds a prestigious reputation in two distinct areas: Dental Medicine and the Humanities. The university is particularly famous for its School of Dental Medicine, which is considered one of the leading private dental programs in Japan, and its Faculty of Literature, which offers deep expertise in Japanese literature, library science, and cultural heritage conservation.

For international students, Tsurumi University offers an intimate environment where students are not just numbers. The faculty-to-student ratio is favorable, allowing for personalized guidance—a crucial factor for those navigating a new cultural and linguistic landscape. The proximity to both Tokyo and central Yokohama makes it a strategic choice for students who want to enjoy the vibrancy of Japan's urban centers while studying in a calm, focused environment.

## 🎓 Academic Excellence and Faculties

### 1. School of Dental Medicine
The School of Dental Medicine is the flagship of Tsurumi University. Since its establishment, it has been a powerhouse in producing high-quality dental practitioners and researchers. The curriculum is rigorous, integrating basic medicine with advanced clinical practice. Students benefit from the Tsurumi University Dental Hospital, one of the largest and most advanced dental facilities in the region, where they observe and participate in cutting-edge treatments. For international students aiming for the Japanese national dental licensure, Tsurumi provides intensive support and a high-tech environment. The school’s research output in oral health, biomaterials, and geriatric dentistry is recognized globally.

### 2. Faculty of Literature
The Faculty of Literature is a hub for the preservation and study of Japanese culture. It is divided into several specialized departments:
- **Department of Japanese Literature:** Focuses on everything from ancient 'Man'yoshu' poetry to modern novelists. It is a prime destination for international scholars of Japanese classics.
- **Department of English and American Literature:** Offers a global perspective, focusing on linguistics and cross-cultural communication.
- **Department of Cultural Properties:** This is a standout program, particularly for international students interested in archeology and the physical restoration of historical artifacts. Japan has strict laws regarding cultural heritage, and Tsurumi is one of the few places where students can learn the scientific and traditional methods of preserving wood, paper, and metal relics.
- **Department of Library, Archival and Information Studies:** Tsurumi is legendary in Japan for this field. The university has historically produced some of the nation’s top librarians and archivists.

### 3. Junior College
The Junior College focuses on Early Childhood Care and Education. While primarily attracting domestic students, its focus on the "Zen spirit" in childcare is a unique pedagogical approach that interests international educational researchers.

## 📚 The Tsurumi University Library: A National Treasure

One cannot discuss Tsurumi University without mentioning its library. It is widely regarded as one of the best university libraries in Japan, particularly in the fields of Japanese literature and Buddhist studies. It houses an extensive collection of rare books, including medieval manuscripts and ancient Buddhist texts that attract researchers from across the globe. For an international student, having access to these archives is an unparalleled advantage, especially for those pursuing graduate-level research in humanities. The library’s architecture and study spaces are designed to facilitate long hours of deep focus, reflecting the Zen ethos of the campus.

## ⛩️ Zen Spirit and Campus Life

The university’s connection to Soji-ji Temple is more than just geographical. Students often participate in Zen meditation sessions (Zazen), which help in developing mental clarity and stress management. This is particularly beneficial for students in the high-pressure School of Dental Medicine. The campus itself is a blend of modern academic buildings and traditional temple architecture, surrounded by lush greenery that changes beautifully with the seasons.

International students often find the atmosphere more welcoming and less overwhelming than larger universities. There are various student clubs (Bukatsu) and circles, ranging from traditional arts like tea ceremony and calligraphy to modern sports and music. Participating in these clubs is the best way for international students to integrate and practice their Japanese in a natural setting.

## 🌏 Admissions and International Support

### Admission Requirements
For the Faculty of Literature, international applicants generally need to provide EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students) scores. A strong command of the Japanese language (JLPT N2 or N1) is essential, as the curriculum is deeply rooted in Japanese texts. For the School of Dental Medicine, the requirements are much higher, involving rigorous science examinations and a high level of Japanese proficiency suited for medical communication.

### Support Systems
Tsurumi University offers various support mechanisms for international students, including:
- **Tuition Reductions:** Many international students qualify for a 30% reduction in tuition fees based on academic merit and financial need.
- **Scholarships:** In addition to the JASSO scholarships, Tsurumi offers internal scholarships specifically for high-achieving international students.
- **Tutoring:** The university provides peer support programs where Japanese students assist international newcomers with language and daily life challenges.

## 📍 Location: Tsurumi, Yokohama

Tsurumi is a vibrant district of Yokohama with a unique history. It has long been a gateway for diverse cultures, which is reflected in its local food and community events. 
- **Accessibility:** Tsurumi Station is served by the JR Keihin-Tohoku Line and the Tsurumi Line, making it incredibly easy to reach Yokohama Station (10 minutes) or Tokyo Station (30 minutes). Haneda Airport is also remarkably close, which is convenient for international travel.
- **Living Costs:** Compared to central Tokyo (like Shinjuku or Minato), Tsurumi offers more affordable housing options for students while maintaining the convenience of a metropolitan area.
- **Culture:** Yokohama is Japan’s second-largest city and is known for its international atmosphere, beautiful harbor (Minato Mirai), and the largest Chinatown in Japan. Living here allows students to experience a cosmopolitan lifestyle.

## 💼 Career Prospects

Graduates of Tsurumi University have a strong track record of employment. 
- **Dental Graduates:** Most go on to pass the national exam and work in major hospitals, private practices, or enter the university’s own robust graduate school for specialized research.
- **Literature Graduates:** Many find employment in education, publishing, and government sectors. The Department of Library and Information Studies has a particularly strong network, often placing graduates in prestigious municipal and academic libraries.
- **Cultural Properties Graduates:** This niche specialization leads to careers in museums, government agencies for cultural affairs, and specialized restoration companies throughout East Asia.

For international students, the university's career center provides counseling on how to navigate the Japanese job market, including assistance with 'shukatsu' (job hunting) etiquette, resume writing, and interview preparation.

## 📝 Conclusion for International Applicants

Tsurumi University is an ideal choice for a specific type of international student: one who seeks a quiet, focused environment and is passionate about specialized fields like Dentistry, Japanese Literature, or Cultural Heritage. It is not a place to 'get lost in the crowd'; rather, it is a place to be mentored and to develop a deep expertise. If you value the intersection of modern science, traditional culture, and spiritual mindfulness, Tsurumi University offers a profound educational experience in one of Japan's most convenient and attractive locations.
