## üè´ University Overview: The Spirit of 'Jindai'

Kanagawa University, affectionately known in Japan as "Jindai," is one of the most prestigious and long-standing private universities in the Kanto region. Founded in 1928 by Yoshimori Yoneda as the Yokohama Academy, the institution was built on the philosophy of "Growth through Self-Education." For nearly a century, it has remained committed to cultivating individuals who possess both specialized expertise and a broad international perspective. 

Located in the vibrant port city of Yokohama, the university serves as a gateway between Japan and the rest of the world. With its recent expansion, including the opening of the landmark Minato Mirai Campus, Kanagawa University has solidified its position as a top-tier destination for international students seeking a modern, globally-focused education within a traditional Japanese academic framework.

## üìç Campus Locations and Atmosphere

### 1. Minato Mirai Campus (The Global Hub)
Opened in 2021, this 21-story vertical campus is a marvel of modern architecture situated in the heart of Yokohama's business district. It houses the Faculty of Foreign Languages, the Faculty of Business Administration, and the Faculty of Cross-Cultural and Japanese Studies. The campus is designed as a 'social common' where students, researchers, and the local business community interact. It features a stunning library, the 'Global Lounge' for language exchange, and open-access areas that encourage collaboration. For international students, this campus offers an urban lifestyle with immediate proximity to major corporations and international organizations.

### 2. Yokohama Campus (The Academic Heart)
Located in Rokkakubashi, this campus retains the traditional academic atmosphere of a Japanese university. It is the primary base for the Faculty of Law, Economics, Humanities, Science, Engineering, and Architecture. Surrounded by residential areas and local shopping streets, it offers a quieter environment conducive to deep study. The campus is well-equipped with advanced laboratories for science and engineering students and extensive athletic facilities.

## üéì Academic Excellence & Specialized Faculties

Kanagawa University offers a diverse range of undergraduate and graduate programs. Here is a breakdown of the key faculties popular among international students:

- **Faculty of Foreign Languages:** This is one of the university's flagship programs. It offers intensive studies in English, Spanish, Chinese, and Korean. The faculty emphasizes 'Global Experience,' encouraging students to engage in study abroad programs and cross-cultural communication workshops.
- **Faculty of Business Administration:** Located at the Minato Mirai Campus, this faculty focuses on practical business skills, management theory, and international trade. It benefits greatly from its location, often hosting guest speakers from neighboring corporate headquarters.
- **Faculty of Engineering & Faculty of Architecture:** Kanagawa University is renowned for its technical education. The Faculty of Architecture, in particular, is highly competitive, producing architects who specialize in sustainable design and earthquake-resistant structures.
- **Faculty of Cross-Cultural and Japanese Studies:** This is a vital department for international students. It provides deep insights into Japanese history, culture, and society, while also offering the 'Japanese Language and Culture Program' (JLC) designed specifically to elevate the language proficiency of non-native speakers.

## üåè International Student Support and Integration

One of the university's greatest strengths is its dedicated support system for international students. The **International Center** acts as a one-stop shop for everything from visa renewals and housing assistance to career counseling. 

- **The Global Lounge:** A dedicated space where international and Japanese students meet daily to chat in various languages, participate in cultural exchange events, and celebrate international holidays. It is the heart of student integration.
- **Tutor System:** New international students are often paired with a Japanese student 'tutor' who helps them navigate the complexities of Japanese life, from opening a bank account to understanding course registration.
- **Japanese Language Education:** Unlike some universities where international students are left to fend for themselves linguistically, Jindai offers robust Japanese as a Second Language (JSL) courses integrated into the curriculum, ensuring students can reach JLPT N1 proficiency by graduation.

## üí∞ Tuition and Financial Aid (The Yoneda Scholarship)

Kanagawa University is well-known for being financially accessible compared to other major private universities in Tokyo. The **Yoneda Yoshimori Memorial Scholarship** is a unique and generous system that provides significant tuition reductions or stipends to high-achieving international students. 

- **Admissions Fee:** Approximately 200,000 JPY (one-time payment).
- **Annual Tuition:** Ranges from 1,000,000 JPY to 1,500,000 JPY depending on whether the student is in a Humanities or Science/Engineering track.
- **Tuition Reduction:** International students with financial need and good academic standing can apply for a 30% reduction in tuition fees. 

## üìù Admission Process for International Students

Admissions are typically conducted through the **'Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students' (EJU)**. 

1.  **Selection Criteria:** Most faculties require EJU scores in 'Japanese as a Foreign Language,' 'Japan and the World' (Liberal Arts), or 'Mathematics/Science' (for STEM tracks).
2.  **English Proficiency:** For programs in the Faculty of Foreign Languages or Business Administration, submitting TOEIC or TOEFL scores is highly recommended and often mandatory.
3.  **Interview and Essay:** Shortlisted candidates are usually invited for an interview (on-campus or online) and a short essay exam to assess their critical thinking and motivation.

## üè† Life in Yokohama

Choosing Kanagawa University means living in Yokohama, Japan‚Äôs second-largest city. Yokohama offers a higher quality of life with slightly lower living costs than central Tokyo. 

- **Housing:** The university provides international student dormitories such as the 'Rikkyu Hall,' which are affordable and located within commuting distance. Many students also opt for 'share houses' in the Kanagawa-ku area.
- **Part-time Work:** Being a major commercial hub, Yokohama provides endless opportunities for part-time work in hospitality, education (English teaching), and logistics. The university's career center provides guidance on finding jobs that comply with student visa regulations.
- **Connectivity:** From the Minato Mirai campus, Shibuya is only 30 minutes away via the Tokyu Toyoko Line, making it easy to enjoy the excitement of Tokyo while living in a more spacious port city.

## üöÄ Career Prospects and Employment Support

Kanagawa University prides itself on its high employment rate. The **Career Support Center** provides specialized workshops for international students, focusing on the unique aspects of 'Shu-katsu' (Japanese job hunting). 

- **Business Japanese Courses:** Specialized classes on honorifics (Keigo) used in corporate settings.
- **Company Research Fairs:** Every year, hundreds of companies, including major manufacturers, banks, and IT firms, visit the campus specifically to recruit Jindai students.
- **Alumni Network:** The university has a massive alumni network (Kyu-shinkai) that spans across major Japanese corporations, providing students with valuable 'OB/OG' (Old Boy/Old Girl) visitation opportunities.
