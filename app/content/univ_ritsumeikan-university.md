## üè´ University Overview

Ritsumeikan University (Á´ãÂëΩÈ§®Â§ßÂ≠¶, Ritsumeikan Daigaku) is a prominent private university based in Kyoto, Japan, with additional campuses in Shiga and Osaka. Established in 1869 as a private academy and formally recognized as a university in 1922, Ritsumeikan has a rich history rooted in a spirit of freedom and innovation. It is renowned as one of the 'KAN-KAN-DORITSU' universities, a prestigious group of leading private institutions in the Kansai region.

The university is part of the extensive Ritsumeikan Trust, which also includes various primary, junior high, and high schools, reflecting a comprehensive educational philosophy. Ritsumeikan is celebrated for its balanced approach to education and research, offering a wide array of programs across humanities, social sciences, and natural sciences. It has consistently ranked among the top private universities in Japan, particularly excelling in internationalization and interdisciplinary studies.

Ritsumeikan University was selected as a Type B institution in Japan's Top Global University Project (SGU), a government initiative aimed at enhancing the international competitiveness of Japanese universities. This designation underscores its commitment to fostering global leaders and promoting international academic exchange. The university boasts a diverse student body, with over 2,600 international students from more than 90 countries and regions, contributing to a vibrant multicultural campus environment. Its robust alumni network extends worldwide, providing valuable connections and career opportunities for graduates.

## üåç International Student Support

Ritsumeikan University places a strong emphasis on providing comprehensive support for its international student community, ensuring a smooth transition and enriching academic experience in Japan.

*   **Dormitory**: The university offers several housing options for international students, including dedicated international dormitories such as International House, Ritsumeikan Global Gate, and various university-approved private apartments. New international students often receive priority for these accommodations, which are designed to foster cross-cultural interaction and provide a safe, convenient living environment.

*   **English Programs**: Ritsumeikan is a pioneer in offering English-medium degree programs. Notable examples include the College of Global Liberal Arts (GLA) and the Community and Regional Policy Studies (CRPS) program, along with several graduate programs taught entirely in English. For students enrolled in Japanese-taught programs, a growing number of individual courses are also offered in English, providing flexibility and support.

*   **Scholarships**: A wide array of scholarships is available specifically for international students. These include generous Ritsumeikan University tuition reduction scholarships, various Japanese government (MEXT) scholarships, scholarships from the Japan Student Services Organization (JASSO), and numerous private foundation scholarships. These financial aid options significantly help offset tuition and living expenses.

*   **Career Support**: The university provides dedicated career services tailored to the needs of international students. This includes workshops on Japanese job-hunting etiquette, resume writing, interview preparation, and opportunities to connect with potential employers through job fairs and company visits. Individual career counseling and a strong global alumni network further assist students in their post-graduation career paths.

*   **Support Services**: The International Center serves as a central hub for international student support, offering assistance with visa procedures, academic advising, cultural adjustment counseling, and organizing various cultural exchange events and activities to help students integrate into university life and Japanese society.


## üìç Campus Location & Access

Ritsumeikan University operates across three main campuses, each offering a distinct academic environment and accessibility. These campuses are strategically located in key areas of Kyoto, Shiga, and Osaka, leveraging the cultural richness and economic dynamism of the Kansai region.

| Campus | Location | Main Access Points |
|---|---|---|
| **Kinugasa Campus** | Kita Ward, Kyoto, Japan | The main campus in north-western Kyoto, accessible by city bus from JR Kyoto Station (approx. 40 mins) or Randen Kitano-Hakubaicho Station (approx. 15 mins by bus). |
| **Biwako-Kusatsu Campus (BKC)** | Kusatsu, Shiga, Japan | Located on the shores of Lake Biwa, accessible by shuttle bus from JR Biwako Line Minami-Kusatsu Station (approx. 20 mins). Home to many science and engineering faculties. |
| **Osaka Ibaraki Campus (OIC)** | Ibaraki, Osaka, Japan | A newer campus offering a blend of urban convenience and modern facilities, accessible by shuttle bus from JR Ibaraki Station (approx. 10 mins) or Hankyu Minami-Ibaraki Station (approx. 15 mins). |
