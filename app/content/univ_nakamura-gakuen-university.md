## 🏫 University Overview

Nakamura Gakuen University (NGU) is a prestigious private university located in Fukuoka City, Japan. Established in 1965, the university was founded on the visionary principles of Haru Nakamura, an educator who believed that academic achievement, personal integrity, and physical vitality are fundamentally rooted in proper nutrition and healthy lifestyle practices. Over more than half a century, the university has evolved into a premier hub for human development, specialized research, and professional training in Western Japan.

While NGU is historically famous for its industry-leading programs in **Nutritional Sciences**, it has successfully expanded its academic portfolio. Today, it features thriving faculties in **Distribution and Marketing Studies** as well as **Education and Psychology**. The university's core educational philosophy is encapsulated in the concept of *Shokuku* (food education), emphasizing that dietary habits form the foundation of human health, spirit, and societal well-being. For international students, Nakamura Gakuen University offers a uniquely supportive, close-knit academic environment where practical skills, industry connections, and local community integration are highly prioritized.

## 🎓 Academic Faculties and Departmental Analysis

Nakamura Gakuen University structures its academic disciplines into three specialized faculties, each designed to meet modern societal and industrial demands.

### 1. Faculty of Nutritional Sciences (栄養科学部)
This is the flagship faculty of NGU, holding a sterling reputation across Japan. It comprises two primary departments:
*   **Department of Nutritional Sciences (栄養科学科):** Highly focused on producing Registered Dietitians (*Kanri Eiyoshi*). The curriculum integrates advanced chemistry, human biology, clinical nutrition, and food toxicology. International students gain hands-on clinical training, preparing them for careers in healthcare facilities, research labs, and public health sectors.
*   **Department of Food Science and Technology (フード・マネジメント学科):** This department bridges the gap between culinary arts, food safety, global logistics, and product development. Students study food chemistry, sensory evaluation, manufacturing processes, and restaurant/food business management. It is highly suited for international students looking to enter the global food supply and manufacturing industries.

### 2. Faculty of Distribution and Marketing Studies (流通科学部)
Recognized as a unique department in the Kyushu region, this faculty approaches economics and business from the specific lens of distribution systems, global supply chains, and market dynamics. 
*   **Key Focus Areas:** Retail management, marketing strategy, international trade, and commercial logistics.
*   **Practical Learning:** The faculty emphasizes case studies of multinational corporations, active-learning seminars, and joint projects with Fukuoka’s thriving retail and logistics sectors. This is an exceptional pathway for international students aspiring to work in Japanese trading companies or set up global business networks.

### 3. Faculty of Education and Psychology (教育学部)
Designed for those who wish to contribute to child development, primary education, and psychological well-being. It is divided into:
*   **Department of Child Education (児童幼児教育学科):** Focuses on early childhood education and elementary school teaching methodologies.
*   **Department of Developmental Clinical Psychology (発達支援教育学科):** Combines educational theory with clinical psychology to support children with diverse learning needs and developmental challenges. *Note: High Japanese language proficiency (JLPT N1 level) is vital here due to the emphasis on psychological counseling and Japanese educational law.*

## 📍 Location and Campus Life (Fukuoka Advantage)

Located in the vibrant, youth-centric **Befu district of Jonan Ward, Fukuoka City**, the NGU campus is perfectly positioned. Fukuoka is widely celebrated as one of Japan's most comfortable, affordable, and safe cities for international students. 

### Why Fukuoka is Ideal for International Students:
*   **Cost of Living:** Rent and dining in Fukuoka are roughly 30% to 40% cheaper than in Tokyo or Osaka. A comfortable single apartment near the university ranges from 35,000 JPY to 50,000 JPY per month.
*   **Accessibility:** The Befu Subway Station is within walking distance of the campus, providing direct, fast access to Tenjin (the central commercial and fashion district) in under 15 minutes, and Hakata Station (the main Shinkansen bullet train hub) in 20 minutes.
*   **Global Gateway:** As Japan’s closest major city to the Asian mainland, Fukuoka has a long-standing history of international exchange. It boasts a welcoming, startup-friendly atmosphere, making it highly receptive to foreign graduates.

On-campus, the atmosphere is warm and communal. The university facilities include modern test kitchens, advanced research laboratories, a comprehensive library, and a beautiful student dining hall that serves nutritionally balanced meals developed in part by the university's own nutritional science students.

## 💼 Career Support and High Employment Rates

One of the most compelling reasons to choose Nakamura Gakuen University is its extraordinary track record in graduate employment. NGU consistently ranks at the very top of universities in the Kyushu region for employment rates, often exceeding **98%** annually.

### Dedicated Career Support Services:
*   **One-on-One Career Counseling:** The Career Support Center provides personalized coaching for international students, covering resume writing (RIREKISHO), industry research, and mock interviews tailored to Japanese business etiquette.
*   **On-Campus Job Fairs:** NGU invites dozens of domestic and regional companies directly to campus each year to recruit students. These include food manufacturers, retail corporations, financial institutions, and educational boards.
*   **Visa Transformation Assistance:** The university assists international graduates with the administrative paperwork required to change their student visas into professional work visas (*Status of Residence for Humanities/International Services* or specialized technical visas).

## 📝 Admission Guidelines for International Applicants

To apply as an international student, candidates must navigate a specialized admission pathway. Below is a breakdown of the standard requirements:

### Eligibility Criteria:
1.  **Educational Background:** Completion of 12 years of formal education outside of Japan.
2.  **Language Proficiency:** A solid command of the Japanese language is essential, as almost all classes are conducted in Japanese. 
    *   **Recommended level:** JLPT N2 or higher, or an equivalent score on the Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU) Japanese subject.

### Selection Process:
*   **Document Screening:** Review of high school transcripts, statement of purpose, and Japanese language test scores.
*   **Written Examination:** A basic academic assessment or essay writing (小論文, *shorunbun*) in Japanese.
*   **Interview:** An in-depth, face-to-face or online interview to assess your motivation, academic interests, Japanese communication skills, and financial stability.

*Tip: Preparing a strong, coherent essay explaining why you want to study your specific discipline under NGU’s philosophy will dramatically increase your chances of admission.*

*   **Tuition Reduction Scheme:** NGU offers tuition fee waivers or reductions (usually 30% to 50%) for privately financed international students who demonstrate financial need and academic excellence.
*   **JASSO Scholarships:** Eligible students can apply for the Honors Scholarship for Privately Financed International Students, which provides monthly stipends of around 48,000 JPY.
*   **Local Private Foundations:** Several Fukuoka-based corporate foundations offer localized scholarships exclusively for international students studying within the prefecture.

The university’s strong emphasis on practical education ensures that you will not just study theories in books, but will engage in field studies, clinical training, and local corporate collaborations. 

To succeed here, focus on building your **Japanese conversational and reading skills prior to arrival**.
