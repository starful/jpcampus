## üìö Courses, Curriculum & Teaching Style

The academic structure at Nichi-A Foreign Language Institute is meticulously designed around the student visa requirements and the progression timeline, offering three primary long-term courses tailored to varying intake periods and duration needs. These tracks are rigorously structured to move students from foundational Japanese to N2/N1 levels, crucial benchmarks for university admission.

**Core Progression Courses:**

1.  **2-Year Progression Course (April Intake):** The standard track for maximum linguistic development, allowing students to systematically absorb complex grammar and Kanji while receiving extensive preparation for the EJU and JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test). This course is ideal for those starting with little or no Japanese proficiency.
2.  **1.5-Year Progression Course (October Intake):** Designed for students who may have some prior basic Japanese knowledge or those requiring a shorter timeline for graduation. It features an accelerated pace compared to the first year of the 2-year course, concentrating heavily on intensive test preparation during the final six months.
3.  **1-Year Progression Course (April Intake):** Typically reserved for students who already possess an intermediate level of Japanese (equivalent to N3 or high N4) and require intensive, focused preparation primarily for the EJU and university entrance exams within a compressed timeframe.

**Curriculum Focus and Specialization:**

A defining characteristic of Nichi-A is its commitment to specialized academic tracks, recognizing that entrance into Japanese higher education requires subject-specific knowledge, not just language ability. The curriculum is stratified, moving from general Japanese instruction in the initial stages to specialized preparatory classes in the advanced stages:

*   **EJU Preparation:** Intensive training in all components of the EJU‚ÄîJapanese as a Foreign Language, Mathematics (Course 1 or 2), and General Studies (for humanities students) or Science (for science students). Mock testing and detailed feedback are core components.

**Teaching Methodology:** The teaching approach emphasizes active participation, blending traditional classroom instruction with practical application.
