## üè´ University Overview & Reputation

Gakushuin University, a prestigious private institution located in Tokyo's serene Mejiro district, holds a unique place in Japan's educational landscape. Founded in 1877, initially as a school for the Imperial Family and the aristocracy, Gakushuin has evolved into a modern university while preserving its traditions of academic excellence and holistic education. The university's history is intertwined with the modernization of Japan, and it continues to uphold its founding principles of fostering intellectual curiosity, personal growth, and social responsibility among its students. Gakushuin's small class sizes, dedicated faculty, and emphasis on individualized attention contribute to its distinctive learning environment.

Gakushuin is widely regarded as a top-tier private university in Japan, often considered within the upper echelon of institutions known for their humanities, social sciences, and sciences programs. While it may not consistently top global university rankings, its strong reputation within Japan is undeniable. The university is particularly respected for its rigorous academic standards, close-knit community, and the success of its graduates in various fields. The university's connections to Japan's Imperial Family further enhance its prestige and legacy within the country. Gakushuin's alumni network spans prominent figures in government, business, academia, and the arts, reflecting the university's influence and impact on Japanese society.

The university's commitment to cultivating well-rounded individuals extends beyond academics. Gakushuin encourages student involvement in extracurricular activities, cultural events, and community service, fostering a sense of belonging and social awareness. The campus itself is a beautiful blend of historical and modern architecture, providing a peaceful and inspiring setting for learning and discovery. Gakushuin University's enduring legacy, combined with its commitment to innovation and student success, makes it a distinguished choice for both domestic and international students seeking a quality education in Japan.

One of Gakushuin University's signature programs is within the **Faculty of International Social Sciences**. This faculty stands out for its interdisciplinary approach to understanding global issues and its focus on cultivating globally-minded citizens. While not a fully English-taught degree program, it offers a number of courses in English, allowing international students with varying levels of Japanese proficiency to engage with the curriculum. The program emphasizes critical thinking, cross-cultural communication, and problem-solving skills, preparing students for careers in international organizations, multinational corporations, and government agencies. The faculty also actively encourages students to participate in study abroad programs and internships, providing them with valuable international experience. The courses are designed to foster deep understanding of global issues, and it has a high number of international students.

Another notable area is the **Faculty of Letters, particularly the Department of Japanese Language and Literature.** While primarily conducted in Japanese, this department attracts international students interested in pursuing advanced studies in Japanese language, literature, and culture. The faculty comprises renowned scholars in their respective fields, offering students access to cutting-edge research and a rigorous academic curriculum. The faculty offers specialized courses on classical and modern Japanese literature, linguistics, and cultural studies, fostering intellectual curiosity and scholarly excellence.

Finally, the **Faculty of Law** is highly respected within Japan. Its rigorous curriculum and emphasis on legal theory and practice prepare students for successful careers in the legal profession, government, and business. The department offers specialized courses in various areas of law, including constitutional law, criminal law, civil law, and international law. Students have opportunities to participate in moot court competitions, legal clinics, and internships, gaining practical experience and developing their legal skills.

## üåç International Student Admissions & Life

Gakushuin University welcomes international students from diverse backgrounds and offers a range of support services to ensure a successful academic journey. The admission process typically involves several key steps, including submitting an application form, providing academic transcripts, and demonstrating Japanese language proficiency. The **Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU)** is generally required for undergraduate programs. Specific score requirements vary depending on the faculty and department. In addition to the EJU, applicants may also be required to submit scores from the **TOEFL or IELTS** to demonstrate English language proficiency, even if they are applying to a program primarily taught in Japanese. Some departments may also conduct interviews as part of the selection process.

While Gakushuin does not offer a wide array of fully English-taught degree programs at the undergraduate level, some courses within certain faculties, particularly the Faculty of International Social Sciences, are offered in English. At the graduate level, there may be more opportunities for English-taught courses and research opportunities, depending on the department and the availability of English-speaking faculty members. It's crucial for prospective international students to carefully review the course offerings and research opportunities within their desired faculty to determine the extent of English language support available.

The **International Center** at Gakushuin University serves as a central hub for international student support. The center provides assistance with visa applications, housing arrangements, cultural orientation programs, and academic advising. Staff members are available to answer questions, provide guidance, and help international students navigate the complexities of life in Japan. The International Center also organizes social events and cultural activities to promote cross-cultural understanding and foster a sense of community among international students. Academic advising is available to help students choose courses, plan their academic progress, and explore career options. Counseling services are also available to provide emotional support and address any personal challenges that international students may face. The university assists with visa renewals and other immigration-related matters.

Gakushuin University has approximately 250 international students, creating a diverse and multicultural campus environment. There are various student clubs and organizations catering to a wide range of interests, from sports and music to cultural exchange and academic pursuits. International students are encouraged to participate in these activities to connect with local students, develop new skills, and explore their interests. The overall campus atmosphere is known for being friendly and welcoming, with a strong emphasis on community and mutual respect. While the university is rooted in tradition, it is also committed to embracing diversity and creating an inclusive environment for all students.

Gakushuin University offers several scholarship opportunities for international students to help alleviate the financial burden of studying abroad. The most prestigious scholarship is the **Japanese Government (MEXT) Scholarship**. This scholarship is highly competitive and covers tuition fees, living expenses, and round-trip airfare. The application process typically involves applying through the Japanese embassy or consulate in the applicant's home country. Gakushuin University also offers its own **Gakushuin University Scholarship for International Students**. The eligibility criteria and application procedures vary depending on the scholarship, but typically, academic merit, financial need, and language proficiency are considered. Several private foundations also offer scholarships for international students studying in Japan. These foundations often have specific eligibility requirements, such as nationality, field of study, or academic background. Examples include the Rotary Yoneyama Memorial Foundation and the Heiwa Nakajima Foundation. Students must research and apply directly to these foundations. Information on all available scholarships can be found on the university's website and through the International Center.

## üìç Campus Location & Access

Gakushuin University's main campus is situated in the upscale and tranquil Mejiro district of Tokyo, known for its leafy residential streets, elegant homes, and peaceful atmosphere. The area offers a welcome respite from the hustle and bustle of central Tokyo, providing a conducive environment for studying and personal reflection. The campus itself is characterized by a blend of historical buildings and modern facilities, surrounded by lush greenery and well-maintained gardens. The neighborhood surrounding the campus is safe and convenient, with a variety of shops, restaurants, cafes, and cultural attractions within easy reach. The Gakushuin campus is also close to other educational institutions and cultural landmarks, making it an ideal location for students seeking a well-rounded academic and cultural experience.

Accessing the main campus from major transport hubs is straightforward and convenient thanks to Tokyo's efficient public transportation system. From **Tokyo Station**, take the JR Yamanote Line directly to Mejiro Station (approximately 25 minutes). From **Shinjuku Station**, take the JR Yamanote Line to Mejiro Station (approximately 15 minutes). From **Ikebukuro Station**, take the JR Yamanote Line to Mejiro Station (approximately 5 minutes). From **Narita Airport (NRT)**, take the Narita Express to Ikebukuro Station and then transfer to the JR Yamanote Line to Mejiro Station (approximately 1 hour 45 minutes). Alternatively, take the Airport Limousine Bus to a major hotel in Ikebukuro and then transfer to the JR Yamanote Line. From **Haneda Airport (HND)**, take the Keikyu Line to Shinagawa Station and then transfer to the JR Yamanote Line to Mejiro Station (approximately 50 minutes). The close proximity of Mejiro Station to the campus entrance makes Gakushuin University easily accessible to students and visitors alike.
