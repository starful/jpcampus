## üè´ University Overview

Gakushuin University, steeped in tradition and prestige, boasts a rich history dating back to 1877. Initially established as a school for the Japanese aristocracy, Gakushuin has evolved into a highly respected private university committed to academic excellence and the cultivation of well-rounded individuals. The university's founding principle, 'education for life,' emphasizes not only intellectual development but also the importance of character building and social responsibility. Located in the serene and upscale Mejiro district of Tokyo, Gakushuin offers a tranquil learning environment, fostering close interactions between students and faculty. It is particularly known for its intimate class sizes, personalized advising, and a strong sense of community. Gakushuin's alumni network includes prominent figures in Japanese politics, business, and culture, further solidifying its reputation as a leading institution of higher learning.

The university is organized into five undergraduate faculties and several graduate schools, offering a diverse range of academic programs in the humanities, social sciences, and natural sciences. Gakushuin is dedicated to providing students with a comprehensive education that prepares them for success in a rapidly changing global landscape. They pride themselves on their commitment to nurturing critical thinking skills, fostering creativity, and promoting ethical leadership.

## üåç International Student Support

Gakushuin University provides comprehensive support services for international students to ensure a smooth transition and a rewarding academic experience. The International Center offers assistance with visa applications, housing arrangements, and cultural orientation programs. While Gakushuin isn't widely known for a vast array of fully English-taught degree programs, there are some courses offered in English, and support is provided to help international students navigate the Japanese-language environment. They also offer Japanese language courses to improve communication skills.

- **Dormitory:** Limited dormitory spaces are available for international students. Information about on-campus and off-campus housing options is provided through the International Center.
- **English Programs:** While the majority of programs are taught in Japanese, some courses and research opportunities are available in English. The Faculty of International Social Sciences, in particular, offers programs designed with international perspectives in mind.
- **Career Support:** The Career Center provides career counseling, resume workshops, and internship opportunities to help international students prepare for their future careers, both in Japan and abroad. They also offer guidance on visa requirements for working in Japan after graduation.
- **Scholarships:** A range of scholarships are available for international students, including scholarships offered by the Japanese government (MEXT), private foundations, and Gakushuin University itself. Information about eligibility criteria and application procedures can be obtained from the International Center.
- **Buddy System:** The university offers a buddy system where local students are paired with international students to help them adjust to life in Japan and provide support with daily tasks.
