## üìö Courses, Curriculum & Teaching Style

Kowa Japanese Language Institute exclusively offers long-term *shingaku* (Higher Education Preparation) courses, strictly adhering to a curriculum designed to take students from foundational Japanese skills to the advanced proficiency required for university entrance. The core courses are structured around standard entry dates: the two-year course (April start), the one-year-nine-month course (July start), and the one-year-six-month course (October start). These specific durations are strategically calibrated to ensure that students complete their necessary 750+ hours of study and reach the required academic level just prior to the crucial university application and examination cycles, which typically peak between the summer and winter of the final year.

The curriculum is rigorously phased, moving through beginner, intermediate, and advanced levels, with a mandatory pivot toward entrance examination training starting at the late-intermediate stage. The teaching style is intense, utilizing a mix of direct instruction, communicative practice, and high-stakes testing simulations. Teachers prioritize clear explanations of complex grammar structures necessary for academic writing and the extensive vocabulary required for the EJU and specialized subject tests. Crucially, the curriculum moves beyond standard JLPT preparation (though N1 achievement is a major goal) to incorporate the specific demands of the EJU.

The EJU-dedicated training is a central pillar of Kowa‚Äôs offering. Students are not only trained in the Japanese as a Foreign Language component of the EJU but are also provided specialized instruction in the optional EJU subject areas, depending on their desired university major‚Äîtypically Mathematics (Course 1 or 2) and General Knowledge (Social Studies/Science). This integration of academic subjects *in Japanese* is paramount for success in the entrance exams and distinguishes a dedicated preparatory school like Kowa. Classes focus on advanced reading comprehension, critical analysis of academic texts, formal essay writing (a requirement for many private and national universities), and presentation skills.

Throughout the advanced phases, the institute places significant emphasis on mock examinations, both for the JLPT and the EJU. These simulations are used to constantly track student progress, identify weak areas, and fine-tune individual study plans. Furthermore, the teaching methodology includes extensive preparation for the often challenging aspects of the application process, such as crafting compelling *shib≈ç riy≈´sho* (statements of purpose) and practicing the formal, respectful language necessary for university entrance interviews (Keigo training). Given the small class sizes, instructors can provide highly detailed feedback on these critical subjective elements of the application, maximizing the student‚Äôs chances of acceptance into competitive programs.

## üåç Student Body & Campus Life

The student body at Kowa Japanese Language Institute is characterized by a high degree of academic seriousness and a shared goal of university enrollment. The area provides access to necessary amenities, libraries, and parks, ideal for focused study sessions outside of the classroom. The school actively encourages students to utilize local facilities and integrate into the neighborhood, helping to ease the transition to life in Japan.

While the primary focus remains academic preparation, the school organizes essential cultural activities designed to deepen the students' appreciation for Japanese culture and provide practical context for their language learning. These often include traditional Japanese craft workshops, historical site visits in Tokyo or nearby regions, and participation in local festivals in Ota Ward. These structured excursions serve the dual purpose of stress relief and practical communication practice. Due to the institute's smaller capacity (180), a strong sense of community and personal belonging is fostered. Students and faculty interact closely, allowing the school to quickly identify and address any personal or academic challenges students may face, enhancing overall well-being and academic performance.
