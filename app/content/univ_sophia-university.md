## üè´ University Overview

Sophia University (‰∏äÊô∫Â§ßÂ≠¶, J≈çchi Daigaku) is a private research university in Tokyo, Japan. Founded in 1913 by the Society of Jesus, it is one of Japan's most prestigious and internationally oriented universities. The university's name, 'Sophia,' is derived from the Greek word for wisdom. Sophia is highly regarded for its liberal arts education, strong language programs, and global outlook.

From its inception, Sophia University has placed emphasis on fostering global citizens. The university boasts a diverse student body, including a substantial number of international students from various countries. It is also renowned for its strong academic programs, dedicated faculty, and comprehensive support services for students. Sophia University is also part of the SGU (Super Global University) project, aiming to improve international competitiveness.

Sophia University has consistently ranked among the top universities in Japan and is particularly well-known for its programs in international relations, languages, and business. Its graduates are highly sought after by both domestic and international employers.

## üåç International Student Support

Sophia University provides extensive support for international students, including:

- **Dormitory:** On-campus and off-campus housing options are available. International students are given priority for on-campus dormitories.
- **English Programs:** A wide range of courses are offered in English, allowing international students to pursue their studies without needing advanced Japanese language skills.
- **Japanese Language Courses:** Comprehensive Japanese language courses are available to help international students improve their language proficiency.
- **Career Support:** The university's career center provides guidance and resources to help international students find internships and jobs in Japan and abroad.
- **Visa Support:** The international student office assists with visa applications and renewals.
- **Cultural Exchange Programs:** Opportunities to participate in cultural events and activities to connect with Japanese students and learn about Japanese culture.
- **Scholarships:** Various scholarships are available for international students, based on academic merit and financial need. The university also publishes information on scholarships provided by external organizations.
