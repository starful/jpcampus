Sophia University is known for its strong emphasis on internationalism and its commitment to fostering a global perspective in its students. The university has a significant international student population and offers a wide range of courses taught in English, making it a popular choice for international students seeking higher education in Japan. The university was initially founded with three departments: Philosophy, German Literature, and Commerce. Over the years, Sophia University has expanded its academic offerings to encompass a wide range of disciplines, including humanities, social sciences, law, economics, science, and technology.

Sophia University emphasizes holistic education, aiming to develop students' intellectual, social, and personal growth. The university offers a variety of extracurricular activities, including sports clubs, cultural organizations, and volunteer opportunities, providing students with opportunities to engage in campus life and develop their leadership skills. The university also has a strong alumni network, which provides support and guidance to graduates as they embark on their careers.
