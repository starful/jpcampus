## üè´ University Overview

Shizuoka University, while perhaps not as internationally renowned as some top-tier universities in Tokyo or Kyoto, occupies a significant position within Japan's national university system. Established in 1949 through the amalgamation of several older institutions, including Shizuoka Normal School, Shizuoka Youth Normal School, Shizuoka Higher School, Hamamatsu College of Technology, and Shizuoka Prefectural College of Agriculture, it boasts a rich history deeply interwoven with the development of education and technology in Shizuoka Prefecture. The university aspires to be a center of academic excellence rooted in its local community, contributing to both regional and global advancement.

Shizuoka University is a national university, meaning it is funded and operated by the Japanese government. This distinguishes it from private universities, which often have higher tuition fees. National universities are generally considered to offer a high standard of education at a more affordable cost. Shizuoka University is known for its strong programs in engineering, informatics, and agriculture, reflecting the key industries and characteristics of the Shizuoka region. It is consistently ranked among the top 50 national universities in Japan, and its research output, particularly in areas like optics and tea science, is well-regarded.

Beyond academics, Shizuoka University emphasizes fostering a diverse and inclusive learning environment. While the international student population may be smaller compared to some of the larger urban universities, it actively promotes international exchange programs and provides support services for international students. The university encourages students to engage in extracurricular activities and participate in community events, aiming to cultivate well-rounded individuals who can contribute positively to society. Shizuoka University aims to equip students with the knowledge, skills, and global perspective necessary for success in their chosen fields.

The EJU assesses Japanese language proficiency, as well as basic academic skills in subjects like mathematics and science. For graduate programs, the requirements may vary, but a bachelor's degree (or equivalent) is typically required. In addition to the EJU (for undergraduates) or bachelor's degree (for graduates), international students typically need to submit transcripts from their previous educational institutions, a personal statement outlining their academic goals and motivations, and letters of recommendation from professors or other academic professionals. English proficiency tests, such as TOEFL or IELTS, are often required, especially for programs where English is used as the medium of instruction. Some faculties may also conduct interviews as part of the selection process. It is crucial to carefully review the application guidelines and deadlines for each program, as they can vary.

Applicants typically need to apply through the Japanese embassy or consulate in their home country.
