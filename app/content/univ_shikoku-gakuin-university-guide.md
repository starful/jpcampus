## 🏫 University Overview

Shikoku Gakuin University (SGU), located in the historic city of Zentsuji in Kagawa Prefecture, is a private Christian liberal arts institution that stands as a beacon of progressive education in the Shikoku region. Founded in 1949 with the support of the Presbyterian Church in the United States, the university has long been committed to the principles of "Love and Service." For international students, SGU offers a unique blend of traditional Japanese culture—being located near the birthplace of the famous monk Kobo Daishi—and a modern, Western-influenced educational philosophy that emphasizes critical thinking, individualism, and global awareness.

Unlike many large, impersonal universities in Tokyo or Osaka, Shikoku Gakuin University prides itself on its tight-knit community. With a relatively small student body, the professor-to-student ratio is exceptionally favorable, ensuring that international students receive personalized attention not only in their academic pursuits but also in their adjustment to life in Japan. The university’s mission is to cultivate 'Global Citizens' who possess the empathy and skills to solve complex social issues, making it an ideal choice for students interested in social work, psychology, and the arts.

## 🎓 The Major-Minor System: A Unique Academic Flexibility

One of the most distinctive features of Shikoku Gakuin University is its "Major-Minor" system within the Faculty of Liberal Arts. In many Japanese universities, students are locked into a rigid curriculum from the moment they enter. At SGU, students have the freedom to design their own educational path. 

### How it Works:
Students choose a primary 'Major' to gain deep expertise and a 'Minor' to broaden their horizons. This allows for interdisciplinary combinations that are rare in Japan. For example:
- **International Liberal Arts (Major) + Performing Arts (Minor):** Ideal for students looking to work in international cultural exchange or global entertainment management.
- **Social Welfare (Major) + Psychology (Minor):** A powerful combination for those aiming to become specialized counselors or social workers in an aging society.
- **Japanese Language & Culture (Major) + Education (Minor):** Perfect for international students who wish to become educators or cultural liaisons.

This flexibility is particularly beneficial for international students who may still be refining their career goals while improving their Japanese language proficiency. It encourages a well-rounded education that prepares graduates for a volatile job market.

## 🎭 The Renowned Performing Arts Program (Notology)

Shikoku Gakuin University is nationally recognized in Japan for its pioneering approach to the performing arts. The university established the first "Theater and Dance" major within a liberal arts context in Japan. Led by prominent figures in the Japanese theater world, the program focuses on "Notology"—a term coined to describe the study of performance as a means of human communication and social intervention.

For international students interested in the arts, the campus offers world-class facilities, including the 'Notology' theater lab and various rehearsal spaces. The program doesn't just train actors; it produces playwrights, lighting technicians, stage managers, and arts administrators. The university frequently hosts professional theater troupes, providing students with direct networking opportunities and exposure to the professional industry. Even if you do not major in the arts, the presence of this creative energy permeates the campus, making SGU a vibrant place for self-expression.

## 🤝 Social Welfare and Community Contribution

Historically, SGU has been a leader in Social Welfare education in Shikoku. The Department of Social Welfare is one of the oldest and most respected in the region. Given Japan's demographic shift towards an elderly population, the expertise provided here is in high demand. 

International students in this track learn about the Japanese welfare system, gerontology, and disability studies. The curriculum is deeply rooted in the Christian ethic of helping the marginalized. Students often engage in fieldwork and volunteer activities within Zentsuji and the surrounding Kagawa area, providing them with real-world experience and a chance to integrate into the local community. This hands-on approach is invaluable for those looking to build a career in NGOs, healthcare management, or public service.

## 🌏 Support Systems for International Students

Transitioning to a rural city in Japan can be daunting, but Shikoku Gakuin University provides a robust support structure to ensure international students succeed.

### 1. Language Support
The university offers intensive Japanese language courses tailored to different proficiency levels. Whether you are aiming to pass the JLPT N1 or just need to improve your conversational skills for daily life, the International Center provides the necessary resources. 

### 2. Tutoring and Peer Support
The 'Buddy System' pairs international students with Japanese students. These Japanese peers help with everything from opening a bank account and registering at the city hall to practicing Japanese over lunch. This system often leads to lifelong friendships and deep cultural immersion.

### 3. Financial Aid and Scholarships
SGU is highly accessible due to its tuition reduction schemes for international students. Many students qualify for a 30% to 50% reduction in tuition based on academic performance and financial need. Additionally, the university assists students in applying for external scholarships like the JASSO honors scholarship.

### 4. Housing
The university offers dormitory options and assists students in finding affordable local apartments. Living costs in Zentsuji are significantly lower than in Tokyo, allowing students to maintain a high quality of life on a modest budget.

## 📍 Location and Campus Life: The Zentsuji Experience

Zentsuji City is a spiritual and historic hub. It is home to Zentsuji Temple, the 75th temple of the 88-temple Shikoku Pilgrimage. The atmosphere is peaceful, safe, and rich with tradition. For an international student, this provides an 'authentic' Japanese experience that is often lost in the neon lights of the mega-cities.

### Campus Atmosphere
The campus itself is a mix of modern facilities and historic architecture, including the iconic White Chapel. The greenery and open spaces provide a contemplative environment perfect for study. Throughout the year, the university holds various events, including the 'Gakuin-sai' (University Festival), where international students often share their home country’s food and culture with the local residents.

### Exploring Kagawa
Kagawa is famously known as the 'Udon Prefecture.' Students can enjoy world-class noodles for just a few hundred yen. Beyond food, the prefecture is a gateway to the Seto Inland Sea, famous for its 'Art Islands' like Naoshima and Teshima. Living in Kagawa means being part of a world-renowned contemporary art scene while residing in a traditional rural setting.

## 📈 Career Prospects and Path After Graduation

Graduating from Shikoku Gakuin University opens several doors:
- **Employment in Japan:** SGU has a dedicated Career Support Center that helps international students navigate the unique 'Shukatsu' (job hunting) process in Japan. Graduates often find roles in the hospitality industry, international trade, social services, and education.
- **Graduate Studies:** Many students leverage the strong liberal arts foundation to pursue Master’s degrees at prestigious national universities in Japan or overseas.
- **The 'Shikoku Advantage':** There is a growing demand for multilingual and culturally flexible staff in the Shikoku and Setouchi regions as they become more popular for international tourism and business. SGU graduates are well-positioned to fill these roles.

## 📝 Admission Guide for International Applicants

Applying to Shikoku Gakuin University is a straightforward process compared to many large institutions, but it requires careful preparation.

### Requirements:
1. **Educational Background:** Completion of 12 years of formal education.
2. **Language Proficiency:** Generally, a level of JLPT N2 or equivalent is recommended to follow lectures, though the university may consider students with N3 if they show high potential and a willingness to attend intensive language courses.
3. **Examination:** The selection process usually involves a review of application documents, a written essay (in Japanese), and an interview (often available online for overseas applicants).

### Application Timeline:
- **Autumn Admission (October):** Applications typically open in late spring.
- **Spring Admission (April):** Applications typically open in late autumn/early winter.

*Consultant's Tip:* Highlight your specific interest in why a 'Liberal Arts' education in a 'Rural' setting appeals to you. The admissions committee values students who want to be active members of their local community.

If you are an international student who prefers a personalized education, has a passion for social welfare or the arts, and wants to experience the 'real' Japan away from the tourist crowds, SGU is an excellent choice. Here, you will not be just a number; you will be a valued member of a community dedicated to making the world a more compassionate and creative place.
