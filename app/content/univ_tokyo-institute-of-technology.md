One of Tokyo Tech's most renowned programs is its **Electrical and Electronic Engineering (EEE) program**, which resides within the School of Engineering. This program is highly regarded for its comprehensive curriculum, world-class faculty, and state-of-the-art research facilities. The EEE program covers a broad range of topics, including microelectronics, power systems, communication networks, and signal processing. Students have the opportunity to specialize in areas of particular interest, such as robotics, artificial intelligence, or renewable energy. The program also fosters close collaborations with leading companies in the electronics and technology industries, providing students with valuable internship and job opportunities. A significant number of courses are offered in English at the graduate level, making it accessible to international students with strong technical backgrounds. The EEE program consistently attracts top students from around the world and produces graduates who are highly sought after by employers in both academia and industry.

Another exceptionally popular program, particularly among international students, is the **Information Science and Engineering program** within the School of Computing. This program focuses on the theoretical foundations and practical applications of computer science and information technology. Students learn about algorithms, data structures, software engineering, computer architecture, and artificial intelligence. The program emphasizes hands-on learning through laboratory exercises, programming projects, and research collaborations. A substantial portion of graduate-level courses are taught in English, making it an attractive option for international students who may not yet be fluent in Japanese. The Information Science and Engineering program at Tokyo Tech is known for its rigorous curriculum, innovative research, and strong industry connections. Graduates of this program are well-prepared for careers in software development, data science, cybersecurity, and other rapidly growing fields.

## üåç International Student Admissions & Life

The application process for international students at Tokyo Tech generally involves several key steps. First, applicants typically need to submit their academic transcripts, standardized test scores (such as TOEFL or IELTS for English proficiency), and letters of recommendation. The **EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students)** is often required for undergraduate applicants, assessing their Japanese language proficiency and basic academic skills. Graduate applicants may need to submit GRE scores, depending on the specific program. A statement of purpose outlining the applicant's research interests and career goals is also a crucial part of the application. Following the initial screening, selected candidates may be invited for an interview, which can be conducted in person or via video conference. The interview assesses the applicant's academic background, research aptitude, and motivation for studying at Tokyo Tech.

Tokyo Tech offers a variety of degree programs entirely in English, primarily at the graduate level (Master's and Doctoral degrees). These programs cater to international students who wish to pursue advanced studies without requiring proficiency in Japanese. Departments such as Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Computer Science, Materials Science, and Chemical Engineering offer English-taught courses and research opportunities. The university website provides a comprehensive list of programs available in English.

The International Student Office at Tokyo Tech plays a vital role in supporting international students throughout their academic journey. The office provides assistance with visa applications and renewals, accommodation arrangements, and general orientation to life in Japan. Academic advising is available to help students choose courses, plan their academic schedules, and navigate the university system. Counseling services are also provided to address personal or academic challenges that international students may face. The International Student Office organizes various events and activities to promote intercultural exchange and integration, fostering a welcoming and supportive environment for international students.

Approximately 1300 international students from diverse backgrounds are enrolled at Tokyo Tech, creating a vibrant and multicultural campus community. The university boasts a wide range of student clubs and organizations catering to various interests, including sports, music, arts, and cultural activities. International students are encouraged to participate in these clubs to connect with local and other international students, develop new skills, and experience Japanese culture firsthand. The overall campus atmosphere is known for its intellectual rigor, collaborative spirit, and openness to diversity.

The cost of studying at Tokyo Tech for international students includes tuition fees, living expenses, and other associated costs. The admission fee is a one-time payment required upon enrollment, while the yearly tuition covers the cost of instruction and access to university facilities. Other fees may include student association fees, insurance, and miscellaneous expenses.

Several scholarships are available to international students at Tokyo Tech. The **Japanese Government (MEXT) Scholarship** is one of the most prestigious and comprehensive scholarships, covering tuition fees, living expenses, and travel costs. Tokyo Tech also offers its own scholarships specifically for international students, based on academic merit and financial need. These scholarships may provide full or partial tuition waivers and/or monthly stipends. Private foundations and organizations also offer scholarships to international students studying in Japan. It is advisable to check the latest scholarship information on the university‚Äôs official website and to apply well in advance of the application deadlines.

The cost of living in Tokyo can be relatively high, but it varies depending on lifestyle and accommodation choices. Rent for a single room apartment near the university can range from 70,000 to 120,000 JPY per month. Food expenses can be estimated at around 30,000 to 50,000 JPY per month, depending on dining habits. Transportation costs depend on the frequency of travel but generally average around 10,000 to 15,000 JPY per month. Other expenses, such as utilities, internet, and personal items, can add up to around 20,000 to 30,000 JPY per month. Therefore, a rough monthly budget estimate for an international student in Tokyo is around **130,000 to 215,000 JPY**.
