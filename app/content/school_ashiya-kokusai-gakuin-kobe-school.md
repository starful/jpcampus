## School Overview & Philosophy

Ashiya Kokusai Gakuin Kobe School (芦屋国際学院神戸校) distinguishes itself within Japanese language education through its highly focused mission and exceptionally small size. Situated in Ashiya, a peaceful and affluent residential area between Osaka and Kobe in Hyogo Prefecture, the school offers a unique combination of academic rigor and a conducive living environment. Unlike larger institutions that accommodate hundreds or thousands of students, Ashiya Kokusai Gakuin maintains a maximum capacity of 60 students. This deliberate limitation is central to its educational philosophy: providing personalized instruction and comprehensive guidance for international students aiming to enter Japan's top universities, both public and private. The school sees itself as a specialized preparatory academy where each student's academic profile and progression are carefully managed.

The strategic location provides several advantages. Ashiya is known for its high quality of life, safety, and refined atmosphere, making it ideal for focused study while remaining accessible to major urban centers. Students benefit from the Kansai region's cultural richness and economic opportunities, home to prestigious national universities like Kobe University, Osaka University, and Kyoto University. The school's small size creates a close-knit community where staff and teachers know each student's strengths, weaknesses, and circumstances. This ensures responsive pastoral care, academic counseling, and mental well-being support, minimizing anxieties associated with studying abroad.

The core mission is securing successful progression to higher education. The curriculum is designed to meet the requirements of the Examination for Japanese University Admission (EJU). This specialization means the school doesn't offer casual or short-term cultural courses; its entire infrastructure is geared toward mastering language proficiency (JLPT N1 level) and academic subjects (Mathematics, Science, Japan and the World) needed for entrance exams. The school's commitment to providing accommodation, with its dormitory facilities, emphasizes its philosophy of creating a supportive environment where students can focus entirely on academic objectives without housing stress. This holistic approach is fundamental to its success.

Furthermore, selection criteria for students and faculty emphasize dedication and focused preparation. Teachers are specialists with experience in university entrance preparation or EJU test strategies. The institution uses its scale to refine teaching methods based on the latest EJU trends and university admission requirements, ensuring relevant and impactful academic content. The ultimate goal is to bridge the gap between intermediate Japanese and the academic-level Japanese required for lectures, research, and examinations, ensuring graduates are not only accepted into good universities but also prepared to thrive academically.

## Courses, Curriculum & Teaching Style

The academic program at Ashiya Kokusai Gakuin Kobe School is exclusively dedicated to university and graduate school progression, offering two primary tracks tailored to entry timing and duration needs:

*   **2-Year Higher Education Course (進学2年コース)**: Starting in April, it provides comprehensive preparation, allowing students to progress from basic/intermediate levels to advanced fluency and academic knowledge for the EJU and university interviews.
*   **1 Year 6 Months Higher Education Course (進学1年6か月コース)**: Starting in October, it is designed for students with a strong Japanese foundation (often JLPT N3 or N2 upon entry) requiring accelerated preparation focused on EJU subject refinement and advanced Japanese argumentation.

The curriculum is divided into two critical components: Japanese Language Mastery and Academic Subject Preparation.

*   **Japanese Component**: Focuses on advanced reading comprehension, academic essay writing, detailed listening comprehension relevant to university lectures, and complex grammatical structures for formal academic discourse.
*   **Teaching Style**: Smaller class sizes enable teachers to use interactive and output-focused methodologies. Students engage in debates, presentations, and mock interviews, receiving immediate, detailed feedback. This personalized attention ensures linguistic weaknesses are addressed through targeted assignments.

Crucially, the school integrates specialized EJU subject courses into the timetable.

*   These mandatory supplementary classes cover Mathematics (Course 1 or 2), Science (Physics, Chemistry, Biology), and Japan and the World. Specialized instruction is vital for students aiming for science or engineering majors.
*   The curriculum includes training in report writing, note-taking techniques, and critical analysis of complex texts, preparing students for both the entrance exam and undergraduate studies.

Teaching effectiveness is assessed through regular, standardized mock EJU examinations.

*   These simulations familiarize students with the pressure and pacing of the actual examination. Comprehensive review sessions are held to analyze performance and adjust study plans.
*   Teachers work with students to formulate individualized target scores for the EJU and JLPT, establishing progress benchmarks. The commitment to focused preparation prioritizes immediate application and iterative feedback, ensuring students master the tools required for academic success in Japan.

## University & Career Pathways

The primary objective of Ashiya Kokusai Gakuin Kobe School is to ensure successful matriculation into the student's chosen university. The school's reputation is tied to its progression rates and the caliber of institutions its graduates enter. Consultation for higher education begins upon enrollment and is highly individualized.

*   Each student works with an academic advisor to set realistic progression goals. This counseling involves initial assessment, selection of target universities, strategic planning for EJU preparation, and detailed support through the application cycle.
*   Guidance focuses on prestigious regional institutions such as Kobe University, Osaka University, Kyoto University, Doshisha University, Ritsumeikan University, and Kansai University.

Beyond high EJU scores, the school provides training for the application documentation and interview process.

*   This includes workshops on writing application essays, translating transcripts, and mastering the etiquette required for university interviews. Mock interviews are conducted by faculty members, simulating actual entrance interviews.
*   The individualized approach also manages entrance exam schedules. The administrative team tracks each student’s application timeline, reducing the risk of errors. For students pursuing vocational training or those aiming to proceed directly to the workforce, personalized guidance is provided to meet their chosen pathway's requirements.

Ultimately, the school acts as a dedicated progression gateway, utilizing its small size and specialized focus to maximize each student's potential for academic success.

### Payment Schedule and Financial Planning

Payments for Japanese language schools are structured in lump sums, often covering the first six months or the entire first year upfront, especially for visa applications. Prospective students should prepare to pay the enrollment fee and the majority of the first-year academic fees (approximately ¥820,000 for the 2-year course) before receiving their Certificate of Eligibility (CoE).
