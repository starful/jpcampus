## 🏫 University Overview

Tenri University (天理大学, Tenri Daigaku) is a private institution located in the heart of Nara Prefecture, a region steeped in the very origins of Japanese history and culture. Established in 1925 as the Tenri Foreign Language School, it holds the distinction of being the first private foreign language vocational school in Japan. While the university is affiliated with the Tenrikyo religion, it operates as a comprehensive academic institution that welcomes students from all faiths and backgrounds. The university is built upon the philosophy of the 'Joyous Life' (Yokigurashi), emphasizing mutual help, selfless service (Hinokishin), and the development of global citizens who can contribute to the welfare of humanity.

For international students, Tenri University offers a unique blend of high-level academic rigor, world-class athletic programs, and an intimate, community-focused environment that is often missing in larger metropolitan universities like those in Tokyo or Osaka. The university is spread across several campuses, primarily the Somanouchi Campus and the Taiyuji Campus, both of which feature the distinct 'Oyasato-yakata' architectural style—a grand, traditional Japanese aesthetic that gives the entire city of Tenri a unique, cohesive atmosphere.

## 🎓 Academic Faculties and Departments

### 1. Faculty of Humanities
This faculty is the philosophical heart of the university. It focuses on the deep study of human nature, history, and the soul. For international students interested in Japanese culture, the Department of History and Culture provides an unparalleled look into the ancient Yamato region (Nara). The Department of Religious Studies is one of the most respected in Japan, offering insights not only into Tenrikyo but also into global religions and their roles in modern society. This faculty encourages students to think critically about their place in the world and how to foster peace through cultural understanding.

### 2. Faculty of International Studies
Carrying on the legacy of the original foreign language school, this faculty is a hub for global exchange. It offers specialized tracks in English, Chinese, Korean, and Spanish. Most importantly for foreign applicants, the Japanese Language and Culture department is specifically designed to bridge the gap between international students and the Japanese professional world. The curriculum isn't just about grammar; it focuses on 'Intercultural Communication,' teaching students how to navigate the nuances of Japanese society while maintaining their own cultural identity.

### 3. Faculty of Physical Education
Tenri University is a household name in the world of sports, particularly Judo. This faculty has produced numerous Olympic gold medalists and world champions. However, it is not just for elite athletes. The curriculum covers sports science, coaching, and health management. International students who are serious about martial arts or sports management will find facilities and coaching staff that are among the best in the world. The spirit of 'Budo' (martial arts) is integrated with modern science to create a holistic approach to physical education.

### 4. Faculty of Health Care
Located at the Taiyuji Campus, this faculty focuses on Nursing and Clinical Psychology. While these programs require a very high level of Japanese proficiency (N1+), they are excellent for students looking to integrate into Japan’s healthcare system. The faculty emphasizes 'compassionate care,' aligning with the university’s overall mission of service to others.

## 🥋 The Judo Legacy: A Global Mecca

One cannot speak of Tenri University without mentioning its Judo program. The university has a legendary status, having produced icons like Anton Geesink’s rivals and modern legends like Tadahiro Nomura (the only person to win three consecutive Olympic gold medals in Judo) and Shohei Ono. For international students who practice Judo, coming to Tenri is akin to a pilgrimage. The training environment is intense, disciplined, and deeply traditional. Even for those not in the Judo club, the presence of such elite athletes creates an atmosphere of discipline and excellence that permeates the entire campus culture.

## 🌏 International Student Support and the Bekka Program

Tenri University provides robust support for its international population. The 'Bekka' (Japanese Language Preparatory Course) is a specialized one-year program for students who need to improve their Japanese before entering a regular degree program. It is highly regarded for its intensive curriculum and high success rate in placing students into undergraduate faculties.

Once enrolled in a degree program, international students benefit from:
- **Tuition Reductions:** Tenri University offers a generous tuition reduction scheme for privately financed international students, often covering 30% or more of the tuition fees based on academic performance and financial need.
- **Tutor System:** New international students are paired with Japanese student tutors who help them navigate everything from opening a bank account to understanding local Nara dialects.
- **Dedicated International Office:** The staff are experienced in handling visa renewals, scholarship applications (such as MEXT and JASSO), and finding part-time work.

## 🏘️ Living in Tenri City: A Unique Experience

Tenri is often described as a 'Religious City' (Shukyo-toshi), similar to how Vatican City functions, though it remains a public Japanese municipality. For a student, this means the city is incredibly safe, clean, and quiet. 

**Cost of Living:** Compared to Tokyo, the cost of living in Tenri is significantly lower. Rent for a decent student apartment can range from 25,000 to 45,000 JPY per month. Food is also affordable, with local markets providing fresh produce from the Nara countryside.

**Cultural Immersion:** The city is filled with 'Oyasato-yakata' buildings and the Tenrikyo Church Headquarters, which are architectural marvels. Students often witness major festivals and the practice of 'Hinokishin' (voluntary service), where thousands of people come together to clean the city. This sense of community is something international students often find deeply moving and welcoming.

**Location:** While Tenri is a quiet city, it is very well-connected. It is a short 20-minute train ride to Nara Park (famous for its deer and the Great Buddha), about 45 minutes to Osaka (for shopping and nightlife), and 60 minutes to Kyoto. This allows students to enjoy a peaceful study environment while having easy access to Japan's major cultural and commercial hubs.

## 📝 Admission Guide for International Applicants

Admission typically involves several steps tailored for international students:
1. **Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU):** Most faculties require EJU scores, particularly in 'Japanese as a Foreign Language.'
2. **University Entrance Examination:** This usually consists of a specialized subject test and an essay (in Japanese).
3. **Interview:** A critical part of the process where professors assess the applicant’s motivation, Japanese level, and alignment with the university’s 'Joyous Life' philosophy.

Applicants should generally aim for a Japanese proficiency level of N2 or higher for the Faculty of International Studies and Humanities, while the Faculty of Health Care almost certainly requires N1.

## 💰 Financial Aid and Scholarships

In addition to the university's own tuition reduction, students can apply for:
- **JASSO Scholarships:** Monthly stipends for high-achieving international students.
- **Local Foundation Scholarships:** Nara-based organizations often provide grants to students living in the prefecture.
- **Tenrikyo-related Scholarships:** Some specific grants may be available for students who are active members of the Tenrikyo community or who demonstrate a strong commitment to humanitarian service.

## 🚀 Career Prospects

Tenri University has a strong reputation for producing graduates with high moral character and resilience. The Career Support Center provides specialized guidance for international students looking to work in Japan, including resume workshops and mock interviews. Many graduates find roles in international trade, tourism (leveraging Nara’s tourism industry), education, and sports coaching. For those returning home, the Tenri name is highly respected in the global martial arts community and among Japanese-affiliated NGOs.

## 🎯 Conclusion: Why Choose Tenri University?

Tenri University is not just a place to get a degree; it is a place to refine one's character. If you are looking for a massive, anonymous campus in a neon-lit city, Tenri might not be for you. However, if you seek a university with a 'soul,' where you will be treated as part of a global family, and where you can immerse yourself in the deepest roots of Japanese culture and physical discipline, Tenri University is an exceptional choice. It offers the rare opportunity to study in a serene, historic environment while gaining the language skills and cultural competence needed to succeed in a globalized world. Whether you are a Judoka aiming for the Olympics or a scholar of Asian history, Tenri provides a supportive, spiritually grounded foundation for your future.
