## 🏫 University Overview

Osaka University of Commerce (OUC), known in Japanese as Osaka Shogyo Daigaku or simply 'Daisho-dai,' is a prestigious private university located in Higashiosaka, Osaka. Established in 1949, the university’s roots trace back to the Osaka Johoku Commercial School founded in 1928. OUC is guided by its founding philosophy: 'Developing individuals who are helpful to the world.' This mission manifests in a curriculum designed to foster entrepreneurship, practical business acumen, and a deep understanding of the global economy.

As an international student, OUC offers a unique vantage point. It is situated in Higashiosaka, a city famous for having one of the highest densities of small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) in Japan. This provides a 'living laboratory' for students studying business and economics. The university focuses heavily on practical education (Jitsugaku), ensuring that students do not just learn theory but understand how to apply it in real-world markets. For those looking to start their own business or join the backbone of the Japanese economy—its robust SME sector—OUC is an ideal destination.

## 🎓 Academic Faculties and Departments

OUC streamlines its expertise into two primary faculties, each offering specialized departments that cater to the evolving needs of the global business landscape.

### 1. Faculty of Business Administration
This faculty is designed for students who aspire to become corporate leaders, entrepreneurs, or managers in the entertainment and leisure industries.
- **Department of Business Management:** Focuses on the core pillars of corporate success: strategy, marketing, human resources, and accounting. It emphasizes the 'Entrepreneurship Course,' which supports students in developing business plans and understanding the risks and rewards of starting a venture.
- **Department of Public Affairs:** This unique department explores the intersection of business and society. It prepares students for careers in public service, non-profits, or corporate social responsibility (CSR) departments, emphasizing how business can solve social issues.

### 2. Faculty of Economics
This faculty provides the analytical tools necessary to understand the movement of money, goods, and services on a macro and micro level.
- **Department of Economics:** Students study traditional economic theory alongside modern applications like data science and financial technology. There is a strong emphasis on the Japanese economy's role in Asia and the world.
- **Department of International Economics:** (Often integrated within specific tracks) This focus area looks at global trade, exchange rates, and the economic development of emerging markets, making it a popular choice for international students.

### 🎡 Research Institute for Amusement Industry
One of OUC’s most distinctive features is its Research Institute for Amusement Industry. As the only academic institution in Japan focusing on the business of gaming, pachinko, theme parks, and the broader 'leisure' industry, it offers students a chance to study a multi-billion dollar sector that is often overlooked by traditional academia. This specialized knowledge is highly valued in the Kansai region, which is a hub for tourism and entertainment.

## 🌏 Support for International Students

OUC is committed to creating an inclusive environment. International students are not just guests; they are an integral part of the campus community. The university provides several layers of support to ensure success:

### 💴 Financial Aid and Scholarships
Understanding the financial burden of studying abroad, OUC offers a 'Tuition Reduction Program' for self-financed international students. Depending on academic performance and financial need, students can receive a 30% reduction in their annual tuition fees. Additionally, the university assists students in applying for external scholarships such as the JASSO Honors Scholarship and various private foundation grants.

### 🇯🇵 Japanese Language and Cultural Integration
While most degree programs are taught in Japanese, the university offers specialized Japanese language support classes. These courses are tailored to business Japanese, helping students master the polite language (keigo) and professional etiquette required to thrive in the Japanese corporate world. The 'International Exchange Lounge' serves as a social hub where international students can interact with Japanese peers to practice their language skills in an informal setting.

### 🏠 Housing and Life in Osaka
While OUC does not have a traditional on-campus dormitory for every student, they provide comprehensive support in finding affordable apartments near the campus. Higashiosaka is significantly more affordable than central Tokyo or even central Osaka (Umeda/Namba), making it easier for students to manage their living expenses. The campus is just a 10-minute walk from Kawachi-Kosaka Station on the Kintetsu Nara Line, providing direct access to the vibrant Namba district in 15 minutes and Nara in 30 minutes.

## 📝 Admission Process for International Students

Applying to OUC requires careful preparation, specifically targeting the 'Special Entrance Examination for International Students.'

### Eligibility Requirements
1.  **Educational Background:** Completion of 12 years of formal education outside of Japan.
2.  **Language Proficiency:** Most applicants should aim for a JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test) level of N2 or higher. Alternatively, scores from the EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students) in the 'Japanese as a Foreign Language' subject are used to evaluate proficiency.

### Examination Components
- **Document Screening:** Assessment of high school transcripts and the Statement of Purpose.
- **Japanese Language Test:** An internal exam if EJU scores are not provided/sufficient.
- **Interview:** A crucial part of the process where faculty members assess the applicant's motivation, logical thinking, and future career goals in Japan.
- **Short Essay (Xiao-lun-wen):** Students are often asked to write their opinions on a current business or social issue to test their ability to structure an argument in Japanese.

## 💼 Career Prospects and Employment Support

OUC boasts one of the highest employment rates in the Kansai region. The Career Support Center begins working with students as early as their second year. For international students, the hurdles of the Japanese 'Shukatsu' (job hunting) season can be daunting, but OUC provides:
- **Individual Counseling:** One-on-one sessions to refine resumes and practice mock interviews.
- **Company Information Sessions:** Over 300 companies visit the campus annually to recruit students.
- **Networking with Alumni:** OUC has a vast network of graduates working in manufacturing, banking, and retail across Osaka. This network is particularly strong among SMEs, where international students can often find roles as 'bridges' between Japanese headquarters and overseas branches.

## 🏫 Campus Facilities and Lifestyle

The campus is a blend of modern architecture and functional spaces. The 'Recreation Center' includes a large gymnasium and fitness facilities, reflecting the university's strong athletic culture. The University Library is well-stocked with business periodicals and global economic reports, providing a quiet space for deep study. 

Outside of class, students can join various 'Circles' (clubs). Whether it is a sports club like baseball or soccer, or a cultural club like calligraphy or photography, these organizations are the best way for international students to make lifelong Japanese friends. The annual 'U-shosai' University Festival is a highlight, where international students often set up food stalls to share their home country's cuisine with the local community.

## 🏁 Conclusion: Is OUC Right for You?

Osaka University of Commerce is not just a place to get a degree; it is a place to build a career. If you are an international student who is:
- Interested in the practical mechanics of business and entrepreneurship.
- Eager to work in Japan after graduation.
- Looking for a supportive, community-focused environment in a culturally rich city like Osaka.

Then, OUC offers a compelling and specialized education that will prepare you to be 'helpful to the world' and successful in the global marketplace. The university's proximity to the industrial heart of Japan and its unique focus on the amusement and service industries make it a standout choice for the pragmatic student.
