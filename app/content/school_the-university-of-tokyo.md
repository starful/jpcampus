## üè´ School Overview & Philosophy

The University of Tokyo, often shortened to Todai, is Japan's most prestigious university and a globally recognized research institution. Established in 1877, Todai has played a crucial role in nurturing Japan's intellectual, political, and economic leaders. Beyond simply imparting knowledge, the university aims to cultivate students' critical thinking, creativity, and leadership skills. The University of Tokyo upholds a long-standing tradition of 'respect for autonomy,' ensuring broad academic freedom for students and supporting them in designing their own learning plans. This educational philosophy encourages students to develop the ability to identify and solve problems independently, helping them grow into innovative individuals necessary for future society.

Beyond academic excellence, Todai emphasizes social responsibility. The university contributes to solving various social issues and strives to contribute to sustainable social development. It supports various research projects and social contribution activities and encourages students to take an interest in and participate in social issues. Furthermore, Todai values international collaboration, actively engaging in exchanges with universities and research institutions worldwide. This allows students to broaden their global perspective and cultivate an international sense. The University of Tokyo is constantly striving to grow into a university that exerts global leadership, going beyond its reputation as the best university in Japan.

The University of Tokyo harmoniously blends historical architecture with modern facilities throughout its campuses. In particular, the Akamon (Red Gate) is an iconic structure of Todai, boasting a long history and beloved by many. Furthermore, the university library, the largest in Japan, supports students' learning and research activities with its vast collection of academic materials. In addition to these, various sports facilities, cultural facilities, and student lounges are provided to help students enjoy a diverse campus life. The University of Tokyo is constantly striving to provide the best environment for students to grow and develop, beyond being a simple learning space. Students will be able to maximize their potential in this environment and grow into leaders of future society.

The University of Tokyo consists of 10 faculties, 15 graduate schools, and various affiliated research institutions. Each faculty and graduate school provides the highest level of education and research in its respective field, and students can choose from a variety of majors according to their interests. In particular, Todai does not spare investment in basic academic fields and is at the forefront of creating new knowledge through innovative research. In addition, Todai encourages integrated research, and experts from various fields are working together to solve complex social problems. The University of Tokyo is constantly striving to cultivate individuals with both academic depth and a broad perspective and supports students to actively cope with changes in future society.

## üåç Student Body & Campus Life

The University of Tokyo is an international campus where students of various nationalities study together. The percentage of international students is steadily increasing, with approximately 3,700 foreign students currently enrolled. The nationalities of the students are very diverse, with students from all over the world, including Asia, Europe, North America, and South America, studying together. This diversity invigorates the campus and provides students with a good opportunity to cultivate an international sense.

The University of Tokyo has various student support systems. It provides orientation, mentoring programs, and language support programs for international students to help them adapt to life in Japan. In addition, students can interact with each other and build friendships through various student clubs, club activities, and sports activities. The University of Tokyo supports students to grow not only through academics but also through various experiences.

## üéì University & Career Pathways

The University of Tokyo provides various support systems for students' successful careers. The Career Support Center provides students with career counseling, resume writing guidance, and interview skills training to help them find jobs that suit their aptitude. It also provides internship opportunities through collaboration with various companies to support students in gaining practical experience. Graduates of the University of Tokyo are active in various fields such as leading domestic and international companies, research institutions, and government agencies.

The University of Tokyo also provides generous support for students who wish to pursue graduate studies. Each faculty and graduate school provides guidance on entering higher education, research guidance, and thesis writing guidance to help students explore their research fields in depth. The University of Tokyo Graduate School is a globally recognized research-oriented graduate school, and its graduates are growing into leaders in various fields such as academia and industry. The University of Tokyo is constantly striving to maximize students' potential and contribute to future society.

## üè° Accommodation & Living Support

The University of Tokyo offers various accommodation options for international students. Dormitories are located on or near campus, allowing students to live safely and conveniently at a low cost. The dormitories consist of single or double rooms and are equipped with various amenities such as a shared kitchen, laundry room, and lounge. In addition, the dormitories host various cultural exchange programs and events to help students interact and build friendships.

In addition to dormitories, the University of Tokyo provides various accommodation information. There are various apartments and studio apartments around the university, and students can choose accommodation according to their budget and preferences. In addition, it cooperates with real estate agencies to support international students in finding safe and convenient accommodation. The University of Tokyo provides various accommodation options and living support services to ensure that students can live comfortably and safely.
