## 🏫 University Overview

Kagawa Prefectural University of Health Sciences (KPUHS), or 香川県立保健医療大学, is a prestigious public institution located in the scenic city of Takamatsu, Kagawa Prefecture. Established in 1999 following its evolution from the Kagawa Prefectural College of Health Sciences, the university has cemented its reputation as a leading center for medical education in the Shikoku region. Its core philosophy, "Humanity and Science," emphasizes the development of healthcare professionals who possess not only advanced technical skills but also a deep sense of empathy and ethical responsibility.

As a prefectural university, KPUHS is deeply integrated into the local community. It serves as a vital pipeline for nurses and clinical laboratory technicians for the hospitals of Kagawa and beyond. For international students, this university offers a unique opportunity to study in a specialized, high-standard environment without the overwhelming bustle of Tokyo or Osaka, providing a more authentic and immersive Japanese cultural experience.

## 🎓 Academic Excellence and Departments

The university is structured primarily around the **Faculty of Health Sciences**, which is divided into two major departments. Each department is designed to meet the rigorous standards of the Japanese national healthcare system.

### 1. Department of Nursing
The Nursing program is the flagship of KPUHS. It goes beyond basic patient care, focusing on "Holistic Nursing." Students are trained to understand the patient’s lifestyle, psychological state, and social background. The curriculum includes intensive clinical practice at major regional hospitals, such as the Kagawa Prefectural Central Hospital. A standout feature is the university's consistently high pass rate for the National Examination for Nurses, often hovering near 100%. Students also have the option to pursue additional qualifications as Public Health Nurses or Midwives through specialized elective tracks.

### 2. Department of Clinical Laboratory Science
This department focuses on the diagnostic side of medicine. Students learn to perform complex analyses of blood, tissue, and body fluids, as well as physiological tests like EKGs and ultrasounds. The university invests heavily in modern laboratory equipment, ensuring that students work with the same technology found in top-tier diagnostic centers. Graduates become Clinical Laboratory Technicians, a role that is increasingly critical in the era of personalized medicine and genetic testing.

### 3. Graduate School of Health Sciences
For students seeking to engage in advanced research, the Graduate School offers Master’s and Doctoral programs. Research areas include advanced nursing practice, infectious disease control, and biomedical analysis. The graduate programs are particularly attractive to international students who already have a healthcare license in their home country and wish to specialize further in the Japanese context.

## 🌏 International Student Support and Admissions

Studying healthcare in Japan as a foreigner is a challenging but rewarding path. KPUHS welcomes international applicants, though it is important to note that the competition is fierce. 

### Admission Requirements
Most international applicants must take the **Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU)**. Since the national medical examinations in Japan are conducted entirely in Japanese, KPUHS requires a high level of language proficiency. Applicants should ideally possess JLPT N1 or an equivalent level of fluency to keep up with the technical terminology and clinical rotations. The entrance examination usually consists of a review of EJU scores, a written essay (in Japanese), and a rigorous interview conducted by faculty members to assess the applicant's motivation and suitability for a career in healthcare.

### Tuition and Financial Aid
One of the biggest advantages of KPUHS is its affordability. As a public university, its tuition is significantly lower than private medical universities. For international students, the standard yearly tuition is approximately 535,800 JPY. There is also an admission fee of 282,000 JPY, though this may be halved for residents of Kagawa Prefecture (including students who have resided there for a certain period). Various scholarships, such as the JASSO scholarship and local Kagawa-based foundations, are available to help cover living costs.

## 🏥 Clinical Training and Career Prospects

KPUHS has established strong partnerships with medical institutions throughout Shikoku. Clinical practice is a cornerstone of the education here. During their third and fourth years, students spend a significant amount of time in hospitals, working alongside veteran professionals. This hands-on experience is invaluable for building the confidence needed to enter the workforce immediately upon graduation.

For international students, the career path in Japan is very clear: upon passing the National Examination, you are eligible to work in any Japanese hospital. Given the country's aging population, there is a chronic shortage of qualified healthcare workers, making the employment rate for KPUHS graduates virtually 100%. The university’s Career Support Center provides specialized guidance for international students on how to navigate the Japanese job hunting (Shu-katsu) process, including resume writing and interview prep.

## 🌊 Student Life in Takamatsu, Kagawa

Living in Takamatsu offers a high quality of life at a fraction of the cost of Japan's major metropolises. The campus is located in the Mure-cho area, famous for its association with the sculptor Isamu Noguchi and its high-quality stone. 

### Accommodation
While the university does not have a dedicated international dormitory, the student affairs office assists students in finding affordable private apartments nearby. Rents in Takamatsu are very reasonable, typically ranging from 30,000 to 50,000 JPY per month for a studio apartment.

### Culture and Leisure
Kagawa is famously known as the "Udon Prefecture." Students can enjoy world-class Sanuki Udon for just a few hundred yen. Takamatsu is also the gateway to the beautiful islands of the Seto Inland Sea, such as Naoshima and Shodoshima, making it a perfect location for those who love art and nature. The climate is mild with little rainfall, providing a comfortable environment for study throughout the year.

## 💡 Why Choose Kagawa Prefectural University of Health Sciences?

1.  **Specialization:** Unlike large comprehensive universities, KPUHS focuses exclusively on health sciences. Every resource, from the library to the simulation labs, is tailored to your specific field of study.
2.  **Community:** With a small student body, you are not just a number. Professors provide personalized attention, which is crucial for international students navigating a foreign medical system.
3.  **Stability:** Public university status ensures a stable, high-quality curriculum and facilities that are maintained to government standards.
4.  **License Path:** The curriculum is strictly aligned with the Japanese National Exams, providing the most direct route to becoming a licensed professional in Japan.

## 📝 Conclusion for Prospective Students

Kagawa Prefectural University of Health Sciences is an ideal choice for the dedicated international student who is serious about a career in the Japanese healthcare sector. It requires a high level of Japanese language proficiency and a strong commitment to service. In return, it offers a world-class education, a supportive community, and a gateway to a stable career in one of the most respected healthcare systems in the world. If you seek a place where science meets humanity, and where you can truly integrate into Japanese society while mastering your craft, KPUHS awaits you in the beautiful 'Udon Prefecture' of Kagawa.
