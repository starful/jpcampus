## üè´ University Overview

Otaru University of Commerce (OUC), known in Japanese as 'Otaru Shoka Daigaku' or the affectionate shorthand 'Tarudai,' is a prestigious national university located in the scenic port city of Otaru, Hokkaido. Established in 1910 as the Otaru Higher Commercial School, it holds the distinction of being the only national university in Japan exclusively dedicated to the study of commerce and social sciences. For over a century, OUC has been a cornerstone of business education in Northern Japan, producing leaders in finance, trade, and public administration. 

For international students, OUC offers a unique proposition: the academic rigor and low tuition of a national university, combined with a highly specialized curriculum and a tight-knit community. Unlike massive urban universities in Tokyo, OUC provides an environment where students and faculty build close relationships. The university is situated on 'Jigoku-zaka' (Hell Slope), a steep climb that rewards students with a breathtaking view of Ishikari Bay and the Otaru Port, symbolizing the grit and perspective the institution aims to instill in its graduates.

## üéì Academic Structure and Departments

Despite its specialized focus, OUC offers a diverse range of studies within the Faculty of Commerce. The undergraduate program is divided into four primary departments, each designed to provide a deep understanding of the global economy and local industrial needs.

1. **Department of Commerce:** This is the flagship department of the university. It focuses on marketing, accounting, and international trade. Students learn the practicalities of business management, from consumer behavior analysis to advanced corporate accounting. It is particularly well-regarded for its high success rate in the national CPA (Certified Public Accountant) examinations.

2. **Department of Economics:** This department explores the theoretical and empirical aspects of economic systems. Students analyze market mechanisms, public policy, and international finance. The curriculum emphasizes both the mathematical foundations of economic theory and the historical context of global economic shifts.

3. **Department of Law:** Focused primarily on corporate and civil law, this department prepares students for the legal aspects of business transactions and public administration. It is an excellent choice for those looking to enter the legal departments of major corporations or pursue careers in the Japanese civil service.

4. **Department of Information and Management Science:** Recognizing the digital transformation of the global economy, this department integrates management theory with information technology and data science. Students learn programming, database management, and operations research, making them highly competitive in the modern tech-driven business world.

OUC also features a 'Major-Minor' system, allowing students to specialize in their primary department while gaining substantial knowledge in a second field, ensuring a well-rounded and versatile skill set.

## üåç The Global Outlook & International Programs

OUC has been proactive in internationalizing its campus. The university maintains exchange agreements with numerous institutions across Asia, Europe, and North America. For international students, the 'Global Learning Studies' (GLS) program is a highlight, offering courses taught in English that cover Japanese business culture, regional economics, and international relations.

International students are not just learners but contributors to the campus culture. The university hosts various cultural exchange events throughout the year, including the 'Otaru International Week,' where students showcase their home cultures. The International Exchange Center provides robust support, including Japanese language training tailored to different proficiency levels, ensuring that students can eventually transition into mainstream courses taught in Japanese.

## üìù Admission Process for International Students

Admission to Otaru University of Commerce is competitive, reflecting its status as a top-tier national institution. International applicants typically enter through the 'Special Admission for Privately Financed International Students' track.

**Requirements:**
- **EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission):** This is the most critical component. Applicants must take the EJU subjects including Japanese as a Foreign Language, Japan and the World (General Studies), and Mathematics (Course 1). High scores in the Japanese section are particularly vital, as most core lectures are conducted in Japanese.
- **English Proficiency:** While OUC is a Japanese-medium university, submission of TOEFL or TOEIC scores is often required or highly recommended to demonstrate global competency.
- **University Entrance Exam:** After passing the initial document screening based on EJU scores, candidates are usually required to participate in an on-campus interview and occasionally a short essay exam (in Japanese). The interview focuses on the applicant's motivation, understanding of their chosen field, and future career plans in Japan or abroad.

## üíº Career Support and Alumni Network

One of the strongest selling points of OUC is its 'Shushoku' (job hunting) support. The university consistently boasts one of the highest employment rates among national universities in Japan. The 'Hokushinkai,' OUC‚Äôs powerful alumni association, plays a pivotal role here. With over 60,000 members, many of whom hold executive positions in Japanese banks, trading companies (Sogo Shosha), and government offices, the network provides students with unparalleled access to internships and job opportunities.

Career services at OUC start early, with seminars on resume writing, mock interviews, and industry analysis. For international students, there is specific guidance on navigating the unique Japanese recruitment season. Many international graduates from OUC find success in Japanese multinational firms or as bridges between Japan and their home countries.

## üèîÔ∏è Campus Life and The Otaru Experience

Life at OUC is defined by its location. Otaru is a city of history, famous for its canal, stone warehouses, and glassworks. It is significantly quieter and more affordable than Tokyo or Osaka, but only 35-45 minutes away from Sapporo, the fifth-largest city in Japan. This allows students to enjoy a peaceful study environment while having easy access to the amenities and excitement of a major metropolis.

The campus itself is intimate. Because all students are in the Faculty of Commerce, there is a strong sense of camaraderie. The 'Hell Slope' ensures that every student stays physically fit, and the sight of the sunset over the harbor after a long day of classes is a rite of passage. Student clubs (circles) are very active, ranging from traditional Japanese arts like Kendo and Tea Ceremony to modern sports and business entrepreneurship clubs.

## üí∞ Tuition, Scholarships, and Financial Support

As a national university, OUC‚Äôs tuition is standardized and significantly lower than private universities. The current annual tuition is 535,800 JPY, with an admission fee of 282,000 JPY. For many international students, the university offers a tuition waiver program (full or half exemption) based on academic merit and financial need.

In addition to university-specific waivers, students can apply for the MEXT scholarship (embassy or university recommendation) and the JASSO (Japan Student Services Organization) honors scholarship. The lower cost of living in Otaru compared to the Kanto region means that these stipends go much further, covering rent, food, and travel comfortably.

## üè† Living in Otaru

Otaru is one of Japan's most popular tourist destinations, known for its seafood, particularly sushi. For a student, this means access to high-quality food and a beautiful urban environment. Housing is much more accessible than in larger cities. OUC provides a dedicated international student dormitory (Gokuraku-ryo) located within walking distance of the campus, offering affordable rooms and a chance to live alongside Japanese students.

Winter in Otaru is legendary. The city receives heavy snowfall, transforming the campus into a winter wonderland. While the cold requires adjustment, it offers unique opportunities for skiing and snowboarding, with some of the world's best resorts (like Niseko and Kiroro) just a short bus ride away. The Otaru Snow Light Path Festival in February is a magical time when the whole city, including the university, is illuminated by lanterns.

## üéØ Conclusion

Otaru University of Commerce is an ideal choice for the serious student of business and economics who seeks a prestigious Japanese degree without the overwhelming chaos of a mega-city. Its century-old reputation, powerful alumni network, and specialization in commerce provide a clear pathway to a career in Japan's corporate sector. While the 'Hell Slope' and the Hokkaido winters are challenging, they forge a resilient spirit in students. For an international student looking to truly master the Japanese language and business culture while enjoying the natural beauty of Hokkaido, OUC stands as one of the best-kept secrets in Japanese higher education. It is not just a place to study; it is a community that prepares you for the global stage from the historic hills of Otaru.
