## 🏫 University Overview

Tezukayama Gakuin University (TGU) is a prestigious private institution located in the heart of the Kansai region, specifically in Sakai City, Osaka. Established with roots dating back to 1916 as an educational academy, the university officially opened its doors in 1966. It carries the weight of over a century of educational tradition, evolving from an elite finishing school for young women into a modern, co-educational institution that emphasizes the "Power of Character" and the cultivation of refined, practical wisdom.

For international students, Tezukayama Gakuin represents a unique blend of Japanese traditional values and forward-thinking academic rigor. Unlike the massive, impersonal national universities, TGU is celebrated for its human-centric approach. With a campus culture that facilitates close interactions between faculty and students, it provides an ideal environment for non-native speakers to master the Japanese language while pursuing specialized degrees. The university’s slogan, "Power to the Individual," reflects its commitment to identifying the unique strengths of every student and polishing them into professional skills suitable for the global market.

## 🎓 Academic Excellence and Faculties

Tezukayama Gakuin University is structured around three main pillars of undergraduate study, each designed to meet the demands of the modern workforce while staying true to the liberal arts tradition.

### 1. Faculty of Liberal Arts
This faculty is the spiritual heart of the university. It focuses on developing multifaceted perspectives. Students here can specialize in various fields including **Japanese Culture**, **Global Communications**, and **Art & Design**. For international students, the Japanese Culture major is particularly attractive as it offers an academic deep-dive into the history, aesthetics, and social structures of Japan, often including practical components like tea ceremony and calligraphy. The Global Communications major, meanwhile, prepares students for international business and diplomatic roles, emphasizing linguistic proficiency and intercultural competence.

### 2. Faculty of Psychology
TGU is widely recognized as a powerhouse in the field of Psychology in the Kansai region. It offers specialized tracks in **Clinical Psychology**, **Social Psychology**, and **Developmental Psychology**. The university operates its own clinical psychology counseling centers, providing students with observation opportunities and practical insights that are rare at the undergraduate level. For those aiming to work in mental health, human resources, or social services in Japan, this faculty provides a highly respected credential.

### 3. Faculty of Food and Health
Located at the intersection of science and wellness, this faculty trains students to become **Registered Dietitians** and food industry professionals. Japan is a world leader in nutrition and longevity, and TGU’s curriculum reflects this expertise. Students engage in rigorous lab work, culinary chemistry, and public health studies. The facility’s labs are state-of-the-art, ensuring that students are career-ready upon graduation. International students interested in Japan’s food culture and its scientific foundations will find this program exceptionally rewarding.

## 🌏 Support for International Students

Recognizing the challenges of studying abroad, Tezukayama Gakuin University offers a robust support system designed to help international students integrate both academically and socially. 

- **Japanese Language Support:** The university provides supplementary Japanese language courses tailored to different proficiency levels. These are not just about grammar; they focus on "Academic Japanese" to help students write reports and participate in seminars effectively.
- **The International Center:** This is the hub for student life. Staff provide assistance with visa renewals, housing, and health insurance. They also organize cultural exchange events where international students can share their home cultures with local Japanese students.
- **Tutoring System:** Many international students are assigned a Japanese student tutor. This peer-to-peer support is invaluable for navigating the nuances of daily life in Osaka, from understanding local dialects to finding the best affordable supermarkets.
- **Scholarships:** TGU offers internal tuition reduction schemes for self-financed international students, typically ranging from 20% to 30% reduction based on academic performance and financial need. They also assist students in applying for prestigious external scholarships like the MEXT honors scholarship.

## 📍 Campus Life and Location: Sakai, Osaka

The Izumigaoka Campus in Sakai City offers a peaceful, green environment that is still incredibly accessible to the bustling city center of Osaka. Sakai is famous for its ancient "Kofun" (keyhole-shaped burial mounds) and its history as a center of trade and traditional knife-making. 

- **Accessibility:** From the campus, it is a short train ride to Namba, the heart of Osaka’s shopping and entertainment district. This allows students to enjoy a quiet study environment while remaining close to the professional and social opportunities of a major metropolis.
- **Facilities:** The campus features a modern library with an extensive collection of psychology and food science journals, well-equipped computer labs, and a student cafeteria that serves healthy, affordable meals—often designed with input from the Faculty of Food and Health.
- **Clubs and Circles:** Students are encouraged to join extracurricular activities. Whether it is the traditional Tea Ceremony Club, the Dance Club, or various sports teams, these groups are the best way for international students to make lifelong Japanese friends and practice conversational Japanese in a natural setting.

## 🍱 Career Prospects and Employment

Tezukayama Gakuin University boasts a high employment rate, thanks in part to its strong reputation among Kansai-based corporations. The Career Center is proactive, offering workshops on Japanese business etiquette, resume writing (RIREKISHO), and mock interviews starting from the students’ third year. 

For international students, the path to employment in Japan is well-supported. The university’s strong ties with local industries in Osaka and Sakai mean that students have access to internships and job fairs specifically looking for bilingual talent. Graduates often find roles in hospitality, international trade, education, and the food industry. Those from the Psychology faculty often pursue further education to become certified clinical psychologists, a field growing in demand across East Asia.

## 📝 Admission Guide for International Applicants

Admission to TGU is based on a holistic review of the applicant's potential. The standard track for international students involves:

1.  **Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU):** Most faculties require scores in Japanese as a Foreign Language. Some may also look at Global Issues or Mathematics depending on the faculty.
2.  **University Entrance Exam:** This usually consists of a written essay (in Japanese) and a comprehensive interview.
3.  **Interview:** This is the most crucial part for TGU. They look for students who are not only academically capable but also have a clear vision of how they want to contribute to society and why they have chosen Tezukayama Gakuin specifically.

Prospective students should aim for at least an N2 level of JLPT proficiency before applying, as most lectures are conducted entirely in Japanese. However, for those with a strong drive, the university’s supportive environment can help close the linguistic gap quickly.
