## üåç International Student Support

Meijo University recognizes the challenges faced by international students and has established specific mechanisms to ensure their successful integration and academic achievement. The International Center serves as the primary contact point for prospective and enrolled students, assisting with visa procedures, campus life orientation, and academic advising.

**Japanese Language Support:** While Meijo does not offer a preparatory Japanese language school, it provides ongoing Japanese language and cultural training classes specifically tailored for enrolled degree students whose proficiency needs improvement to handle specialized lectures. This support is vital for navigating the largely Japanese-taught curriculum.

**Housing:** University-managed dormitory options for international students are limited and highly competitive. While Meijo assists in connecting students with reliable private housing agencies (like those managed by JASSO or affiliated partners), incoming students should budget for and expect to secure private accommodation in the surrounding areas of the Tempaku or Yagoto campuses. Nagoya‚Äôs public transport system is excellent, making commuting feasible.

**Scholarships and Financial Aid:** Meijo offers access to several pivotal financial aid programs, making studying here more accessible:

1.  **Meijo University's Internal Scholarship System:** Specific scholarships target academically high-performing international students, offering reductions or waivers on tuition fees after admission, often contingent on maintaining high academic standing.
2.  **MEXT (Monbukagakusho) Scholarship:** Students applying through university recommendation or embassy recommendation for MEXT scholarships can often designate Meijo University, especially in STEM fields, due to its research profile.
3.  **JASSO (Japan Student Services Organization) Scholarships:** Many international students at Meijo are eligible for the JASSO Honors Scholarship for Privately Financed International Students, which provides a monthly stipend.
4.  **Private Foundation Scholarships:** Located in a major economic hub, Meijo students have excellent access to numerous regional private foundation scholarships that prioritize students studying in the Chubu area. Support staff assist students in identifying and applying for these external opportunities.

Support services also extend to career guidance. Given Meijo‚Äôs excellent connections with the industries in the Chubu region (automotive, machinery, aerospace, and electronics), the Career Support Center offers specialized assistance, resume workshops, and interview practice tailored for international graduates seeking employment in Japan, resulting in remarkably high employment rates post-graduation.
