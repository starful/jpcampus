## 🏫 University Overview
Established in 1996 in the scenic coastal city of Takamatsu, Kagawa Prefecture, Takamatsu University (高松大学, Takamatsu Daigaku) is a private institution that has earned a solid reputation for its commitment to practical, community-oriented higher education. The university operates under the educational philosophy of "Respect for Humanity" and "Practical Learning," aiming to cultivate self-reliant professionals who can contribute actively to regional revitalization and international society.

Takamatsu University's modern campus features state-of-the-art facilities designed to foster interactive learning, close student-faculty relationships, and a warm, inclusive community. Unlike massive urban universities, Takamatsu University prides itself on its "face-to-face" education system. Here, professors maintain close advisory roles, guiding students through their academic journeys, job hunting, and daily life adjustments in Japan. This personalized support system is highly beneficial for international students navigating a new cultural environment.

Situated on the beautiful island of Shikoku, the university provides a peaceful, safe, and highly focused environment for academic pursuit, away from the distractions and high living costs of megacities. At the same time, it maintains robust career connections with regional and national enterprises, ensuring students achieve their professional goals.

## 🎓 Academic Faculties & Programs
Takamatsu University offers highly specialized undergraduate programs designed to meet the demands of the modern global and local job markets. The university consists of two primary faculties:

### 1. Faculty of Management (Department of Management)
The Faculty of Management is the cornerstone for students aspiring to build careers in business, entrepreneurship, tourism, and regional development. The curriculum is structured around key specialized pathways:
- **Business Administration & Entrepreneurship:** Focuses on corporate strategy, marketing, human resource management, and venture business development.
- **Tourism & Hospitality Management:** Leverages Kagawa's rich cultural heritage and proximity to the famous Seto Inland Sea art islands (like Naoshima) to teach destination marketing, hospitality operations, and regional tourism planning.
- **Information Technology & Accounting:** Equips students with digital literacy, data analysis, corporate finance, and accounting skills essential for modern corporate environments.

The faculty emphasizes active learning, encouraging students to participate in "Local Area Research" projects where they collaborate directly with regional businesses to solve real-world challenges.

### 2. Faculty of Human Development (Department of Child Development)
This faculty is dedicated to training compassionate educators and childcare professionals. It offers comprehensive programs for students wishing to obtain Japanese national qualifications for teaching and childcare:
- **Childhood Education & Care:** Deeply explores child psychology, pediatric healthcare, creative arts education, and early childhood pedagogy.
- **Elementary School Teaching:** Prepares students for the rigorous teacher employment examinations in Japan, offering extensive hands-on teaching practicums in local primary schools.

International students enrolled in this faculty gain an intimate understanding of Japan's world-renowned educational frameworks, opening unique career paths in international schools, language academies, or educational consultation firms.

## 💴 Tuition, Fees & Financial Support
One of the most compelling reasons for international students to choose Takamatsu University is its affordability. Both tuition fees and the local cost of living are significantly lower than those in Japan's major metropolitan areas.

### Academic Fees Structure (Standard Annual Estimates)
- **Admission Fee (First Year Only):** 200,000 JPY
- **Annual Tuition Fee:** 730,000 JPY
- **Facilities & Practical Training Fees:** Approx. 150,000 JPY
- **Total First-Year Cost:** Approx. 1,080,000 JPY (Subsequent years cost around 880,000 JPY).

### Financial Aid & Tuition Reduction Schemes
Takamatsu University offers robust financial support mechanisms to ease the financial burden on international students:
- **Tuition Fee Reduction Program:** Eligible self-financed international students can receive a 30% to 50% reduction in tuition fees based on academic performance, financial need, and conduct.
- **Internal & External Scholarships:** Merit-based scholarships awarded annually to top-performing international students, alongside assistance in applying for prestigious external funding such as the JASSO honors scholarship, local government grants, and private foundation awards (e.g., Rotary Yoneyama Memorial Foundation).

## 🤝 Support for International Students
Takamatsu University is deeply committed to ensuring that international students feel safe, welcomed, and fully supported throughout their academic journey.

### 1. International Exchange Center (IEC)
The IEC acts as a dedicated home-away-from-home. It assists with essential administrative processes, including visa renewals, part-time work permits, national health insurance registration, and housing searches. The center also organizes regular cultural exchange events, bus tours around Shikoku, international food festivals, and homestay programs with local Japanese families.

### 2. Language and Academic Mentoring
To bridge the language gap, the university provides supplementary Japanese language support. Tutors and senior Japanese students are assigned to international newcomers to assist them with academic writing, presentation preparation, and everyday conversational practice.

### 3. Career Support and Job Placement
The Career Support Center offers intensive, individualized guidance starting from the student's sophomore year. Services include:
- Resume and entry-sheet writing workshops tailored to Japanese corporate expectations.
- Mock interviews and business etiquette training sessions.
- On-campus job fairs featuring regional enterprises eager to hire bilingual talent.
- Dedicated counseling for obtaining work visas upon graduation.
- Part-time job (arubaito) screening to ensure students find safe, legal, and fair-paying work that fits their academic schedules.

## 🗺️ Campus Life & Location
Located in Takamatsu, the capital city of Kagawa Prefecture, the university offers an enviable quality of life. Takamatsu is known as the "Gateway to Shikoku" and is famous for its mild, sunny climate, beautiful seaside parks, and world-class culinary scene (most notably Sanuki Udon).

### Cost of Living Advantage
Living in Takamatsu is exceptionally affordable:
- **Rent:** A private, modern apartment near the campus or city center typically costs between 30,000 JPY and 45,000 JPY per month—less than half of what a comparable apartment would cost in Tokyo.
- **Daily Expenses:** Local produce, seafood, and dining out are highly affordable, allowing students to live comfortably on a budget of approximately 80,000 to 100,000 JPY per month (including rent, food, utilities, and mobile plans).

### Transportation & Campus Accessibility
The campus is easily accessible via local bus routes and Kotoden train lines. Many students choose to commute by bicycle, as Takamatsu is flat and exceptionally bicycle-friendly. The city is well-connected to the rest of Japan via Takamatsu Airport and the JR Seto-Ohashi Line, making weekend trips to Okayama, Osaka, or Hiroshima highly convenient.

## 📈 Admission Requirements & How to Apply
Takamatsu University welcomes applications from ambitious international students. The admission process is designed to evaluate both academic readiness and the applicant’s motivation to study and integrate into Japanese society.

### Core Application Requirements
1. **Academic Eligibility:** Completion of 12 years of formal education outside of Japan (or equivalent) recognized by the Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology (MEXT).
2. **Language Proficiency:** Since all undergraduate lectures are conducted in Japanese, applicants must typically demonstrate proficiency equivalent to JLPT N2 or higher. Alternatively, a satisfactory score on the Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU) is accepted.
3. **Financial Solvency:** Proof of sufficient financial resources to cover tuition, living expenses, and academic materials during the study period (such as bank balances or sponsor letters).

### Selection Process
The entrance examination for international students generally consists of three parts:
- **Document Screening:** Evaluation of high school transcripts, statement of purpose, and recommendation letters.
- **Written Examination:** A basic Japanese language essay test to assess written expression, vocabulary, and logical structure.
- **Interview:** An in-depth, individual interview conducted in Japanese to evaluate communication skills, academic goals, and cultural adaptability.
