## üè´ University Overview

Rikkyo University, also known as Saint Paul's University, is a private university located in Tokyo, Japan. Founded in 1874 by Bishop Channing Moore Williams, an American Episcopal missionary, Rikkyo boasts a rich history and a strong reputation for academic excellence and a commitment to liberal arts education. Originally established as a theological school, Rikkyo has evolved into a comprehensive university offering a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs. The university is particularly well-known for its international focus and its beautiful campuses, especially the iconic Ikebukuro campus with its ivy-covered buildings. Rikkyo is a member of the Global 30 project and has a long history of welcoming international students. It consistently ranks among the top private universities in Japan.

The university emphasizes a liberal arts approach, fostering critical thinking, creativity, and communication skills in its students. Its commitment to internationalization is evident in its numerous exchange programs and the diverse student body. Rikkyo aims to cultivate global citizens who can contribute to society in meaningful ways.

Rikkyo University maintains a strong network of alumni who are active in various fields, including business, politics, academia, and the arts. This network provides valuable support and opportunities for current students and graduates.

## üåç International Student Support

Rikkyo University offers a comprehensive range of support services for international students to help them adjust to life in Japan and succeed in their studies. These services include:

- **Dormitory:** Rikkyo provides several dormitories and off-campus housing options specifically for international students. These residences offer a comfortable and supportive environment for students to live and study.
- **English Programs:** A variety of courses are taught in English, particularly within the College of Global Liberal Arts and some programs in other colleges. Japanese language courses are also offered to help students improve their language proficiency.
- **Career Support:** The university's career center provides career counseling, job search assistance, and internship opportunities for international students. They also organize workshops and seminars on Japanese business culture and job application skills.
- **International Student Advisors:** Dedicated advisors are available to assist international students with visa issues, academic advising, cultural adjustment, and other concerns.
- **Student Clubs and Organizations:** International students can participate in a wide range of student clubs and organizations, allowing them to connect with Japanese and other international students and pursue their interests.

## üìç Campus Location & Access

Rikkyo University has two main campuses:

- **Ikebukuro Campus:** This is the main campus and is located in the heart of Tokyo. It houses most of the undergraduate faculties.
- **Niiza Campus:** This campus is located in Saitama Prefecture and houses some of the undergraduate faculties and graduate schools. It is known for its more spacious and natural setting.
