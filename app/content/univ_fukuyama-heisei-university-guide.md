## 🏫 University Overview

Fukuyama Heisei University (FHU), established in 1994, is a forward-thinking private university located in Fukuyama City, Hiroshima Prefecture, Japan. Founded by the prestigious Fukuyama University Educational Corporation (Hakuyo Academy), the university was built upon a clear and noble mission: to foster highly skilled professionals who possess both practical expertise and a deep sense of humanity. The institution stands out for its strong emphasis on regional integration, preparing students to become central pillars of the local and global communities.

For international students, Fukuyama Heisei University offers a unique and highly supportive educational environment. Situated in the heart of the Chugoku region, Fukuyama is a prosperous industrial city renowned for its manufacturing, shipbuilding, and textile industries, while still retaining a peaceful, safe, and culturally rich atmosphere. Unlike the overcrowded and highly expensive metropolitan hubs of Tokyo or Osaka, Fukuyama provides an authentic immersion into Japanese lifestyle, language, and community. The campus itself is located in Kannabe-cho, featuring modern, state-of-the-art facilities, extensive green spaces, and high-tech specialized laboratories designed for practical learning.

FHU’s educational philosophy revolves around 'Autonomy, Creation, and Cooperation.' The university structures its curricula around real-world applications, encouraging students to actively participate in internships, local community projects, and research initiatives that directly solve societal challenges. This pragmatic approach makes FHU a highly appealing destination for ambitious international students seeking a direct path to employment in Japan.

## 🎓 Academic Programs & Faculties

Fukuyama Heisei University offers specialized undergraduate programs across two main faculties, each meticulously designed to meet the demands of the modern job market:

### 1. Faculty of Management
The Faculty of Management is the premier destination for international students wishing to launch a career in business, trade, IT, or public administration. The program focuses on teaching contemporary management strategies, digital literacy, and economic theories with a focus on local and global markets.
*   **Department of Business Administration:** Students can specialize in several key areas, including Marketing, Accounting, Information & Media Design, and Tourism Management. The curriculum is highly experiential; students regularly collaborate with local businesses in Hiroshima to develop marketing plans, analyze consumer behavior, and learn how to manage small-to-medium enterprises (SMEs). This hands-on training ensures that graduates are ready to contribute to any corporate environment from day one.

### 2. Faculty of Welfare and Health Science
This faculty is dedicated to raising professionals who can address the shifting health and societal needs of Japan's super-aged society. It consists of three highly specialized departments:
*   **Department of Social Welfare:** Focuses on the administrative, psychological, and practical elements of welfare services. Students learn about mental health counseling, elderly care systems, child welfare, and community development. Graduates are prepared for leadership roles in social welfare corporations, NPOs, and government offices.
*   **Department of Health and Sports Science:** Designed for students interested in sports management, fitness instruction, physical therapy preparation, and athletic training. The department is equipped with professional-grade athletic facilities, science labs, and gymnasiums, allowing students to conduct advanced research in human biomechanics and athletic performance.
*   **Department of Nursing:** A highly rigorous program focused on clinical nursing practice, medical technology, and healthcare ethics. International students who enroll in this department receive intensive training to pass the national nursing exam in Japan, unlocking high-paying, stable careers in hospitals and healthcare networks across the nation.

## 🌏 International Student Support & Campus Life

Transitioning to life in a new country can be challenging, which is why Fukuyama Heisei University has built a comprehensive support network tailored specifically for international students.

### Dedicated International Center
The International Center at FHU serves as the primary hub for overseas students. From the moment students arrive, the center assists with essential administrative tasks, such as visa renewals, national health insurance enrollment, setting up bank accounts, and registering at the local ward office. The friendly staff are bilingual and always ready to provide personal counseling for any academic or lifestyle concerns.

### Japanese Language & Cultural Support
To ensure academic success, FHU offers supplementary Japanese language courses. These classes are designed to help students transition smoothly from basic conversational Japanese to the advanced business-level Japanese required for university lectures and job interviews in Japan. Additionally, the university operates a 'Tutor System,' which pairs international students with Japanese student mentors. This program not only helps international students with their coursework and language practice but also fosters deep cross-cultural friendships on campus.

### Vibrant Campus Life & Activities
Life at FHU is rich with cultural exchange. Throughout the year, the university organizes various events, including foreign language speech contests, international exchange parties, and local field trips to historical landmarks in Hiroshima and the Seto Inland Sea. The annual university festival, 'Heisei-sai,' is a major highlight, where international students set up food stalls showcasing their native cuisines and participate in talent shows, bringing the entire campus together in celebration.

## 💰 Tuition Fees, Scholarships & Financial Aid

One of the most significant advantages of studying at Fukuyama Heisei University is its affordability. When compared to universities in Tokyo and Osaka, the cost of both tuition and daily living at FHU is remarkably manageable.

### Estimated Academic Costs
For most undergraduate departments, the first-year academic expenses are structured as follows:
*   **Admission Fee (One-time):** ¥200,000
*   **Yearly Tuition (Base):** Approximately ¥750,000 to ¥950,000 (varies by department)
*   **Facility & Lab Fees:** Approximately ¥100,000 to ¥150,000 per year

### Tuition Reduction Program for International Students
To ease the financial burden on self-financed international students, FHU offers an institutional **Tuition Reduction Program**. Eligible students who demonstrate financial need and maintain good academic standing can receive a **30% deduction** on their annual tuition fees. This makes the net tuition cost highly competitive.

### Scholarship Opportunities
Several prestigious scholarships are available to international students enrolled at FHU:
*   **JASSO Honors Scholarship:** Offered by the Japan Student Services Organization, providing selected students with a monthly stipend of ¥48,000 for one year.
*   **Local Government & Private Foundation Scholarships:** Local organizations in Hiroshima Prefecture, such as the Rotary Yoneyama Memorial Foundation and the Heiwa Nakajima Foundation, offer monthly stipends ranging from ¥30,000 to ¥100,000 to exemplary international students.
*   **FHU Merit Scholarships:** Internal academic awards presented annually to the top-performing students in each department, covering partial or full tuition costs.

## 🏡 Accommodation & Living in Fukuyama

Fukuyama City is exceptionally student-friendly, offering a incredibly low cost of living compared to Japan's major metropolises. 

### Housing Options
While FHU does not have a large on-campus residential dormitory exclusively for international students, the university's housing office collaborates with trusted local real estate agencies to secure safe, affordable, and fully furnished apartments near the campus. 
*   **Average Rent:** Monthly rent for a comfortable single apartment (equipped with a kitchen, bathroom, and air conditioning) ranges between **¥25,000 and ¥35,000**. In comparison, a similar apartment in Tokyo would cost upwards of ¥70,000 per month.
*   **Utility Costs:** Around ¥10,000 to ¥15,000 per month for electricity, gas, water, and high-speed internet.

### Living in Fukuyama City
Fukuyama is the second-largest city in Hiroshima Prefecture, home to about 460,000 residents. It is a scenic city famous for Fukuyama Castle and the historic port town of Tomonoura, which served as the visual inspiration for Studio Ghibli's film *Ponyo*. The city is exceptionally well-connected; it is a major stop on the Sanyo Shinkansen line, allowing students to travel to Hiroshima City in 20 minutes, Osaka in 1 hour, and Tokyo in under 4 hours. Daily grocery costs, dining out, and transportation are highly economical, allowing students to maintain a healthy and active lifestyle on a budget of approximately **¥60,000 to ¥80,000 per month** (including rent).

## 💼 Career Prospects & Employment Support

Fukuyama Heisei University boasts an outstanding employment rate, consistently exceeding 95% for its graduates. The university’s strong relationship with the industrial sector of Hiroshima and the neighboring Okayama Prefecture provides international students with unmatched career opportunities.

### The Career Center’s Personalized Approach
The Career Support Office at FHU does not believe in a one-size-fits-all approach. Knowing that international students face unique hurdles in the Japanese job market, the center provides tailored, one-on-one guidance, including:
*   **Resume and Entry Sheet (ES) Corrections:** Helping students frame their overseas backgrounds and bilingual skills as strong assets to Japanese recruiters.
*   **Mock Interview Practice:** Preparing students for the unique and structured Japanese corporate interview process, including polite business Japanese and proper etiquette.
*   **On-Campus Job Fairs:** Regularly inviting dozens of local and regional companies to the campus specifically to recruit FHU students.

### Industry Pathways
Graduates from the Faculty of Management frequently secure positions in global logistics, regional banking, trade companies, hospitality, IT services, and manufacturing companies in the Chugoku region. Graduates from the Faculty of Welfare and Health Science find stable, highly respected employment in medical corporations, elderly care facilities, rehabilitation centers, and sports business enterprises across Japan.

## 📝 Admission Guide for International Students

Applying to Fukuyama Heisei University requires careful preparation, especially regarding language proficiency and academic credentials.

### Eligibility Criteria
To apply as an international student, you must meet the following basic requirements:
1.  **Academic Background:** Completion of 12 years of formal primary and secondary education outside of Japan (or equivalent).
2.  **Language Proficiency:** Since all regular undergraduate courses are conducted in Japanese, applicants must demonstrate sufficient Japanese language ability. This is typically verified by:
    *   Passing **JLPT N2** or higher, OR
    *   Scoring **200 points or more** on the Japanese as a Foreign Language section of the EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students).

### Application & Selection Process
The admission process usually opens in the autumn/winter preceding the April enrollment. The selection process generally consists of three parts:
1.  **Document Screening:** Review of high school transcripts, recommendation letters, financial viability statements, and language certificates.
2.  **Written Examination:** A basic Japanese language test conducted on-campus (or at specified testing venues).
3.  **Interview:** An oral interview conducted in Japanese to evaluate the applicant’s academic motivation, language fluency, and future career plans.
