## üè´ University Overview

Doshisha University, located in the ancient capital of Kyoto, stands as one of Japan's most prestigious private universities. Founded in 1875 by the visionary educator Joseph Hardy Neesima (Niijima Jo), Doshisha was established on a commitment to Christian principles and a spirit of liberal arts education, aiming to cultivate individuals with a conscience who would contribute to society. Neesima's educational philosophy emphasized not just academic excellence but also the development of character, critical thinking, and a global perspective. This foundational ethos continues to shape Doshisha's academic environment, fostering a vibrant intellectual community where students are encouraged to pursue knowledge with integrity and compassion.

The university is widely recognized for its high academic standards, strong research output, and diverse student body. It is often counted among the 'Kankandoritsu' (Kansai Gakuin, Kwansei Gakuin, Doshisha, Ritsumeikan), a group of top private universities in the Kansai region. Doshisha's reputation extends beyond Japan, attracting students and scholars from around the world. Its alumni network is influential, with graduates making significant contributions in various fields, including business, politics, academia, and the arts. The university's commitment to internationalization is further demonstrated by its participation in the Japanese government's Top Global University Project, aiming to enhance its global competitiveness and academic exchange.

Doshisha offers a rich campus life, blending traditional Japanese culture with modern academic pursuits. Its historical Imadegawa Campus, nestled next to the Kyoto Imperial Palace, provides a serene and inspiring setting, while the Kyotanabe Campus offers state-of-the-art facilities for science, engineering, and sports. The university's motto, 'Sola fide, Sola gratia, Sola scriptura' (By faith alone, By grace alone, By scripture alone), reflects its Christian heritage, which underpins its dedication to holistic education and social responsibility.

## üåç International Student Support

Doshisha University is dedicated to fostering a diverse and inclusive environment, offering robust support services for its international student community. This commitment aligns with its mission to cultivate global citizens.

*   **Dormitory Accommodation**: Doshisha provides dedicated international student dormitories, such as the Doshisha International House (Kyotanabe and Shin-machi Campuses), offering a safe and supportive living environment. These dormitories facilitate cultural exchange and integration into university life.
*   **English-Taught Programs**: The Institute for the Liberal Arts (ILA) offers a full undergraduate degree program taught entirely in English, attracting students from around the globe. Additionally, several graduate programs and specific courses are available in English across various faculties, expanding opportunities for non-Japanese speaking students.
*   **Japanese Language Education**: For students seeking to improve their Japanese proficiency, Doshisha offers comprehensive Japanese language courses at various levels, from beginner to advanced. These courses are designed to help international students adapt to daily life in Japan and excel academically.
*   **Career Support**: The Career Center provides specialized support for international students, including guidance on job searching in Japan, resume writing, interview preparation, and information on visa requirements for employment. They organize career fairs and networking events to connect students with potential employers.
*   **Scholarships**: Doshisha University offers various scholarships specifically for international students, including the Doshisha University International Student Scholarship, which provides tuition fee reductions or stipends. Information on external scholarships from government bodies and private foundations is also readily available.
*   **Counseling and Advising**: The International Center offers academic and personal counseling services, providing support for any challenges international students might face during their studies or daily life in Japan. Regular orientation sessions and cultural events are also organized to help students settle in and build a community.
