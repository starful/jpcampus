## University Overview

Kwansei Gakuin University (KGU), often known simply as "Kwansei," is a distinguished private, co-educational university celebrated for its strong academic heritage and dedication to developing global citizens. Established in 1889 by American missionary Walter R. Lambuth, a bishop of the Methodist Episcopal Church, South, KGU holds a prominent position among Japan's private universities, particularly in the Kansai region. It is often grouped with Doshisha, Ritsumeikan, and Kansai University as one of the "Kankandoritsu" – the four leading private universities in Kansai. Its founding philosophy, "Mastery for Service," emphasizes cultivating individuals who not only excel academically but also contribute meaningfully to society.

KGU is recognized for its beautiful historical campuses, most notably the Nishinomiya Uegahara Campus, which boasts a distinctive Spanish Mission-style architecture. This aesthetic contributes to its reputation as one of Japan's most picturesque university settings. Over its extensive history, Kwansei Gakuin has evolved from a small theological seminary and academy into a comprehensive university offering a wide array of undergraduate and graduate programs across diverse disciplines, including humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, and professional studies.

The university consistently achieves high rankings in various surveys for academic reputation, student satisfaction, and career outcomes. Its strong emphasis on internationalization is evident in its diverse student body, extensive study abroad programs, and an increasing number of English-taught courses and degrees designed to attract and support international students. While KGU is not formally designated as a "Top Global University" by the Japanese government, it has independently pursued ambitious internationalization strategies, establishing partnerships with over 200 universities worldwide and actively promoting cross-cultural understanding and global leadership. The university's alumni network is vast and influential, with graduates holding key positions in various sectors, from business and politics to education and the arts, both in Japan and internationally. KGU's commitment to holistic education, combining academic rigor with character development and a global outlook, remains a cornerstone of its mission.

Kwansei Gakuin University provides a wide spectrum of academic disciplines through its 14 undergraduate faculties, offering students diverse educational pathways. Each faculty is dedicated to fostering in-depth knowledge and critical thinking within its specialized field, preparing graduates for a wide range of professional and academic pursuits. The university regularly reviews and updates its faculty structures to adapt to evolving societal needs and academic trends, as demonstrated by recent expansions and reorganizations in its science and technology-related fields.

## International Student Support

Kwansei Gakuin University is deeply committed to fostering a vibrant and supportive environment for its international student community. Recognizing the unique challenges and opportunities that come with studying abroad, KGU provides comprehensive services designed to ensure a smooth transition and successful academic journey for students from around the world.

**English-Taught Programs:** KGU offers several programs conducted entirely in English, making it accessible to students who may not be proficient in Japanese. Notable programs include:

*   **School of International Studies (SILS):** Offers the "Contemporary Japan Program" and other courses providing a deep understanding of Japanese society, culture, and international relations from a global perspective.
*   **Undergraduate and Graduate Programs:** Various departments and graduate schools offer courses and degrees where English is the primary language of instruction, particularly in fields like international studies, business, and science.
*   **Exchange Programs:** KGU hosts a significant number of short-term and long-term exchange students through its partnerships with universities worldwide, many of whom study in English.

**Dormitory & Accommodation:** KGU offers various housing options tailored for international students, ensuring comfort and convenience.

*   **International Student Dormitories:** The university manages several dormitories specifically for international students, such as the Kwansei Gakuin University International Residence, which provides a safe, multicultural living environment. These dormitories are typically furnished and offer common spaces for interaction.
*   **KGU Residences:** Other university-managed dormitories are also available for both Japanese and international students, fostering integration.
*   **Housing Support:** The International Affairs Office provides guidance and support for students seeking off-campus accommodation, including information on private apartments and homestays.

**Career Support:** KGU places a strong emphasis on preparing its graduates for successful careers, both in Japan and internationally.

*   **Career Center:** The dedicated Career Center offers a wide range of services, including career counseling, resume/CV workshops (in English and Japanese), interview preparation, and job search strategies.
*   **International Career Support:** Specific workshops and seminars are organized for international students, addressing the nuances of job hunting in Japan, visa requirements for employment, and opportunities for global careers.
*   **Internship Opportunities:** The university facilitates internship placements with various companies and organizations, allowing students to gain practical work experience.
*   **Alumni Network:** KGU's extensive and influential alumni network provides valuable connections and mentorship opportunities, particularly for those seeking employment in Japan.

Beyond these core services, the International Affairs Office acts as a central hub for international students, offering support with immigration procedures, cultural adjustment, health and wellness, and daily life advice. Regular cultural events and support groups are also organized to help students integrate into university life and the broader Japanese society.

## Campus Location & Access

Kwansei Gakuin University boasts multiple distinct campuses, each offering a unique academic and living environment. The primary campuses are Nishinomiya Uegahara, Nishinomiya Seiwa, and Kobe Sanda, with additional facilities in Osaka Umeda and Takarazuka. Each campus is well-connected by public transportation, making them accessible from major cities in the Kansai region.

**Nishinomiya Uegahara Campus (Main Campus):** This is the flagship campus, known for its iconic Spanish Mission-style architecture and lush greenery. It houses most of the humanities, social sciences, and some science faculties.

**Nishinomiya Seiwa Campus:** Located near the main campus, this campus is home to the School of Education's facilities, focusing on teacher training.

**Kobe Sanda Campus (KSC):** A modern campus located in a thriving academic research city, KSC is home to the Schools of Policy Studies, Science and Technology, Bioscience and Chemistry, and Informatics, fostering innovation and interdisciplinary research.

**Osaka Umeda Campus:** A satellite campus in the heart of Osaka's business district, primarily used for graduate programs, continuing education, and career support for students commuting from Osaka.

**Takarazuka Campus:** Focuses on various aspects related to disaster and environmental studies, often linked with the School of Policy Studies.
