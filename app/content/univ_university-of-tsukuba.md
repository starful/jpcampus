## üè´ University Overview

The University of Tsukuba, established in 1973, is a leading comprehensive national university located in Tsukuba Science City, Ibaraki Prefecture, Japan. While its official establishment was in 1973, its roots trace back to the Tokyo Higher Normal School, founded in 1872, making it one of Japan's oldest institutions of higher education. The university was reformed and relocated to Tsukuba as part of a national project to decentralize educational and research institutions from Tokyo and foster scientific advancement.

Tsukuba is renowned for its strong emphasis on research and innovation, boasting a high number of faculty members and research centers dedicated to various scientific disciplines. As a designated Top Global University (SGU) by the Japanese government, the University of Tsukuba is committed to enhancing its international competitiveness and attracting top researchers and students from around the world. The university's academic strength spans across various fields, including physics, chemistry, biology, engineering, and medicine. It has produced numerous notable alumni who have made significant contributions to science, technology, and society.

The University of Tsukuba plays a crucial role in the Tsukuba Science City, collaborating with other research institutions and industries to drive technological advancements and contribute to Japan's economic growth. It actively promotes international collaboration through partnerships with universities and research institutions globally, fostering academic exchange and joint research projects.

The university's educational philosophy focuses on cultivating well-rounded individuals with strong critical thinking skills and a global perspective. It offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs taught in both Japanese and English, catering to a diverse student body. The campus provides a vibrant and supportive learning environment with state-of-the-art facilities, including libraries, laboratories, and sports facilities.

## üåç International Student Support

The University of Tsukuba provides comprehensive support for international students, including:

*   **Dormitory:** On-campus housing is available for international students, offering affordable accommodation and opportunities for cultural exchange.
*   **English Programs:** A variety of courses and programs are offered in English, catering to students who may not be proficient in Japanese.
*   **Career Support:** The university provides career counseling and job placement assistance to help international students find employment after graduation.
*   **International Student Advising:** Dedicated advisors are available to assist international students with visa matters, academic guidance, and other personal needs.
*   **Japanese Language Courses:** Intensive Japanese language courses are offered to help international students improve their language skills and integrate into Japanese society.
*   **Cultural Exchange Programs:** The university organizes various cultural events and activities to promote intercultural understanding and friendship between international and Japanese students.
