## üè´ University Overview & Reputation

Hitotsubashi University, nestled in the tranquil city of Kunitachi, Tokyo, stands as a beacon of academic excellence, consistently ranked among Japan's most prestigious institutions, particularly in the realm of social sciences. While not typically categorized within the same group as the former Imperial Universities like Tokyo or Kyoto University, Hitotsubashi occupies a unique and highly respected niche, often considered the pinnacle for students aspiring to careers in business, economics, and law. Its reputation is comparable to the London School of Economics in the UK or Wharton School of Business in the US, albeit with a distinctly Japanese academic culture.

Founded in 1875 by Arinori Mori as the Shosho Koshujo, Hitotsubashi's origins lie in the pressing need to modernize Japanese commerce and equip the nation with skilled business leaders. This initial focus on practical training laid the foundation for its continued emphasis on real-world application and analytical rigor. Over the years, it evolved from the Tokyo Higher Commercial School into the Hitotsubashi University we know today, officially adopting its current name in 1949. The university's historical roots in commerce continue to shape its identity, fostering a strong connection to the business community and a commitment to producing graduates who are not only academically sound but also exceptionally well-prepared for leadership roles in various sectors.

Hitotsubashi University's alumni network is a testament to its enduring influence. Graduates hold prominent positions in major Japanese corporations, government ministries, and international organizations. This impressive track record contributes significantly to the university's prestige and attractiveness to prospective students, both domestic and international. The university's small size compared to other top-tier Japanese universities fosters a close-knit community and personalized learning environment, allowing for more direct interaction between students and faculty. This intimacy promotes a vibrant intellectual atmosphere where critical thinking, innovative problem-solving, and cross-disciplinary collaboration are highly valued. Hitotsubashi's reputation extends beyond Japan, attracting students and researchers from around the globe seeking a world-class education in the social sciences.

One of Hitotsubashi University's flagship programs, particularly attractive to international students, is its graduate program in **International Business Strategy (IBS)** within the School of Commerce. This program is offered entirely in English and is designed to equip students with the knowledge and skills necessary to navigate the complexities of the global business environment. The IBS program attracts a diverse cohort of students from around the world, fostering a truly international learning environment. The curriculum covers a wide range of topics, including international finance, marketing, strategic management, and cross-cultural communication. Students have the opportunity to participate in case studies, simulations, and internships, providing them with practical experience and valuable networking opportunities. The faculty consists of leading academics and experienced business professionals, ensuring that students receive a cutting-edge education that is both theoretically rigorous and practically relevant.

Another highly regarded program is the **Economics program** within the School of Economics. While undergraduate courses are primarily offered in Japanese, graduate-level programs provide options for English-taught courses, especially in specialized fields. The program emphasizes rigorous analytical training and provides students with a solid foundation in economic theory, econometrics, and quantitative methods. Students have the opportunity to specialize in various areas of economics, such as development economics, international economics, and public finance. The faculty members are actively engaged in cutting-edge research and are committed to providing students with a high-quality education. The program attracts students who are passionate about economics and are seeking to pursue careers in academia, government, or the private sector. The strong quantitative skills developed in this program are highly valued by employers worldwide.

## üåç International Student Admissions & Life

Admission to Hitotsubashi University for international students typically involves a multi-stage application process. For undergraduate programs, the **Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU)** is generally required, alongside standardized English proficiency tests like **TOEFL iBT** or **IELTS Academic**. Specific score requirements vary by department but generally, a TOEFL iBT score of 80 or IELTS Academic score of 6.0 is considered competitive. Furthermore, applicants are often required to submit academic transcripts, letters of recommendation, and a statement of purpose outlining their academic goals and research interests. Some departments may also conduct interviews, either in person or via video conference, to assess the applicant's motivation, communication skills, and suitability for the program. Graduate programs may have specific requirements, such as GRE scores for some disciplines. Careful review of the program's specific admission guidelines is crucial.

Several degree programs are offered entirely in English, primarily at the graduate level. These include the aforementioned **International Business Strategy (IBS) program** within the School of Commerce and certain specialized tracks within the **Economics program**. Prospective students should consult the university's official website for the most up-to-date information on English-taught programs, as offerings may change. The availability of these programs is a significant draw for international students who may not yet have sufficient Japanese language proficiency to pursue courses taught in Japanese.

Hitotsubashi University's **International Student Office** plays a vital role in supporting international students throughout their academic journey. The office provides a range of services, including assistance with visa applications, housing arrangements, and health insurance. They also offer academic advising and counseling services to help students navigate the academic system and address any personal challenges they may face. Additionally, the International Student Office organizes cultural events and activities to promote cross-cultural understanding and integration. Visa support is a crucial service, providing guidance on the often-complex procedures required to obtain and maintain a student visa in Japan.

The university boasts a vibrant international student community, with approximately **300 international students** enrolled from various countries. A wide array of student clubs and organizations caters to diverse interests, providing opportunities for international students to connect with Japanese students and fellow international students. These clubs range from sports and cultural activities to academic and professional organizations. The campus atmosphere is generally welcoming and inclusive, fostering a sense of community among students from different backgrounds. While Japanese language proficiency is certainly beneficial for daily life, many students find that they can thrive at Hitotsubashi even with limited Japanese skills, particularly if they are enrolled in an English-taught program. Active participation in extracurricular activities and engagement with the local community can further enhance the international student experience.

Several scholarships are available to support international students pursuing their studies at Hitotsubashi University. The **Japanese Government (MEXT) Scholarship** is one of the most prestigious and competitive scholarships available. It provides full tuition coverage, a monthly stipend, and round-trip airfare to Japan. The application process for the MEXT scholarship is typically conducted through the Japanese embassy or consulate in the applicant's home country. Hitotsubashi University also offers its own scholarships specifically for international students, such as the **Hitotsubashi University Scholarship for International Students**. These scholarships provide varying levels of financial support, including tuition waivers and stipends. In addition to government and university-sponsored scholarships, various private foundations offer scholarships to international students studying in Japan. Some examples include the **Rotary Yoneyama Memorial Foundation Scholarship** and the **Kyoritsu International Foundation Scholarship**. It is advisable to research and apply for multiple scholarships to increase the chances of receiving financial assistance.

The cost of living in Kunitachi, Tokyo, is generally lower than in central Tokyo. A rough monthly budget estimate for an international student would be around **80,000 - 120,000 JPY**. This includes accommodation (dormitory or apartment), food, transportation, utilities, and personal expenses. Dormitory accommodation is typically more affordable than renting an apartment. Food costs can be minimized by cooking at home and taking advantage of student discounts at local restaurants. Transportation costs can be reduced by using public transportation and purchasing a student commuter pass. Careful budgeting and planning are essential for managing expenses and ensuring a comfortable student life. Part-time work opportunities are also available for international students, but there are restrictions on the number of hours that can be worked per week.

## üìç Campus Location & Access

| Location          | Access                                                                               |
|-------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

Hitotsubashi University's main campus, the Kunitachi Campus, is situated in the charming city of Kunitachi, a western suburb of Tokyo known for its serene atmosphere and intellectual vibe. Kunitachi boasts tree-lined streets, numerous cafes, and bookstores, creating an ideal environment for focused study and relaxation. The neighborhood exudes a strong sense of community, with local residents often engaging with students and contributing to a welcoming atmosphere. The campus itself is characterized by its lush greenery, traditional architecture, and a tranquil ambiance that distinguishes it from the bustling city center.

The Kunitachi Campus is easily accessible from major transportation hubs in Tokyo, making it a convenient location for students from all over the metropolitan area. The most direct route is via the **JR Chuo Line** to **Kunitachi Station**. From Shinjuku Station, for example, the journey takes approximately 30-40 minutes on a rapid train. Upon arriving at Kunitachi Station, the campus is a pleasant **10-minute walk** along a tree-lined avenue. The walk is straightforward and well-signposted. From Tokyo Station, you can take the JR Chuo Line (Rapid) directly to Kunitachi Station. The journey will take around 50 minutes. The West Campus, housing some graduate schools and research facilities, is located a short walk from the main campus and easily accessible by foot or bicycle.
