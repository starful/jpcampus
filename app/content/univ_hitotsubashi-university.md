## University Overview & Reputation

Hitotsubashi University, situated in the serene city of Kunitachi, Tokyo, is a renowned institution consistently ranked among Japan's top universities, particularly in the social sciences. While not typically grouped with former Imperial Universities like Tokyo or Kyoto University, Hitotsubashi holds a unique and highly respected position, often considered the ideal choice for students aiming for careers in business, economics, and law. Its reputation is akin to the London School of Economics in the UK or the Wharton School of Business in the US, though with a distinctly Japanese academic atmosphere.

Founded in 1875 by Arinori Mori as the Shosho Koshujo, Hitotsubashi originated from the urgent need to modernize Japanese commerce and cultivate skilled business leaders. This initial focus on practical training has shaped its continued emphasis on real-world application and analytical rigor. Over time, it evolved from the Tokyo Higher Commercial School into the Hitotsubashi University of today, officially adopting its current name in 1949. The university's historical roots in commerce continue to define its identity, fostering a strong connection to the business community and a commitment to producing graduates who are academically strong and exceptionally prepared for leadership roles.

Hitotsubashi University's alumni network demonstrates its enduring influence. Graduates hold prominent positions in major Japanese corporations, government ministries, and international organizations. This impressive track record significantly contributes to the university's prestige and attractiveness to prospective students, both domestic and international. Its smaller size, compared to other top-tier Japanese universities, fosters a close-knit community and a personalized learning environment, facilitating more direct interaction between students and faculty. This intimacy cultivates a vibrant intellectual atmosphere where critical thinking, innovative problem-solving, and cross-disciplinary collaboration are highly valued. Hitotsubashi's reputation extends beyond Japan, attracting students and researchers worldwide seeking a world-class education in the social sciences.

One of Hitotsubashi University's flagship programs, especially appealing to international students, is its graduate program in **International Business Strategy (IBS)** within the School of Commerce. This program is entirely in English and equips students with the knowledge and skills to navigate the complexities of the global business environment. The IBS program attracts a diverse cohort of students from around the world, fostering a truly international learning environment. The curriculum covers international finance, marketing, strategic management, and cross-cultural communication. Another highly regarded program is the **Economics program** within the School of Economics. While undergraduate courses are primarily in Japanese, graduate-level programs offer English-taught options, especially in specialized fields. The program emphasizes rigorous analytical training and provides a solid foundation in economic theory, econometrics, and quantitative methods. Students can specialize in areas such as development economics, international economics, and public finance. The faculty members are actively engaged in research and committed to providing a high-quality education. The program attracts students passionate about economics and seeking careers in academia, government, or the private sector. The strong quantitative skills developed here are highly valued by employers worldwide.

## International Student Admissions & Life

Admission to Hitotsubashi University for international students typically involves a multi-stage application process. For undergraduate programs, the **Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU)** is generally required, along with standardized English proficiency tests like **TOEFL iBT** or **IELTS Academic**. Specific score requirements vary by department, but a TOEFL iBT score of 80 or IELTS Academic score of 6.0 is generally considered competitive. Applicants often need to submit academic transcripts, letters of recommendation, and a statement of purpose outlining their academic goals and research interests. Some departments may also conduct interviews, in person or via video conference, to assess motivation, communication skills, and program suitability. Graduate programs may have specific requirements, such as GRE scores for some disciplines. Careful review of the program's specific admission guidelines is crucial.

Several degree programs are offered entirely in English, primarily at the graduate level. These include the **International Business Strategy (IBS) program** within the School of Commerce and certain specialized tracks within the **Economics program**. Prospective students should consult the university's official website for the most current information on English-taught programs, as offerings may change. The availability of these programs significantly attracts international students who may not yet have sufficient Japanese language proficiency to pursue courses taught in Japanese.

Hitotsubashi University's **International Student Office** plays a vital role in supporting international students throughout their academic journey. The office provides services including assistance with visa applications, housing arrangements, and health insurance. They also offer academic advising and counseling to help students navigate the academic system and address personal challenges. Additionally, the International Student Office organizes cultural events and activities to promote cross-cultural understanding and integration. Visa support is a crucial service, providing guidance on the often-complex procedures for obtaining and maintaining a student visa in Japan.

The university has a vibrant international student community, with approximately **300 international students** enrolled from various countries. A wide array of student clubs and organizations caters to diverse interests, providing opportunities for international students to connect with Japanese students and fellow international students. These clubs range from sports and cultural activities to academic and professional organizations. The campus atmosphere is generally welcoming and inclusive, fostering a sense of community among students from different backgrounds. While Japanese language proficiency is certainly beneficial for daily life, many students find they can thrive at Hitotsubashi even with limited Japanese skills, particularly if enrolled in an English-taught program. Active participation in extracurricular activities and engagement with the local community can further enhance the international student experience.

The cost of studying at Hitotsubashi University is relatively affordable compared to universities in other developed countries. The table above provides a breakdown of the average first-year costs. These figures are approximate and may vary depending on the student's individual circumstances. The "Other Fees" category includes costs such as student association fees and insurance.

Several scholarships are available to support international students pursuing their studies at Hitotsubashi University. The **Japanese Government (MEXT) Scholarship** is one of the most prestigious and competitive scholarships available. It provides full tuition coverage, a monthly stipend, and round-trip airfare to Japan. The application process for the MEXT scholarship is typically conducted through the Japanese embassy or consulate in the applicant's home country. Hitotsubashi University also offers its own scholarships specifically for international students, such as the **Hitotsubashi University Scholarship for International Students**. These scholarships provide varying levels of financial support, including tuition waivers and stipends. In addition to government and university-sponsored scholarships, various private foundations offer scholarships to international students studying in Japan. Some examples include the **Rotary Yoneyama Memorial Foundation Scholarship** and the **Kyoritsu International Foundation Scholarship**. It is advisable to research and apply for multiple scholarships to increase the chances of receiving financial assistance.

The cost of living in Kunitachi, Tokyo, is generally lower than in central Tokyo. A rough monthly budget estimate for an international student would be around **80,000 - 120,000 JPY**. This includes accommodation (dormitory or apartment), food, transportation, utilities, and personal expenses. Dormitory accommodation is typically more affordable than renting an apartment. Food costs can be minimized by cooking at home and taking advantage of student discounts at local restaurants. Transportation costs can be reduced by using public transportation and purchasing a student commuter pass. Careful budgeting and planning are essential for managing expenses and ensuring a comfortable student life. Part-time work opportunities are also available for international students, but there are restrictions on the number of hours that can be worked per week.

## Campus Location & Access

The following table outlines location and access information:

| Location          | Access                                                                               |
|-------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Kunitachi Campus  | 10-minute walk from Kunitachi Station on the JR Chuo Line                             |
| West Campus        | Located a short walk from the main campus, easily accessible.                      |

Hitotsubashi University's main campus, the Kunitachi Campus, is located in the charming city of Kunitachi, a western suburb of Tokyo known for its serene atmosphere and intellectual vibe. Kunitachi boasts tree-lined streets, numerous cafes, and bookstores, creating an ideal environment for focused study and relaxation. The neighborhood exudes a strong sense of community, with local residents often engaging with students and contributing to a welcoming atmosphere. The campus itself is characterized by its lush greenery, traditional architecture, and a tranquil ambiance that distinguishes it from the bustling city center.

The Kunitachi Campus is easily accessible from major transportation hubs in Tokyo, making it a convenient location for students from all over the metropolitan area. The most direct route is via the **JR Chuo Line** to **Kunitachi Station**. From Shinjuku Station, for example, the journey takes approximately 30-40 minutes on a rapid train. Upon arriving at Kunitachi Station, the campus is a pleasant **10-minute walk** along a tree-lined avenue. The walk is straightforward and well-signposted. From Tokyo Station, you can take the JR Chuo Line (Rapid) directly to Kunitachi Station. The journey will take around 50 minutes. The West Campus, housing some graduate schools and research facilities, is located a short walk from the main campus and easily accessible by foot or bicycle.
