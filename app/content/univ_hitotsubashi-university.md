## üè´ University Overview

Hitotsubashi University, located in Kunitachi, Tokyo, is one of Japan's most prestigious universities, renowned for its specialization in the social sciences. Founded in 1875 as the Shosho Koshujo (Institute for Business Training) by Arinori Mori, it was initially established to modernize Japanese commerce and business education. It later evolved into the Tokyo Higher Commercial School and finally became Hitotsubashi University in 1949.

Hitotsubashi University has a long-standing reputation for producing leaders in the fields of business, economics, law, and social sciences. The university has consistently ranked among the top universities in Japan and Asia, and its graduates are highly sought after by major corporations and government agencies. The university's commitment to fostering critical thinking, analytical skills, and a global perspective makes its graduates well-prepared for success in a rapidly changing world.

Hitotsubashi is a relatively small university, which contributes to its close-knit community and personalized learning environment. It maintains a strong emphasis on small-class sizes and interactive teaching methods, fostering a vibrant intellectual atmosphere. The university's campus, located in a quiet residential area, provides a conducive environment for study and research.

## üåç International Student Support

Hitotsubashi University actively supports international students through various programs and services. The university provides on-campus dormitory accommodation for international students, which offers a convenient and affordable housing option. The International Affairs Office provides comprehensive support to international students, including assistance with visa applications, housing arrangements, and cultural adjustment.

English-taught programs are available at both the undergraduate and graduate levels, catering to students who may not have sufficient Japanese language proficiency. The university also offers Japanese language courses to help international students improve their language skills. Career support services are available to assist international students in finding internships and job opportunities in Japan and abroad.
