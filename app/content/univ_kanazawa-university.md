## University Overview & Reputation

Kanazawa University, located in Ishikawa Prefecture, is a distinguished national university with a long history and a strong dedication to academic excellence. While not one of the original 'Imperial Universities,' it's considered a top-tier national university in Japan, often ranking within the top 15-20. It holds a vital position in the Hokuriku region's academic and cultural landscape.

*   Founded in 1862 as a medical school, Kanazawa University has grown into a comprehensive university with diverse academic disciplines.
*   The university's core values emphasize contributing to society, both regionally and globally, through education and research.
*   It has a solid academic reputation, particularly in medicine, pharmaceutical sciences, and engineering.
*   Kanazawa University is recognized for its research output and fostering innovation and creative thinking among its students.
*   Designated as a 'Super Global University' by the Japanese government, reflecting its commitment to internationalization.

Kanazawa University is positively perceived and considered an accessible and welcoming institution. It actively promotes international exchange and collaboration, attracting students and researchers worldwide. Its location in Kanazawa, a city celebrated for its historical charm and cultural heritage, is a significant attraction, providing students the chance to experience traditional Japanese culture alongside their studies. The University is respected in the region as a driver of technological and economic advancement.

*   The university's ranking places it firmly among the best national universities in Japan.
*   It is a competitive option for domestic and international students seeking high-quality education and research.
*   Its commitment to globalization and strategic location makes it an attractive study destination in Japan.

## Faculties & Signature Programs

**Faculty Breakdown:**

*   **College of Human and Social Sciences:** Offers a broad range of humanities and social science disciplines, including Departments of Human Sciences, Regional Creation Studies, and International Studies.
*   **College of Law:** Focuses on legal studies and jurisprudence, offering a Department of Law.
*   **College of Economics:** Emphasizes economic theory and analysis, with a Department of Economics.
*   **College of Science and Engineering:** Strong in science and engineering with Departments of Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Mechanical Engineering, Electrical Engineering, and more. English programs are available.
*   **College of Medicine:** Renowned for its medical research and education, offering a Department of Medicine.
*   **College of Pharmacy:** Focuses on pharmaceutical studies and research with Departments of Pharmacy and Pharmaceutical Sciences.
*   **College of Health Sciences:** Focuses on health-related professions, including Departments of Nursing and Medical Technology.

**Signature Programs:**

*   **Frontier Science Initiative (FSI):** Primarily in the College of Science and Engineering, this program aims to develop future leaders in science and technology. It emphasizes interdisciplinary research and offers opportunities to engage in cutting-edge projects, fostering creativity and innovation. Research opportunities for international students and courses in English are often available.
*   **Medical Science (English Program):** The College of Medicine offers a comprehensive medical program with some course content delivered in English. This program attracts international students seeking a globally recognized medical degree, focusing on clinical training, research opportunities, and international collaboration, preparing graduates for global careers in medicine and related fields.
*   **Japan-EU Joint Master's Programme in Global Peace, Security and Strategic Studies (GLOSS):** A unique joint master's program with European universities, focusing on the interdisciplinary study of peace, security, and strategic studies with a global perspective. It offers a blend of theoretical knowledge and practical skills, preparing students for careers in international organizations, government, and academia, drawing students in with its international perspective and providing cross-cultural learning opportunities.

## International Student Admissions & Life

**Admissions Process:**

*   The application process for international students typically involves several steps.
*   Applicants must meet eligibility requirements, including holding a high school diploma or equivalent.
*   The Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU) is generally required, though some programs might waive it based on other qualifications.
*   Proof of English proficiency is essential, usually through TOEFL or IELTS scores (some programs might accept other tests).
*   Successful candidates are usually invited for an interview, conducted in person or via video conference.
*   Detailed information is available on the university's international admissions website.

**English-Taught Programs:**

Kanazawa University offers several degree programs entirely in English, primarily at the graduate level, including:

*   **Graduate School of Natural Science and Technology:** Offers Master's and Doctoral programs in fields like Mechanical Science, Electrical and Computer Engineering, Environmental Design, and Materials Chemistry, designed for students with a strong background in science and engineering.
*   **Graduate School of Medical Sciences:** Offers research-focused programs with instruction and research opportunities in English.
*   **Japan-EU Joint Master's Programme in Global Peace, Security and Strategic Studies (GLOSS):** Taught entirely in English.

**Student Support:**

*   Kanazawa University provides comprehensive support services for international students.
*   The International Student Affairs Office offers assistance with visa applications, housing, and general orientation to life in Japan.
*   Academic advisors provide guidance on course selection and academic progress.
*   Counseling services are available to address personal or academic challenges.
*   Japanese language courses are offered to improve communication skills and cultural integration.

**Campus Life & Diversity:**

*   Kanazawa University has a diverse student body with many international students.
*   This creates a vibrant and multicultural campus atmosphere.
*   A wide range of student clubs and organizations cater to various interests.
*   These clubs provide opportunities for international students to interact with Japanese students and participate in campus life.
*   The university actively promotes intercultural exchange and understanding through various events and programs.
*   University dormitories support cultural exchange for international students.

## Tuition, Scholarships & Cost of Living

**Detailed Cost Breakdown:**

*   Admission Fee: 282,000 JPY
*   Yearly Tuition: 535,800 JPY
*   Examination Fee: 17,000 JPY

*Note: These are standard fees. Science and engineering faculties may have additional costs for laboratory fees or equipment. Always check the specific program's fee structure.*

**Scholarship Opportunities:**

Kanazawa University offers various scholarships for international students:

*   **Japanese Government (MEXT) Scholarship:** A highly competitive scholarship covering tuition fees, providing a monthly stipend, and covering travel expenses.
*   **Kanazawa University Scholarship for Privately Financed International Students:** Provides financial assistance to privately funded international students who demonstrate academic excellence and financial need.
*   **JASSO Scholarship:** Offered by the Japan Student Services Organization, providing a monthly stipend.
*   **Private Foundation Scholarships:** Various private foundations offer scholarships with specific eligibility requirements and application procedures.

**Cost of Living:**

*   The cost of living in Kanazawa is generally lower than in major metropolitan areas.
*   Rent for a single apartment near the university ranges from 40,000 to 60,000 JPY per month.
*   Food expenses can be estimated at around 30,000 to 40,000 JPY per month.
*   Transportation costs will vary, but a monthly budget of 10,000 to 20,000 JPY should be sufficient.
*   Overall, a monthly budget of 80,000 to 120,000 JPY should cover basic living expenses.

## Campus Location & Access

**Main Campus Vibe:**

*   Kanazawa University's main campus, Kakuma Campus, is located in the outskirts of Kanazawa City.
*   It provides a peaceful and spacious learning environment surrounded by nature.
*   The campus is well-connected to the city via public transportation.
*   The surrounding neighborhood is primarily residential, with student apartments, local shops, and restaurants.

**Access Information:**

*   **From Kanazawa Station:** Take the Hokutetsu bus (bound for Kanazawa Daigaku) from bus stop #6 (30-40 min)
*   **From Komatsu Airport:** Take the airport bus to Kanazawa Station, then the Hokutetsu bus (60-70 min)
*   **From Higashi Chaya District:** Take the Kanazawa Loop Bus to Kanazawa Station, then the Hokutetsu bus (50-60 min)
