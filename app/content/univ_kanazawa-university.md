## üè´ University Overview & Reputation

Kanazawa University, situated in Ishikawa Prefecture, stands as a prominent national university with a rich history and a strong commitment to academic excellence. While not belonging to the 'Imperial Universities' group, it is considered one of the leading national universities in Japan, often placed within the top 15-20 nationally. It plays a significant role in the Hokuriku region's academic and cultural landscape. Founded in 1862 as a medical school, Kanazawa University has evolved into a comprehensive university encompassing a wide range of disciplines.

The university's founding principles emphasize contributing to regional and global society through education and research. It maintains a strong academic reputation, particularly in fields like medicine, pharmaceutical sciences, and engineering. Kanazawa University is known for its research output and its commitment to fostering innovative and creative thinking among its students. It is designated as a 'Super Global University' by the Japanese government, reflecting its dedication to internationalization and global engagement.

Kanazawa University enjoys a positive social reputation, being regarded as an accessible and welcoming institution. It actively promotes international exchange and collaboration, attracting students and researchers from around the world. The university's location in Kanazawa, a city known for its historical charm and cultural heritage, adds to its appeal. This provides students with a unique opportunity to experience traditional Japanese culture alongside their academic pursuits. The University is well respected within the region as a driver of technological and economic growth.

Its ranking tier firmly places it among the top national universities in Japan. This makes it a competitive choice for both domestic and international students seeking high-quality education and research opportunities. Its commitment to globalization and its strategic location make it an attractive destination for those wishing to study in Japan.

**Signature Programs:**

**1. Frontier Science Initiative (FSI):** This program, primarily within the College of Science and Engineering, is designed to cultivate future leaders in science and technology. It emphasizes interdisciplinary research and provides students with opportunities to engage in cutting-edge projects. What makes it stand out is its focus on fostering creativity and innovation. The program often has research opportunities for international students and offers courses in English.

**2. Medical Science (English Program):** Kanazawa University's College of Medicine offers a comprehensive medical program that has some course content delivered in English. This program attracts international students seeking a globally recognized medical degree. Its strengths lie in its strong clinical training, research opportunities, and its emphasis on international collaboration. Graduates are well-prepared for careers in medicine and related fields globally.

**3. Japan-EU Joint Master's Programme in Global Peace, Security and Strategic Studies (GLOSS):** This is a unique joint master's program offered in collaboration with European universities. This program focuses on the interdisciplinary study of peace, security, and strategic studies, with a strong emphasis on global perspectives. The program offers a unique blend of theoretical knowledge and practical skills, preparing students for careers in international organizations, government, and academia. The international aspect is a major draw, providing students with cross-cultural learning opportunities.

## üåç International Student Admissions & Life

**Admissions Process:** The application process for international students typically involves several steps. Firstly, applicants must meet the eligibility requirements, including holding a high school diploma or equivalent. Secondly, the submission of the Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU) is generally required, though some programs might waive it based on other qualifications. Thirdly, proof of English proficiency is essential, usually through TOEFL or IELTS scores. Some programs might accept other English proficiency tests. Finally, successful candidates will usually be invited for an interview, conducted either in person or via video conference. The application process is detailed on the university's international admissions website, and it's recommended to consult this resource for the most up-to-date information.

**English-Taught Programs:** Kanazawa University offers several degree programs entirely in English, primarily at the graduate level. These include programs in:

*   **Graduate School of Natural Science and Technology:** Various master's and doctoral programs in fields like Mechanical Science, Electrical and Computer Engineering, Environmental Design, and Materials Chemistry. These programs are designed for students with a strong background in science and engineering.
*   **Graduate School of Medical Sciences:** Certain research-focused programs offer instruction and research opportunities in English.
*   **Japan-EU Joint Master's Programme in Global Peace, Security and Strategic Studies (GLOSS):** As mentioned above, this master's program is taught entirely in English.

**Student Support:** Kanazawa University provides comprehensive support services for international students. The International Student Affairs Office offers assistance with visa applications, housing, and general orientation to life in Japan. Academic advisors are available to provide guidance on course selection and academic progress. Counseling services are also available to address any personal or academic challenges students may face. The university also offers Japanese language courses for international students to improve their communication skills and enhance their cultural integration.

**Campus Life & Diversity:** Kanazawa University has a diverse student body, with a significant number of international students from various countries. This creates a vibrant and multicultural campus atmosphere. The university offers a wide range of student clubs and organizations, catering to various interests, from sports to cultural activities. These clubs provide opportunities for international students to interact with Japanese students and participate in campus life. The university actively promotes intercultural exchange and understanding through various events and programs. The university dormitories also provide living accommodations that can support cultural exchange for international students.

**Scholarship Opportunities:** Kanazawa University offers a variety of scholarships for international students. These include:

*   **Japanese Government (MEXT) Scholarship:** This is a highly competitive scholarship offered by the Japanese government to outstanding international students. It covers tuition fees, provides a monthly stipend, and covers travel expenses.
*   **Kanazawa University Scholarship for Privately Financed International Students:** This scholarship provides financial assistance to privately funded international students who demonstrate academic excellence and financial need.
*   **JASSO Scholarship:** Offered by the Japan Student Services Organization, this scholarship provides a monthly stipend to international students.
*   **Private Foundation Scholarships:** Various private foundations offer scholarships to international students studying in Japan. These scholarships often have specific eligibility requirements and application procedures.

**Cost of Living:** The cost of living in Kanazawa is generally lower than in major metropolitan areas like Tokyo or Osaka. Rent for a single apartment near the university can range from 40,000 to 60,000 JPY per month. Food expenses can be estimated at around 30,000 to 40,000 JPY per month. Transportation costs will vary depending on your commuting habits, but a monthly budget of 10,000 to 20,000 JPY should be sufficient. Overall, a monthly budget of 80,000 to 120,000 JPY should cover basic living expenses.
