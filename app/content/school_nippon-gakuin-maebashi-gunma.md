## üìö Courses, Curriculum & Teaching Style

NIPPON Gakuin‚Äôs curriculum is structured around the paramount goal of higher education progression, offering a comprehensive suite of courses ranging from the shortest 1-year track to the maximum 2-year enrollment (specifically: 2-Year, 1 Year 9 Months, 1 Year 6 Months, 1 Year 3 Months, and 1 Year Progression Courses). This staggered entry system (admitting students in April, July, October, and January) maximizes entry flexibility and ensures that students can align their study period precisely with the intake schedules of Japanese universities and graduate schools. The core offering, the *Shingaku* (Progression) Course, is distinguished by its intensity and depth, designed to rapidly elevate students from zero proficiency to N2/N1 levels of the JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test) while simultaneously building the academic foundation necessary for university entrance exams.

In the initial stages (Beginner to Intermediate), the focus is on establishing a solid grammatical and foundational vocabulary base, executed through a balanced approach encompassing reading, writing, listening, and speaking. However, as students transition into the Upper Intermediate and Advanced levels, the curriculum bifurcates and specializes. Dedicated tracks are implemented for EJU preparation, crucial for students targeting Japanese universities. These EJU courses are further segmented into Humanities (focused on Liberal Arts, History, Economics, and Math Course 1) and Science (focused on Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and Math Course 2), mirroring the structure of the actual EJU exam. The curriculum integrates specific preparation for the Japanese component of the EJU, which demands advanced essay writing, comprehension, and critical analysis skills far exceeding the requirements of the standard JLPT.

The faculty, comprising experienced and dedicated Japanese language specialists, are adept at identifying individual student weaknesses and providing targeted instruction and supplementary materials.
