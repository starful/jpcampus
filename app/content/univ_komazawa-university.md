## üè´ University Overview

Komazawa University, "ÈßíÊæ§Â§ßÂ≠¶" in Japanese, stands as a distinguished private university located in Setagaya, Tokyo, with a rich history rooted deeply in Zen Buddhist philosophy. Its origins trace back to the "S≈çt≈çsh≈´ Daigakurin" (S≈çt≈ç Zen Buddhist University) founded in 1882, evolving from an even older seminary established in 1592. This profound historical lineage imbues the university with a unique academic culture, emphasizing the pursuit of wisdom, self-discovery, and a comprehensive understanding of humanity and society, encapsulated in its motto "Gakuin to Eichi no Kensei" (Cultivating Learning and Wisdom).

Komazawa University has steadily built a reputation as a solid mid-tier comprehensive university in Japan, particularly renowned for its strong programs in humanities, social sciences, and Buddhist studies. It attracts students from across Japan and the globe who are seeking a balanced education within a supportive and historically rich environment. Beyond academics, Komazawa is well-known for its competitive sports teams, especially its Hakone Ekiden (relay marathon) team, which has garnered national attention and fostered a strong sense of pride and community among students and alumni. The university boasts a robust alumni network, with graduates making significant contributions in various sectors including academia, business, law, and media. With its commitment to fostering well-rounded individuals equipped with critical thinking skills and a global perspective, Komazawa University continues to be a respected institution in the landscape of Japanese higher education. The university continually adapts to modern educational needs while preserving its foundational values, making it an appealing choice for students seeking a meaningful and impactful university experience.

## üåç International Student Support

Komazawa University is committed to fostering a diverse and inclusive academic environment, actively supporting its international student body. Approximately 400 international students from various countries currently enrich the campus community. The university offers a range of services designed to help international students successfully adapt to life and study in Japan.

*   **Dormitory & Accommodation**: Komazawa University provides the "Komazawa International Plaza," a modern and well-equipped dormitory specifically for international students. This facility offers a safe, convenient, and multicultural living environment, facilitating integration and community building. For students seeking external accommodation, the International Center also provides guidance and resources for finding suitable apartments or shared housing options.
*   **Japanese Language & Cultural Programs**: While most degree programs are taught in Japanese, the university offers comprehensive Japanese language support for international students. These programs cater to various proficiency levels, aiming to enhance language skills necessary for academic success and daily life. Cultural exchange events and workshops are also regularly organized to help students immerse themselves in Japanese culture and interact with local students.
*   **Academic and Life Support**: The International Center serves as a primary point of contact for international students, offering advice on academic matters, visa procedures, health insurance, and general daily life concerns. Mentoring programs and peer support initiatives are also available to ensure a smooth transition and continuous support throughout their studies.
*   **Career Support**: For international students aspiring to work in Japan after graduation, Komazawa University's Career Center provides specialized support. This includes guidance on job searching strategies, resume writing, interview preparation, and understanding the Japanese job market. Workshops and seminars tailored for international students are regularly held, connecting them with potential employers and internship opportunities.
*   **Scholarships**: Komazawa University offers several scholarship programs specifically for international students, both upon admission and during their studies. These scholarships aim to alleviate financial burdens and encourage academic excellence. Information on these opportunities, including eligibility criteria and application procedures, is available through the International Center and the university's official website.
*   **English-Taught Courses**: While full degree programs in English are not a primary feature, certain faculties, particularly the Faculty of Global Media Studies, offer a selection of courses conducted in English, providing opportunities for students to engage with academic content in their native language or develop their English proficiency.
