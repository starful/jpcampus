## 🏫 University Overview

Established in 1886 by Reverend Teizaburo Sunamoto and pioneering American missionaries, Hiroshima Jogakuin University (HJU) stands proudly as the oldest private institution for women's higher education in the Chugoku and Shikoku regions of Japan. Rooted deeply in Protestant Christian principles, HJU has dedicated over 135 years to cultivating self-reliant women of intellect, compassion, and grace who can contribute meaningfully to global society and local communities alike. 

Situated in Hiroshima—a city globally recognized as a powerful symbol of peace, reconciliation, and resilience—the university naturally integrates rigorous peace studies, human rights education, and cross-cultural understanding into its core academic curriculum. The university's educational philosophy is summarized by its school motto, which emphasizes the development of the whole person: body, mind, and spirit. 

HJU is highly renowned for its small-class system. Rather than attending massive, impersonal lecture halls, students benefit from deep, personal interactions with faculty members who act as dedicated mentors. This tight-knit, nurturing academic environment is incredibly beneficial for international students, who may initially find the transition to life and study in Japan challenging. For female students seeking a transformative educational experience that balances specialized professional training with personal growth and global humanitarian values, Hiroshima Jogakuin University offers an ideal, welcoming home in western Japan.

## 📍 Campus & Location

The university is situated on a beautiful, leafy hilltop in the peaceful Ushita-higashi district of Higashi-ku, Hiroshima City. The campus features an elegant blend of modern, state-of-the-art academic buildings and tranquil, historic spaces that reflect the university's Christian heritage. The iconic Gaines Hall and the stunning university chapel are central to campus life, offering quiet places for contemplation, musical events, and spiritual reflection. 

Living and studying in Hiroshima provides international students with a uniquely rich cultural and historical backdrop. As a global city of peace, Hiroshima hosts numerous international conferences, peace seminars, and memorial ceremonies, particularly surrounding the August 6th anniversary. Students have direct access to the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park and Museum, serving as active spaces for academic research, volunteering, and global exchange.

Beyond its historical significance, Hiroshima is a vibrant, modern metropolis. It offers a bustling downtown shopping district (Hondori), scenic coastal areas along the Seto Inland Sea, and world-heritage sites like Miyajima Island. Crucially for students, Hiroshima's cost of living is significantly lower than that of major metropolitan areas like Tokyo, Kyoto, or Osaka. Affordable housing, inexpensive local dining, and excellent public transit (including Hiroshima’s iconic streetcars) make it a highly practical and stress-free city for international students to navigate on a budget. The campus itself is located just a short 15-minute bus ride from Hiroshima Station, the region's main transit hub.

## 📚 Faculty & Department Breakdown

Hiroshima Jogakuin University operates two primary faculties, each offering specialized departments designed to equip women with practical, industry-ready skills and deep intellectual perspectives:

### 1. Faculty of Humanities
This faculty is designed for students passionate about language, literature, intercultural communication, and global affairs. It aims to develop women who can bridge cultural divides and lead in international environments.
- **Department of International English**: Focuses on advanced English proficiency, global communications, and international relations. Students are encouraged to develop a global mindset, with opportunities to study abroad, engage in debate, and participate in international peace initiatives. The program focuses on practical language skills useful in tourism, diplomacy, and global business.
- **Department of Japanese Culture**: Offers deep academic pathways into Japanese literature, traditional arts, linguistics, and history. This department is popular among international students who wish to perfect their Japanese language proficiency while building a comprehensive understanding of Japan’s historical and contemporary cultural identity.

### 2. Faculty of Human Life Science
This faculty emphasizes practical sciences and professional training that directly affect health, community, environment, and early childhood development.
- **Department of Living Space Design**: Combines architecture, interior design, and fashion. Students learn to create functional, sustainable, and aesthetically pleasing environments. This program prepares students for professional licensing exams in interior planning and architectural design.
- **Department of Management and Nutrition**: A highly respected program that trains future registered dietitians and nutrition specialists. It combines advanced scientific knowledge of food safety and metabolic health with management principles, preparing students for careers in hospitals, schools, and the corporate food industry.
- **Department of Child Education**: Prepares students to become licensed nursery school, kindergarten, and elementary school teachers. The curriculum pairs robust pedagogical theory with extensive field placements in local schools.

## 🤝 International Support & Scholarships

Hiroshima Jogakuin University provides a highly supportive ecosystem to help international students adjust to life in Japan and thrive academically:
- **The International Center**: Serves as a dedicated home-base for international students. The friendly, English-speaking staff assist with visa renewals, finding off-campus housing, national health insurance enrollment, and part-time job applications.
- **The Buddy and Tutor System**: To ease the social transition, newly arrived international students are paired with Japanese student mentors. Buddies help with practical tasks like opening a bank account, grocery shopping, navigating local transit, and practicing conversational Japanese.
- **Tailored Japanese Language Support**: HJU offers specialized Japanese language classes designed specifically for non-native speakers. These classes focus on academic writing, research presentation skills, and business Japanese, ensuring students are prepared for professional environments in Japan.
- **Tuition Reductions & Financial Aid**: Privately-funded international students can apply for a 30% tuition fee waiver, significantly lowering their financial burden. The university also actively facilitates applications for external scholarships, including the Japanese Government (MEXT) Honors Scholarship and local foundation grants funded by Hiroshima-based corporations and humanitarian groups.

## 📝 Admissions Guide for International Students

HJU welcomes applications from ambitious female students around the world. The standard admission process includes the following stages:
1. **Eligibility Review**: Applicants must have completed 12 years of formal primary and secondary education outside of Japan and must possess (or be eligible for) a student visa.
2. **Language Requirements**: For programs in the Faculty of Human Life Science and the Department of Japanese Culture, applicants typically need a Japanese proficiency level equivalent to JLPT N2 or higher. For the Department of International English, high English proficiency test scores (such as TOEFL or IELTS) paired with basic Japanese communication skills are highly valued.
3. **Application Portfolio**: Applicants must submit high school transcripts, proof of graduation, a comprehensive statement of purpose outlining their career goals, and recommendation letters from previous educators.
4. **Selection Examination**: The selection process usually consists of a holistic document screening, a short written essay, and a comprehensive interview. To accommodate overseas applicants, interviews are frequently conducted online, focusing on the applicant\'s academic motivation, language aptitude, and alignment with the university’s values of peace and service.

## 💼 Career Prospects & Graduate Pathways

Hiroshima Jogakuin University maintains an outstanding employment rate, consistently exceeding 95% annually. The university's Career Support Center is highly proactive, engaging with students from their first year of study to offer personalized counseling, resume writing workshops, and simulated job interviews.

Graduates from the Faculty of Humanities regularly secure positions in global tourism, hospitality, aviation, translation services, English education, and international trade. Graduates from the Faculty of Human Life Science are highly sought after by architectural firms, interior design studios, food product manufacturers, regional health networks, and local educational systems. 

Because HJU has spent over a century building deep relationships with corporate partners and regional enterprises in western Japan, employers trust the caliber of HJU graduates. For international students wishing to remain in Japan after graduation, HJU’s reputation as an elite, ethical, and practical women’s institution serves as a powerful asset in the competitive Japanese job market, providing a clear and reliable pathway to long-term career success.
