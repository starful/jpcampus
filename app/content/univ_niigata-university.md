## üè´ University Overview & Reputation

Niigata University, situated in Niigata Prefecture, is a prominent national university in Japan, playing a vital role in the region's education and research landscape. Unlike the elite 'Soukeisen' private universities in Tokyo, Niigata University stands as a pillar of public higher education, committed to serving the community and advancing knowledge across diverse fields. As a national university, it benefits from government funding and adheres to rigorous academic standards, offering students a high-quality education at an affordable cost.

Established in 1949 through the consolidation of several existing institutions, including the Niigata Medical College and the Niigata Normal School, Niigata University carries a rich history rooted in regional development. Its founding principles emphasize academic excellence, fostering a spirit of inquiry, and contributing to the well-being of society. Over the years, it has grown into a comprehensive university with a broad range of faculties, attracting students from across Japan and around the world. The university's reputation is built on its strong research capabilities, dedicated faculty, and commitment to providing students with a well-rounded education. While not typically ranked as high as the top-tier national universities like Tokyo or Kyoto, Niigata University consistently holds a respectable position among Japanese universities, particularly for its contributions to regional development and specific fields like medicine and agriculture.

Niigata University is known for its strong emphasis on research and its contributions to various fields, particularly in areas relevant to the local environment and economy. The university's faculty actively engage in cutting-edge research, publishing their findings in leading academic journals and collaborating with industry partners. The university fosters a vibrant academic community, encouraging students to participate in research projects and pursue their intellectual interests. Socially, Niigata University offers a diverse and inclusive environment, with numerous student clubs and organizations catering to a wide range of interests. The university also actively promotes international exchange, welcoming students from around the world and providing opportunities for Japanese students to study abroad. This international focus enriches the campus atmosphere and prepares students for a globalized world.

**Signature Programs:**

One of Niigata University's signature programs is its **Faculty of Agriculture**, which has a long and distinguished history of contributing to the development of agricultural technology and practices in the Niigata region, known for its rice production. The faculty conducts research on a wide range of topics, including crop breeding, soil science, pest management, and agricultural economics. It also operates experimental farms and research facilities, providing students with hands-on learning opportunities. What makes this program stand out is its strong connection to the local community and its commitment to addressing the challenges facing agriculture in the 21st century, such as climate change, food security, and sustainable farming practices. The program also attracts international students interested in learning about Japanese agricultural techniques and contributing to global food security.

Another noteworthy program is the **Faculty of Medicine**. The Faculty of Medicine at Niigata University is renowned for its commitment to providing high-quality medical education, conducting cutting-edge research, and delivering excellent patient care. The program offers a comprehensive curriculum that covers all aspects of medicine, from basic sciences to clinical practice. Students have the opportunity to gain hands-on experience through rotations at the university hospital and affiliated medical institutions. What distinguishes this program is its strong emphasis on research, with faculty members actively engaged in investigating a wide range of medical conditions, including cancer, cardiovascular disease, and infectious diseases. The faculty also has a strong international focus, collaborating with researchers around the world and offering opportunities for students to participate in international exchange programs. This program attracts highly motivated students who are passionate about pursuing careers in medicine and contributing to the advancement of medical knowledge.

## üåç International Student Admissions & Life

**Admissions Process:**

The application process for international students at Niigata University typically involves several steps. Firstly, prospective students must meet the academic requirements for their chosen program, which usually includes a high school diploma or equivalent for undergraduate programs and a bachelor's degree for graduate programs. Secondly, international students are generally required to take the Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU) to demonstrate their Japanese language proficiency and academic abilities. The specific EJU subjects required vary depending on the faculty and program. In addition to the EJU, many programs require international students to submit scores from English proficiency tests such as TOEFL or IELTS, particularly for programs taught in English or those with a significant English language component. Some programs may also require applicants to submit essays, letters of recommendation, and portfolios. Finally, shortlisted applicants may be invited to participate in interviews, either in person or via video conference, to assess their suitability for the program.

**English-Taught Programs:**

Niigata University offers a limited but growing number of degree programs entirely in English, particularly at the graduate level. One example is the **Graduate School of Science and Technology**, which offers several master's and doctoral programs in fields such as engineering, science, and agriculture with courses and research conducted entirely in English. Specific English-taught programs vary from year to year, so prospective students should always check the university's official website for the most up-to-date information. The university is actively working to expand its offerings of English-taught programs to attract more international students and enhance its global presence. Details on available English-taught courses and programs can usually be found on the individual faculty or graduate school websites.

**Student Support:**

Niigata University provides a range of support services for international students to help them adjust to life in Japan and succeed in their studies. The **International Student Office** offers assistance with visa applications, housing arrangements, and general inquiries. Academic advising is available to help students choose courses, plan their academic pathways, and address any academic challenges they may face. Counseling services are also available to provide emotional support and guidance. The university also offers Japanese language classes for international students to improve their communication skills and cultural understanding. In addition, the university has a network of student mentors who can provide peer support and guidance.

**Campus Life & Diversity:**

Niigata University has a vibrant and diverse campus community, with a growing number of international students from around the world. While the exact number fluctuates, there are approximately 600 international students enrolled. The university has numerous student clubs and organizations catering to a wide range of interests, including sports, arts, culture, and academics. These clubs provide opportunities for international students to connect with Japanese students and fellow international students, fostering a sense of community and belonging. The overall campus atmosphere is welcoming and inclusive, with a strong emphasis on intercultural exchange and understanding. The university also organizes various events and activities throughout the year to promote internationalization and celebrate cultural diversity.

**Scholarship Opportunities:**

Niigata University offers several scholarship opportunities for international students. The most prestigious is the **Japanese Government (MEXT) Scholarship**, which covers tuition fees, provides a monthly stipend, and covers travel expenses to and from Japan. MEXT scholarships are highly competitive and require a rigorous application process. The university also offers its own scholarships for international students, such as the **Niigata University International Student Scholarship**, which provides partial tuition fee waivers. In addition, there are several private foundation scholarships available to international students studying in Japan, such as the **Rotary Yoneyama Memorial Foundation Scholarship** and the **JASSO Scholarship**. Information on these scholarships can be found on the university's website or through the Japan Student Services Organization (JASSO).

**Cost of Living:**

The cost of living in Niigata City is relatively affordable compared to major metropolitan areas like Tokyo or Osaka. Rent for a single apartment near the university typically ranges from 30,000 to 50,000 JPY per month. Food expenses can vary depending on dietary habits, but a reasonable monthly budget for groceries and eating out is around 30,000 to 40,000 JPY. Transportation costs are relatively low, as the university is well-connected by public transportation, and many students choose to cycle. A rough monthly budget for rent, food, transportation, and other expenses would be around 80,000 to 120,000 JPY.

**Main Campus Vibe:**

The main campus of Niigata University is located in the Ikarashi district of Niigata City, a relatively quiet and suburban area. The campus is spacious and green, providing a peaceful and conducive environment for studying and research. While not in the bustling city center, the campus is well-connected to the rest of Niigata City by public transportation, with easy access to shopping, dining, and entertainment options. The surrounding neighborhood is primarily residential, with a mix of houses and apartments, creating a safe and welcoming atmosphere for students. The Shinano River, the longest river in Japan, flows nearby, providing opportunities for recreation and relaxation.
