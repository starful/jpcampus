## üè´ University Overview: A Legacy of Engineering Excellence

Kogakuin University, established in 1887 as the "Koshu Gakko" (Industrial School), stands as one of Japan's oldest and most prestigious private technical institutions. Located in the heart of Tokyo, it has played a pivotal role in Japan's modernization and industrial growth for over 135 years. For an international student, Kogakuin offers a unique blend of historical prestige and cutting-edge urban facilities. Unlike many technical universities that are situated in remote suburban areas, Kogakuin‚Äôs primary identity is tied to its skyscraper campus in Shinjuku‚Äîthe world‚Äôs busiest transport hub.

The university‚Äôs mission is centered on "Kogaku" (Engineering) as a discipline that serves the public good. It emphasizes practical application over abstract theory, ensuring that graduates are ready to enter the workforce with tangible skills. This "hands-on" philosophy is particularly attractive to international students who aim to secure employment within Japan‚Äôs competitive technology and construction sectors.

## üè¢ Campus Life: Shinjuku vs. Hachioji

One of the most defining characteristics of Kogakuin University is its dual-campus system, which offers two very different Japanese experiences.

### The Shinjuku Campus: The Urban Laboratory
Rising 28 stories high, the Shinjuku Campus is a vertical university. It is situated just a 5-minute walk from Shinjuku Station. This campus houses the upper-level students (3rd and 4th years) and graduate researchers. Studying here means being surrounded by Japan‚Äôs corporate headquarters, giving students an immediate sense of the industry they are about to enter. The high-rise building contains state-of-the-art laboratories, a massive library with panoramic views of the city, and specialized design studios for architecture students. The proximity to Shinjuku means that networking opportunities, internships, and part-time jobs are literally at your doorstep.

### The Hachioji Campus: Innovation in Nature
First- and second-year students primarily spend their time at the Hachioji Campus, located in the western outskirts of Tokyo. This campus provides a traditional university atmosphere with sprawling grounds, modern athletic facilities, and specialized research centers that require more space than a skyscraper can provide, such as the wind tunnel labs and heavy machinery workshops. Hachioji is known as a "university town," offering a lower cost of living and a quieter environment that helps students focus on their foundational science and mathematics courses before moving to the high-pressure environment of central Tokyo.

## üß™ Academic Strengths and Faculties

Kogakuin University is divided into four main faculties, each catering to specific sectors of the global tech economy.

### 1. Faculty of Architecture
This is arguably Kogakuin‚Äôs most famous department. It was the first private university in Japan to establish a dedicated Faculty of Architecture. The program covers everything from urban planning and earthquake-resistant design to interior aesthetics and historical preservation. Given Japan's leadership in seismic engineering, international students in this faculty gain world-class knowledge that is highly applicable globally.

### 2. Faculty of Informatics
As the digital transformation (DX) sweeps through Japan, the Faculty of Informatics has become the most popular choice for tech-savvy international students. The curriculum covers Artificial Intelligence, Computer Science, and Media Network Engineering. Students are taught to design systems that integrate hardware and software, preparing them for roles in software development, cybersecurity, and data science.

### 3. Faculty of Engineering
This is the traditional heart of the university, featuring Mechanical Engineering, Electrical Engineering, and Applied Chemistry. The focus here is on "Monozukuri" (the art of making things). The university maintains deep ties with Japan's manufacturing giants (like Toyota, Honda, and Mitsubishi Electric), ensuring the curriculum stays updated with current industrial standards.

### 4. Faculty of Advanced Engineering
This faculty focuses on the intersection of engineering and life sciences. It includes departments like Applied Physics and Environmental Chemical Engineering. It is ideal for students interested in sustainable energy solutions, biotechnology, and the development of new materials.

## üåç International Student Support and Admissions

Kogakuin University is actively expanding its international footprint. While most undergraduate programs are conducted in Japanese, the university provides robust support for non-native speakers.

### Admission Process
For undergraduate international students, the primary route is the "Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students" (EJU). Prospective students must demonstrate proficiency in Japanese (usually N2 level or higher) and take subject tests in Mathematics and Sciences (Physics, Chemistry, or Biology depending on the faculty). There is also an interview and an internal screening process. 

### Scholarship Opportunities
Recognizing the financial burden of living in Tokyo, Kogakuin offers several financial aid packages. These include:
- **Tuition Reduction:** International students with excellent academic records can receive a 25% to 30% reduction in tuition fees.
- **Kogakuin University Scholarships:** Merit-based grants awarded to top-performing students in each department.
- **External Scholarships:** The university office helps students apply for the JASSO honors scholarship and private foundation grants.

### Language Support
The "International Center" provides Japanese language assistance, helping students bridge the gap between conversational Japanese and the technical Japanese required for engineering reports and laboratory presentations.

## üíº Career Prospects: The "Kogakuin Brand"

The strongest selling point for Kogakuin University is its employment rate. In Japan, Kogakuin is known as a "hidden gem" among recruiters. Because the university focuses exclusively on STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics), Japanese companies know that Kogakuin graduates possess high technical literacy and a disciplined work ethic.

Every year, the university boasts a job placement rate of over 98%. The Career Support Center offers specialized guidance for international students, including workshops on Japanese business etiquette, resume writing for the "Shukatsu" (job hunting) season, and mock interviews. Major employers include Sony, Panasonic, JR East, Obayashi Corporation, and various government agencies. For an international student, having the Kogakuin name on a resume serves as a strong endorsement of one's technical capabilities within the Japanese labor market.

## üèòÔ∏è Living in Tokyo: Housing and Costs

Studying at Kogakuin means navigating the Tokyo lifestyle. 

- **Housing:** While the university does not have traditional on-campus dormitories at the Shinjuku site, they partner with private international student dorms (like Kyoritsu Maintenance) to provide safe, affordable housing near the Chuo Line or Keio Line, allowing for an easy commute to both campuses.
- **Budgeting:** Students should budget approximately 120,000 to 150,000 JPY per month for living expenses (including rent, food, and transport). Part-time work is permitted up to 28 hours a week, and many students find roles in convenience stores, language teaching, or as lab assistants.
