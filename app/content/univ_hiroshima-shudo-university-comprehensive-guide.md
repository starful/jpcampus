## 🏫 University Overview

Hiroshima Shudo University (HSU), locally known as 'Shudai', is widely recognized as the most prestigious private university in the Chugoku and Shikoku regions of Japan. Established in its current form in 1960 but tracing its origins back to the 'Kogakkan' founded in 1725 by the Hiroshima Domain, the university carries a legacy of three centuries of academic excellence. The university's philosophy is rooted in 'Shudo' (Following the Path), emphasizing the cultivation of individuals who can contribute meaningfully to society through both specialized knowledge and ethical integrity.

Located in the vibrant city of Hiroshima, the university offers a serene yet intellectually stimulating environment. It has evolved from a single-faculty institution into a comprehensive university with seven faculties and multiple graduate schools. For international students, HSU represents a gateway to understanding the 'Real Japan'—a blend of historical resilience, industrial innovation, and a high quality of life far more affordable than Tokyo or Osaka.

## 🎓 Academic Excellence & Faculties

Hiroshima Shudo University is structured to address the diverse needs of modern society. The academic offerings are divided into seven primary faculties:

1.  **Faculty of Commercial Sciences:** The flagship faculty and one of the oldest in the region. It focuses on accounting, marketing, and management. Students here often secure positions in top-tier regional banks and manufacturing firms.
2.  **Faculty of Humanities and Human Sciences:** Offering departments in Psychology, Education, and Sociology. This faculty is known for its research into the human mind and social structures, providing a solid foundation for those interested in social services or corporate HR.
3.  **Faculty of Law:** A highly respected faculty that produces a significant number of civil servants and legal professionals in Hiroshima Prefecture. The curriculum emphasizes legal reasoning and political science.
4.  **Faculty of Economic Sciences:** Focuses on both macro and microeconomics, with a strong emphasis on data analysis and regional economic revitalization.
5.  **Faculty of Human Environmental Studies:** This interdisciplinary faculty combines social sciences with environmental concerns, focusing on sustainable urban development and environmental policy.
6.  **Faculty of Health Sciences:** One of the newer additions, focusing on nursing and psychiatric social work, addressing the needs of Japan’s aging society.
7.  **Faculty of International Studies:** A popular choice for international students, focusing on cross-cultural communication, global politics, and English proficiency. It provides a hub for international exchange on campus.

## 🌏 International Student Support & Environment

Hiroshima Shudo University is deeply committed to internationalization. The Center for International Education (CIE) serves as a dedicated support hub for foreign students. 

### Financial Support
One of the most attractive features for international applicants is the **Tuition Reduction System**. Eligible international students can receive a 30% reduction in their tuition fees. Additionally, the university offers internal scholarships (such as the Hiroshima Shudo University Scholarship for International Students) and assists students in applying for external funds like the JASSO Monbukagakusho Honors Scholarship.

### Living and Housing
While the university does not have a traditional 'on-campus' dormitory for all, it provides specialized housing support and has dedicated international residences like the **i-House**. The cost of living in Hiroshima is significantly lower than in Tokyo; students can live comfortably on approximately 80,000 to 110,000 JPY per month, including rent and food.

### Language and Tutoring
To help students integrate, HSU offers a 'Tutor System' where Japanese students are paired with international newcomers to assist with daily life, study habits, and Japanese language practice. There are also intensive Japanese language courses available for those who need to sharpen their proficiency to keep up with specialized lectures.

## 🏢 Career Prospects and Job Placement

HSU boasts one of the highest job placement rates in Western Japan. The university has an incredibly strong 'Shudo Network' consisting of thousands of alumni who hold leadership positions in local government and regional industries such as Mazda, Hiroshima Bank, and various global manufacturing firms based in the region.

For international students, the Career Center provides:
- Specialized seminars on Japanese business etiquette.
- Mock interviews and resume (Rirekisho) correction.
- Direct networking events with companies looking to hire bilingual talent.
- Internships within Hiroshima-based companies that operate globally.

## 📍 Campus Life in Hiroshima

The campus is situated in Asaminami-ku, easily accessible via the Astram Line (a modern monorail system). The 'Green Campus' is known for its beautiful architecture and natural surroundings, offering a peaceful atmosphere for study. 

Beyond the classroom, students can join over 100 'Circles' (clubs) ranging from traditional Japanese arts like Kendo and Tea Ceremony to modern sports and music. Participating in these clubs is often the best way for international students to make lifelong Japanese friends and fully immerse themselves in the local culture.

Hiroshima city itself is a 'City of Peace' with a global outlook. It is safe, clean, and full of cultural landmarks like the Peace Memorial Park and the nearby Miyajima Island. For a student, it provides a perfect balance of urban convenience and natural beauty.

## 📝 Admission Guide for International Students

### Application Requirements
International applicants typically need to undergo the 'Special Entrance Examination for International Students.' Key requirements include:
- Completion of 12 years of formal education.
- **EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission):** Most faculties require scores from the Japanese as a Foreign Language, Japan and the World, and Mathematics sections.
- **JLPT:** While EJU is preferred, holding a JLPT N2 or N1 level is highly recommended for academic success.
- **Interview:** A crucial part of the process where your motivation and communication skills are assessed.

### Key Dates
Applications usually open in the late autumn (October/November) for April enrollment. Some faculties may have second-round applications in January, but it is always safer to apply early to secure scholarship opportunities.

## 💡 Why Choose Hiroshima Shudo University?

Choosing Shudai means choosing a university that values the individual. Unlike massive urban universities where international students can sometimes feel lost in the crowd, HSU provides a close-knit community. You receive the prestige of a top regional private university while benefiting from personalized support, a lower cost of living, and direct pathways into the Japanese labor market. If you are looking for a rigorous academic experience combined with the warmth of Japanese regional culture, Hiroshima Shudo University is an exceptional choice.
