## 🏫 University Overview

The University of Nagasaki (長崎県立大学, Nagasaki Kenritsu Daigaku) is a distinguished public institution located in the scenic and historically rich Nagasaki Prefecture. Established through the merger of the Sasebo Public University and the Siebold University of Nagasaki in 2008, it has evolved into a powerhouse of practical education and regional innovation. The university operates across two main sites: the Sasebo Campus, which serves as the administrative heart and focuses on business and regional development, and the Siebold Campus in Nagayo (near Nagasaki City), which focuses on global studies, information science, and health sciences.

For international students, the University of Nagasaki offers a unique proposition: the academic rigor of a public university combined with the intimate, supportive atmosphere of a regional community. Unlike the massive, impersonal campuses found in Tokyo or Osaka, this university provides a setting where professors know their students by name and where the curriculum is deeply intertwined with the needs of the local and global economy.

## 🎓 Academic Excellence and Faculties

### 1. Faculty of Management (Sasebo Campus)
This faculty is designed for students aiming to become leaders in the corporate world or entrepreneurs. It covers business administration, accounting, and marketing. International students often find this faculty attractive because of its focus on the 'Asian Business' context, reflecting Nagasaki's historical role as Japan's gateway to the rest of the continent.

### 2. Faculty of Regional Creation (Sasebo Campus)
Focusing on economics and policy, this faculty aims to solve modern societal issues. It is ideal for students interested in urban planning, public administration, or regional revitalization. The curriculum emphasizes fieldwork, encouraging students to step out of the classroom and engage with local businesses and government offices to understand the economic dynamics of rural and semi-urban Japan.

### 3. Faculty of International Relations (Siebold Campus)
Located on the more modern Siebold campus, this faculty is a hub for global communication. It offers specialized tracks in Global Studies and American/European culture. For an international student, this environment is highly stimulating as it fosters a multicultural dialogue. The language programs here are robust, designed to produce graduates fluent in multiple languages with a deep understanding of international politics and sociology.

### 4. Faculty of Information Policy (Siebold Campus)
This is arguably the university's flagship faculty. In 2016, it established the **Department of Information Security**, the first of its kind in a Japanese public university. In an era where cybersecurity is paramount, this department receives significant government and corporate attention. Students learn everything from high-level coding to ethical hacking and digital forensics. The facility includes advanced security operation centers (SOC) for hands-on training.

### 5. Faculty of Nursing and Nutrition (Siebold Campus)
While highly competitive and requiring high Japanese proficiency, these programs are renowned for their high success rates in national licensing exams. They are dedicated to improving public health and wellness, utilizing state-of-the-art medical equipment and simulation labs.

## 🌏 Why Choose Nagasaki?

Nagasaki is not just a city; it is a symbol of resilience and internationalism. Historically, during Japan's period of isolation (Sakoku), Nagasaki was the only port open to international trade. This legacy of openness is reflected in the university's DNA. 

**The Sasebo Campus** is located in a city with a heavy naval history and a distinct American influence due to the U.S. Navy base, making it a culturally diverse environment. **The Siebold Campus** is near Nagasaki City, famous for its hilly terrain, tramcars, and breathtaking night views. For a student, the cost of living in Nagasaki is significantly lower than in the Kanto or Kansai regions. You can enjoy high-quality fresh seafood, unique 'Champon' noodles, and a rich historical landscape without the financial stress of Tokyo life.

## 📝 Admissions for International Students

The University of Nagasaki actively seeks international talent, primarily through the **Special Selection for International Students** (私費外国人留学生特別入試). 

### Key Requirements:
1. **EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission):** Most faculties require scores in 'Japanese as a Foreign Language,' 'Mathematics,' and 'Japan and the World.'
2. **JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test):** While EJU is the standard, having a N2 or N1 certification is highly recommended for navigating the rigorous academic environment.
3. **English Proficiency:** For the Faculty of International Relations and Information Policy, TOEFL or TOEIC scores are often required.
4. **Interview:** A crucial part of the process where your motivation, understanding of the chosen field, and future goals are assessed.

## 💴 Tuition and Financial Aid

As a public university, the tuition fees are standardized and significantly lower than those of private universities. 
- **Admission Fee:** Approximately 282,000 JPY (Note: Residents of Nagasaki Prefecture may receive a 50% discount on this fee).
- **Annual Tuition:** Approximately 535,800 JPY.

**Financial Support:**
The university offers a **Tuition Waiver System** for international students facing financial hardship. Depending on academic performance and need, students can receive a 50% or even 100% waiver of their tuition fees. Additionally, students are eligible to apply for the JASSO honors scholarship and various local foundation scholarships specific to Nagasaki-based students.

## 🏠 Student Life and Career Support

### Housing
While the university does not have a traditional 'on-campus' dormitory for all students, they provide extensive support in finding affordable 'Student Apartments' nearby. Many of these are within walking distance and cost between 25,000 to 45,000 JPY per month, which is nearly impossible to find in larger cities.

### Part-time Work
With the 'Permission to Engage in Activity other than that Permitted under the Status of Residence Previously Granted,' students can work up to 28 hours a week. Sasebo and Nagasaki offer many opportunities in retail, hospitality, and tourism, which are excellent ways to improve Japanese language skills.

### Career Prospects
The university’s Career Center is exceptionally proactive. Because of the university's strong reputation in the Kyushu region, graduates are highly sought after by regional banks, IT firms, and manufacturing companies. For those in the Information Security department, the job placement rate is near 100%, with many students receiving offers from major tech firms in Tokyo even before graduation.

## 💡 Expert Tip for Applicants

If you are interested in the **Information Policy** or **International Relations** faculties, emphasize your desire to bridge the gap between Japan and your home country through technology or diplomacy in your statement of purpose. The University of Nagasaki values students who don't just want to study, but want to contribute to the 'Regional Vitalization' (Chiiki Sosei) of Nagasaki while maintaining a global perspective. Mentioning the specific 'Department of Information Security' and its unique labs will also demonstrate that you have done your research, which is highly viewed during the interview process.

In conclusion, the University of Nagasaki is a hidden gem for international students. It offers a high-level public education, specialized cutting-edge fields like cybersecurity, and a peaceful, culturally rich lifestyle at a fraction of the cost of Japan's major metropolitan centers. It is an ideal choice for the focused student who wants to truly integrate into Japanese society while preparing for a modern, global career.
