## 🏫 University Overview

Kyushu Institute of Information Sciences (KIIS), or *Kyushu Joho Daigaku* (九州情報大学), is a progressive private university in Fukuoka Prefecture, Japan. Founded in 1998, KIIS was created with a clear mission: to bridge the gap between rapidly advancing information technology (IT) and modern business management. In an era where digital transformation (DX) dictates global enterprise success, KIIS stands out by equipping students with both technical programming skills and strategic business insights. The university operates under a philosophy of practical learning, emphasizing real-world applications over purely theoretical instruction, making it highly attractive to international students seeking a direct career pathway in Japan's tech and business sectors.

KIIS is celebrated for its highly personalized educational style. With a close-knit student cohort, professors and career advisors provide individual guidance to every student. This support is incredibly beneficial for international students navigating a new country, language, and academic culture. The campus has a diverse student body, with many international students arriving from across East and Southeast Asia. This multicultural atmosphere fosters a global perspective, allowing students to build lifelong networks with peers from diverse backgrounds while mastering Japanese business etiquette.

## 📍 Campus Locations: Dazaifu and Hakata

KIIS operates two distinct campuses, each offering unique benefits:

1. **Dazaifu Main Campus:** Located in the historic city of Dazaifu, this serves as the primary academic hub. Famous for the Dazaifu Tenmangu Shrine, Dazaifu is surrounded by ancient temples and tranquil landscapes, offering a peaceful environment ideal for academic concentration.
2. **Hakata Station Campus:** Situated in the heart of Fukuoka City, right next to Hakata Station—the largest transit hub in Kyushu—this campus is designed for upper-year students and those actively job hunting. Fukuoka is recognized as one of Japan's most dynamic, livable, and entrepreneurial cities, functioning as a special national startup zone. The Hakata Campus provides students with direct access to corporate headquarters, networking events, part-time jobs, and urban amenities.

## 🎓 Academic Structure: Faculty of Management Information

The academic core of KIIS is the **Faculty of Management Information**, which offers a unified undergraduate program designed to integrate computer science and business administration. Students specialize in structured curriculum tracks:

*   **Information Systems Course:** Designed for students aiming to become system engineers, programmers, network administrators, or cybersecurity specialists. The curriculum focuses heavily on practical coding languages (such as Java, Python, and C++), database structure, cloud computing, artificial intelligence, and network architecture.
*   **Business Management Course:** For future managers, entrepreneurs, or business consultants, this track covers marketing, human resource management, financial accounting, and corporate strategy. Students learn how to leverage digital tools and big data analytics to make modern business decisions.
*   **Global Business Course:** Tailored for international students seeking careers in multinational corporations, emphasizing trade logistics, intercultural communication, and business Japanese.

## 🌐 International Student Experience & Support

KIIS is a pioneer in welcoming international students, building a robust support network to ensure personal and academic success. The **International Center** at KIIS serves as a dedicated one-stop hub for all non-Japanese students, assisting with visa processing, COE issuance, airport pick-ups, and transition into local housing.

Moreover, KIIS provides comprehensive Japanese language support. For students needing to polish their language skills, the curriculum includes intensive Japanese language and culture classes. These courses help students transition from conversational Japanese to the formal 'Keigo' (business Japanese) necessary for navigating corporate interviews. Additionally, the university organizes frequent intercultural exchange events and local home-stay programs.

## 💰 Tuition, Fees, and Scholarship Programs

KIIS works hard to remain one of the most financially accessible private universities in Japan. The standard tuition structure is transparent and highly competitive:

*   **Admission Fee (First Year Only):** ¥200,000
*   **Annual Tuition Fee:** ¥740,000
*   **Other Fees (Facilities, Insurance, etc.):** Approximately ¥150,000 - ¥200,000 annually.

To ease the financial burden, KIIS offers an excellent **International Student Tuition Reduction Program**. Under this system, privately funded international students who meet academic and financial criteria can receive a **30% to 50% tuition waiver**.

Furthermore, outstanding students are nominated for external scholarships, including the prestigious **JASSO Honors Scholarship** and various local foundation grants. Fukuoka's overall cost of living is also significantly lower than Tokyo’s—rent for a comfortable private apartment near the Dazaifu campus ranges from ¥30,000 to ¥50,000 per month.

## 📝 Admission Process & Requirements

KIIS welcomes applications from students globally who have completed 12 years of formal education. The admissions process evaluates a candidate’s academic potential, motivation, and language capability. While the primary language of instruction is Japanese, the university looks at the applicant’s overall potential.

**Key Application Requirements:**
1.  **Academic Records:** High school graduation certificate and official academic transcripts.
2.  **Language Proficiency:** Generally, applicants should possess Japanese language proficiency equivalent to JLPT N2 or higher. However, students who have completed a course at a recognized Japanese language school in Japan, or those who score well on the EJU Japanese subject, are highly competitive.
3.  **Statement of Purpose:** A detailed written essay outlining why the student wants to study at KIIS and their future career goals in Japan.
4.  **Admissions Interview:** Conducted in Japanese to assess communication skills and readiness.

## 💼 Career Prospects and Job Placement Support

KIIS achieves an exceptional job placement rate (often exceeding 90% for active job-seekers) by operating a highly proactive **Career Support Center**. Advisors work directly with students starting from their second and third years. The support includes:

*   **Individualized Career Counseling:** Helping students identify their strengths and map out target industries.
*   **Resume and Entry Sheet Writing Workshops:** Teaching students how to draft compelling job applications in Japanese style.
*   **Mock Interview Practice:** Preparing students for the rigorous multi-stage interview process typical of Japanese corporate hiring.
*   **On-Campus Company Information Sessions:** Bringing prominent employers directly to the university to recruit KIIS students.

Graduates of KIIS are highly sought after by companies in the IT sector (such as software developers and tech startups in Fukuoka’s special economic zone), international logistics, trade, and hospitality/tourism sectors.

## 🏠 Student Life and Housing in Fukuoka

Fukuoka is routinely voted one of the most livable cities in the world due to its perfect size, safety, friendly locals, outstanding public transit, and legendary food culture.

**Accommodation & Part-Time Work:** KIIS assists international students in securing safe, affordable housing. Many students choose to rent private apartments close to the Dazaifu campus. Under Japanese law, international students can work up to 28 hours per week (and up to 40 hours during vacations) with a simple permit. Fukuoka's thriving economy offers abundant part-time work in restaurants, convenience stores, English teaching, and hospitality, helping students cover their living expenses.

**Extracurriculars:** KIIS hosts various student clubs and circles, ranging from computer programming and esports to traditional sports. The university's Sumo Club is nationally famous, having trained several professional sumo wrestlers. Engaging in these clubs is the fastest way for international students to make close Japanese friends and immerse themselves in local culture.

However, if you are looking for an affordable, highly supportive environment where you can master high-demand IT skills, integrate deeply with Japanese business practices, and live in one of Japan's most exciting regions, KIIS is an exceptional choice. The unique integration of business and IT ensures you graduate as a specialized bilingual professional who understands how to apply technology to drive business value. Supported by generous tuition discounts and an active career service team, KIIS offers an incredible return on investment.
