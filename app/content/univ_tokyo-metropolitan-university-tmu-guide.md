## üéì Faculties & Departments

Tokyo Metropolitan University offers seven distinct undergraduate faculties, designed to cover a broad spectrum of modern academic inquiry, with a consistent emphasis on high-quality research and practical application. These faculties are strategically aligned with the needs of contemporary society, particularly in the context of advanced urban living and technological innovation.

**1. Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences:** This faculty is highly diverse, encompassing fields such as Behavioral Studies, Human Social Dynamics, Culture and Society, and Contemporary Thought. Unlike many traditional humanities schools, TMU emphasizes research relevant to contemporary urban society, focusing heavily on linguistics, sociology, anthropology, and philosophy as they intersect with modern societal issues. Its structure allows students flexibility to pursue interdisciplinary topics.

**2. Faculty of Law:** Distinguished by its focus on practical legal application and governance structures relevant to metropolitan administration. Departments include the Department of Law and the Department of Political Science. The curriculum is rigorous, designed to train future leaders in law, policy-making, and public administration, often directly feeding into the Tokyo Metropolitan Government.

**3. Faculty of Economics and Business Administration:** TMU‚Äôs approach here balances theoretical economic models with empirical analysis crucial for understanding global markets and corporate strategy within the Japanese context. Specializations include Economics and Business Administration. A strong emphasis is placed on quantitative methods, financial analysis, and strategic management, preparing graduates for competitive roles in finance, consulting, and commerce.

**4. Faculty of Science:** A bedrock of fundamental scientific inquiry, this faculty includes departments such as Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, and Biological Sciences. TMU‚Äôs Science faculty is particularly noted for its excellence in materials science and environmental chemistry, contributing significantly to TMU‚Äôs overall high research output. State-of-the-art facilities support complex experimental work, often involving collaboration with external research institutes.

**5. Faculty of Urban Environment Sciences:** This faculty is arguably TMU's most distinctive offering, deeply connected to its unique mission. It integrates multiple disciplines necessary for sustainable urban development. Departments include Geography, Civil and Environmental Engineering, Architecture and Building Science, and Urban Liberal Arts. Students here learn to analyze environmental stressors, design resilient infrastructure, and plan equitable urban spaces, directly leveraging the insights gained from studying Tokyo itself as a living laboratory.

**6. Faculty of Systems Design (Hino Campus):** Located slightly separate to foster innovation, this faculty focuses on advanced engineering and design principles. It houses departments like Mechanical Engineering Systems, Precision Engineering, Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, and Industrial Art and Engineering. This faculty bridges the gap between theoretical engineering and industrial application, specializing in robotics, informatics, and human-centered design, catering to the sophisticated technological needs of modern manufacturing and IT sectors.

**7. Faculty of Health Sciences (Arakawa Campus):** Crucial for Japan's aging society, this faculty trains highly skilled healthcare professionals. It includes Nursing Sciences, Physical Therapy, Occupational Therapy, and Radiological Sciences. The curriculum is structured to integrate clinical knowledge with public health perspectives, utilizing collaborations with local hospitals and TMU's own health facilities to provide comprehensive practical training.

Admission to all undergraduate programs requires exceptional academic records, performance on the Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU), and often supplementary examination/interview administered by TMU itself. Due to the high standard of instruction and competitive admission, prospective students must demonstrate not only academic aptitude but also profound dedication to mastering the Japanese language.

## üåç International Student Support

TMU is dedicated to fostering a diverse and inclusive academic environment, offering robust support services specifically tailored to the unique needs of its growing international student body. These services encompass academic advising, language support, housing, and a comprehensive scholarship framework aimed at mitigating the financial burden of studying abroad.

### Comprehensive Orientation and Academic Support

Upon arrival, international students participate in detailed orientation programs covering academic life, administrative procedures, campus facilities, and navigating daily life in Tokyo. The **International Center** acts as the primary hub for support, offering counseling on visa status, residency renewals, national health insurance, and cultural adjustment. TMU recognizes that language proficiency remains a significant hurdle, and while comprehensive Japanese language courses are available, they are often supplementary; students are expected to enter with high proficiency. Academic advisors are assigned to help international students acclimate to the Japanese classroom style, emphasizing effective study habits and cultural norms within higher education.

### Housing Opportunities

Securing affordable accommodation is a major concern in Tokyo, and TMU addresses this through dedicated facilities:

1.  **Minami-Osawa International Student Dormitory (MOISD):** This facility provides subsidized, safe, and conveniently located housing for international students, prioritizing newly arrived undergraduates and graduates. Living in the dormitory significantly reduces initial housing costs and provides an immediate community network. Spaces are limited and highly competitive, requiring early application.
2.  **Private Housing Support:** For students who cannot secure dormitory placement, the International Center assists in locating suitable private apartments, often connecting students with local housing agencies familiar with the needs of foreign residents, and providing guidance on contract negotiation and guaranteeing. TMU often partners with the Tokyo Metropolitan Government to offer housing guarantees or subsidies through affiliated organizations.

### Scholarship and Financial Aid

TMU‚Äôs strong public funding allows it to offer highly competitive tuition rates, which are further enhanced by extensive financial aid options:

1.  **Tuition Fee Waiver Program (Reduction/Exemption):** TMU offers significant fee reduction or full exemption programs for both admission and yearly tuition fees for students demonstrating financial hardship and exceptional academic performance. These waivers are typically reviewed annually and can cover up to 100% of the costs, making TMU remarkably affordable.
2.  **JASSO Scholarship:** International students are strongly encouraged to apply for the Japan Student Services Organization (JASSO) Honors Scholarship for Privately Financed International Students. TMU actively recommends eligible candidates, providing a monthly stipend to support living expenses.
3.  **Tokyo Metropolitan Government (TMG) Scholarships:** TMU students are eligible for specialized scholarships funded by the TMG, aimed at high-achieving individuals who commit to contributing to the city's future development. These are often highly prestigious and financially generous.
4.  **Private and Corporate Scholarships:** The International Center maintains a database of external scholarship opportunities provided by various Japanese foundations and private corporations, aiding students in the application process throughout the academic year.

### Career Services and Global Mobility

The Career Support Center provides targeted assistance for international students navigating the complex Japanese job market, including tailored seminars on *Shukatsu* (job hunting), resume critique, interview practice, and networking events connecting students with companies actively seeking international talent. Given TMU's connection to the Tokyo Metropolitan Government, graduates often find strong pathways into local government roles and public service. Furthermore, TMU promotes global mobility through numerous student exchange partnerships with over 150 universities worldwide, allowing international students to also participate in short-term programs abroad, enhancing their global perspective and intercultural competencies.

### Detailed Financial Considerations

#### Cost-Effectiveness and Public Status
The yearly tuition of ¬•520,800 is highly competitive. For comparison, tuition fees at major private universities in Tokyo often exceed ¬•1,200,000 annually, making TMU less than half the price. This financial efficiency, coupled with TMU's high academic ranking and research prowess, explains the severe competition for international student slots. The fees are also consistent across all undergraduate faculties, whether pursuing highly expensive engineering degrees or humanities degrees.

#### Fee Waivers and Financial Aid
The availability of the Tuition Fee Waiver Program is a critical factor. International students demonstrating financial need and strong academic performance can apply for a waiver covering 30%, 50%, or 100% of the tuition fee. This is a life-changing benefit for many students. Given that the waiver program is heavily supported by TMG funding, the success rate for needy and deserving international students is relatively high compared to some national universities, though not guaranteed. Students must apply for the waiver every semester and maintain excellent grades to continue receiving the reduction.

#### Other Mandatory Expenses
Beyond tuition, students must budget for the following estimated annual expenses:

*   **Insurance/Health:** Approximately ¬•30,000 (National Health Insurance is mandatory and subsidized).
*   **Textbooks/Materials:** Approximately ¬•50,000 - ¬•100,000 (Varies heavily by major, STEM fields are usually higher).
*   **Dormitory Fees (If applicable):** ¬•30,000 - ¬•50,000 per month, significantly cheaper than market rates.
*   **Cost of Living (Excluding Housing):** Tokyo life is expensive. A student should budget ¬•80,000 - ¬•120,000 per month for food, utilities, transportation, and personal expenses, though the Minami-Osawa location (Hachioji) is generally slightly less expensive than living in central wards like Shinjuku or Shibuya.

#### Opportunities for Work
International students on a student visa are permitted to work up to 28 hours per week (up to 40 hours during official university breaks) after obtaining permission from immigration. Given the minimum wage in Tokyo is high (over ¬•1,113 per hour as of 2024), part-time work serves as a significant method for covering living expenses, though students are strongly advised to focus primarily on their demanding academic studies, especially in the first year.

TMU represents a superb investment in higher education. Its public status ensures affordability, while its research excellence guarantees a world-class academic experience. Strategic utilization of the fee waiver system and available scholarships can reduce the overall educational cost to minimal levels, making TMU highly accessible despite its prime location in one of the world's most costly cities.
