## 🏫 University Overview

The **University of East Asia** (東亜大学, *Tōa Daigaku*), established in 1966 and located in Shimonoseki, Yamaguchi Prefecture, is a progressive private university with a deeply rooted international outlook. Guided by its founding philosophy of fostering global citizens who can contribute to the progress, peace, and integration of the East Asian region, the university has long been a welcoming hub for students from across Asia and the world.

Unlike traditional academic institutions that focus solely on theoretical studies, the University of East Asia is highly regarded for its highly practical, industry-aligned curricula. The university offers a unique blend of scientific inquiry, creative arts, and professional vocational training. Notably, it is one of the few four-year universities in Japan where students can earn a bachelor's degree in fields like Cosmetology (Total Beauty), Clinical Engineering, and Art & Design—disciplines that are traditionally restricted to two-year vocational schools (*Senmon Gakko*). This unique academic structure allows international students to acquire highly specialized, job-ready technical skills while securing the prestigious administrative and academic benefits of a full four-year Japanese bachelor's degree.

## 🗺️ Location & Campus Life: Shimonoseki, Yamaguchi

Situated at the westernmost tip of Japan's main island (Honshu), **Shimonoseki** is a historic and vibrant port city. It serves as a natural maritime gateway connecting Japan with the Asian mainland, particularly South Korea and China. 

### Why Shimonoseki is Perfect for International Students:
1. **Ultra-Affordable Cost of Living**: Compared to metropolitan giants like Tokyo, Kyoto, or Osaka, the cost of rent, food, and daily necessities in Shimonoseki is roughly 40% to 50% cheaper. Comfortable student apartments near the campus can be rented for as little as 25,000 to 35,000 JPY per month.
2. **Proximity to Fukuoka**: Shimonoseki is located directly across the Kanmon Straits from Kyushu. By taking the local train or Shinkansen from the nearby Shin-Shimonoseki Station, students can reach Fukuoka City—Kyushu's largest cultural, economic, and entertainment hub—in under an hour. This allows students to enjoy a quiet, focused study environment on weekdays and explore a bustling metropolitan area on weekends.
3. **Safe, Welcoming Community**: Shimonoseki is known for its exceptionally friendly locals, coastal scenic beauty, fresh seafood (especially its famous *Fugu* or blowfish), and low crime rate. The local community warmly supports international students, offering a rich environment for cultural immersion.

## 🎓 Academic Faculties & Unique Programs

The university consists of three main faculties, each housing specialized departments designed to meet modern industrial demands:

### 1. Faculty of Human Sciences (人間科学部)
This faculty focuses on the complexities of human behavior, society, health, and education. It is ideal for students interested in psychology, wellness, or sports management.
* **Department of Psychological and Clinical Sciences**: Trains future counselors and mental health professionals.
* **Department of Sports Science**: Combines physical education with sports medicine, training athletic coaches, trainers, and sports facility managers.
* **Department of Child Education and Social Welfare**: Prepares students for roles in early childhood development and community care.

### 2. Faculty of Medical Science (医療科学部)
This faculty is dedicated to training high-level healthcare professionals and nutritionists. It features state-of-the-art laboratory equipment mirroring real-world hospitals.
* **Department of Clinical Engineering (Emergency Medical Care)**: A rare and highly valuable program for international students. It trains clinical engineers who operate life-support systems (such as artificial heart-lung machines and dialysis equipment) and emergency medical technicians.
* **Department of Food and Nutrition**: Focuses on dietetics, food safety, and nutrition management, preparing students to work in hospitals, wellness centers, and the food industry.

### 3. Faculty of Art and Design (芸術学部)
Perhaps the most popular faculty among international students, this division blends traditional artistic disciplines with cutting-edge commercial arts and design.
* **Department of Art and Design (Anime, Manga, and Graphic Design)**: Offers intensive, hands-on portfolios in illustration, digital animation, graphic design, and video production, taught by active industry professionals.
* **Total Beauty Course (Cosmetology & Esthetics)**: This is a flagship program at the University of East Asia. While most beauty schools in Japan are vocational schools (which carry rigid visa restrictions and do not grant degrees), this program offers a fully accredited Bachelor's Degree. Students learn high-level skills in hair design, makeup artistry, nail care, skin therapy, and bridal styling, alongside salon management and cosmetic chemistry.
* **Architecture and Interior Design**: Focuses on spatial planning, sustainable housing, and interior styling.

## 💰 Tuition, Fees, and Scholarships

The University of East Asia strives to make higher education in Japan financially viable for students from diverse backgrounds. To this end, they offer robust financial support systems.

### Estimated Cost of Study (First Year):
* **Admission Fee**: 200,000 JPY *(Paid only once upon entry)*
* **Yearly Tuition**: 740,000 JPY *(Basic academic tuition; actual costs may vary slightly by department due to lab or studio fees)*
* **Other Fees (Insurance, association fees, etc.)**: Approx. 50,000 - 80,000 JPY

### International Student Tuition Waiver & Scholarships:
* **Tuition Reduction System**: The university offers a special tuition reduction program specifically for international students. Depending on academic performance, Japanese language proficiency (JLPT scores), and financial need, eligible students can receive a **30% to 50% waiver** on their annual tuition fees.
* **External Scholarships**: The university's international office actively helps students apply for prestigious external awards, such as the **JASSO (Japan Student Services Organization) Scholarship**, which provides monthly stipends of 48,000 JPY, and various local private foundation grants.

## 🤝 Comprehensive International Student Support

Transitioning to life in Japan can be challenging, but the University of East Asia provides a robust support network to ensure student safety, well-being, and academic success:

* **Dedicated International Center**: A specialized office staffed with multilingual advisors (English, Chinese, Vietnamese, and Korean) who assist students with visa renewals, national health insurance enrollment, opening bank accounts, and resolving personal concerns.
* **On-Campus and Partner Dormitories**: The university offers affordable, fully furnished student housing close to campus. These dormitories provide a safe environment where international students can live alongside Japanese peers, promoting natural language practice and cultural exchange.
* **Japanese Language Support**: For students who need to boost their linguistic capabilities, the university provides supplementary Japanese language courses focused on business Japanese, academic writing, and preparation for the JLPT N2 and N1 examinations.

## 💼 Career Paths & Employment Success in Japan

The ultimate goal of most international students is to secure employment in Japan after graduation. The University of East Asia maintains an outstanding track record, with an overall employment rate regularly exceeding **95%**.

### Targeted Career Support Programs:
* **One-on-One Career Counseling**: Starting from the third year, career advisors work individually with students to identify goals, refine resumes (Rirekisho), and prepare for rigorous Japanese job interviews.
* **On-Campus Job Fairs**: The university hosts regular company information sessions on campus, inviting regional employers from Yamaguchi, Hiroshima, Fukuoka, and Tokyo who are actively seeking global talent.
* **Vocational Visa Assistance**: Because graduates receive a full Bachelor's Degree, they easily qualify for the "Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services" visa. Graduates of the Total Beauty course often find employment in luxury cosmetics brands, high-end salons, bridal companies, and hotel chains, while Art and Design graduates find placement in game studios, animation houses, and design agencies across Japan.

## 📝 Admission Requirements & Process for Global Applicants

To apply for undergraduate admission as an international student, candidates typically need to fulfill the following requirements:

1. **Educational Background**: Completion of 12 years of formal education outside of Japan (or equivalent certification).
2. **Language Proficiency**: Applicants generally need to demonstrate Japanese language proficiency of at least **JLPT N3 level** or equivalent (e.g., scoring 200+ on the Japanese section of the EJU). For highly technical or creative majors, N2 is highly recommended to keep up with the coursework.
3. **Application Documents**: 
   * Completed Application Form
   * High School Graduation Certificate and Official Transcripts
   * Proof of Financial Support (to demonstrate ability to cover living costs and tuition)
   * Copy of Passport
4. **Selection Process**: Admission is determined based on a comprehensive screening of application documents, a written Japanese essay exam, and an online or in-person interview designed to evaluate the student's motivation, character, and language aptitude.
