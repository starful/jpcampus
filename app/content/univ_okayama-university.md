## üè´ University Overview & Reputation

Okayama University, a prominent national university in Japan, holds a distinguished position within the academic landscape. While not one of the 'Soukeisen' rivals (referring to Waseda and Keio), it is consistently ranked among the top national universities in Japan, often placing within the top 20-30 nationally and recognized internationally. It is recognized for its comprehensive range of faculties and its commitment to research and education. Okayama University aims to contribute to society by nurturing talented individuals and creating new knowledge. 

Founded in 1949, Okayama University has its roots in older institutions, including the Okayama Medical School, established in 1870. This rich history has shaped its academic culture and fostered a tradition of excellence. The university's founding principles emphasize contributing to the local community and broader society through education and research. This commitment is reflected in its various community engagement initiatives and its focus on addressing societal challenges through its research activities. 

Okayama University enjoys a strong academic reputation, particularly in fields like medicine, science, and engineering. It's known for its research output and the quality of its faculty. Socially, the university provides a vibrant and supportive environment for students, with numerous clubs, organizations, and extracurricular activities. Its national university status implies a strong emphasis on academic rigor and a commitment to serving the public good. The university fosters a culture of collaboration and innovation, encouraging students and researchers to work together to address complex problems. The university also prides itself on its relatively diverse student body, welcoming students from across Japan and around the world.

**Signature Programs:**

One of Okayama University's signature programs is its medical program within the **Faculty of Medicine**. The program is highly competitive and renowned for its comprehensive curriculum, experienced faculty, and state-of-the-art facilities. It emphasizes both theoretical knowledge and practical skills, providing students with extensive clinical experience through affiliated hospitals. The program also focuses on research, encouraging students to participate in research projects and contribute to advancements in medical science. This program is highly sought after by students aiming to become leading medical professionals and researchers.

Another notable program is the **Discovery Program for Global Learners**. This interdisciplinary program is specifically designed for international students and Japanese students seeking a globally oriented education. It offers a unique curriculum that combines various disciplines, such as humanities, social sciences, and natural sciences, fostering critical thinking, problem-solving, and intercultural communication skills. All courses are taught in English, providing an immersive environment for international students. The program prepares students for diverse career paths in international organizations, global businesses, and research institutions.

## üåç International Student Admissions & Life

**Admissions Process:**

International students applying to Okayama University typically need to go through a specific admissions process tailored to their background. This usually involves several key steps. Firstly, demonstrating academic eligibility by submitting transcripts from previous educational institutions. For undergraduate programs, the Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU) is often required, with specific subject requirements varying depending on the faculty. Secondly, proving English proficiency through standardized tests like TOEFL or IELTS is essential, unless the applicant's previous education was entirely in English. Many programs also require interviews, conducted either in person or via video conferencing, to assess the applicant's motivation, communication skills, and suitability for the program. Some programs also have additional requirements like essays or portfolios.

**English-Taught Programs:**

Okayama University offers a limited number of degree programs entirely taught in English, with the **Discovery Program for Global Learners** being the most prominent. This program is a four-year undergraduate program designed to provide a broad interdisciplinary education with a global perspective. Within other faculties, while complete degrees in English are rare, specific courses or research opportunities in English might be available, particularly at the graduate level. Prospective students should carefully check the program details on the university's website or contact the relevant faculty to confirm the availability of English-taught options.

**Student Support:**

Okayama University provides extensive support services for international students. The **International Affairs Department** serves as the primary point of contact, offering assistance with visa applications, housing arrangements, and general orientation to life in Japan. Academic advising is available through each faculty, helping students choose courses, plan their studies, and navigate academic requirements. Counseling services are also available to address personal or academic challenges. The university also provides Japanese language support for students who want to improve their language skills. There are also many social activities and events organized by the university and student groups to promote intercultural exchange and integration.

**Campus Life & Diversity:**

Okayama University has a diverse student body, with over 800 international students from various countries. The campus offers a welcoming and inclusive environment, with numerous student clubs and organizations catering to diverse interests, including cultural exchange, sports, and academic pursuits. The university actively promotes intercultural understanding and provides opportunities for international students to interact with Japanese students. The campus also hosts various events and festivals throughout the year, showcasing different cultures and traditions. This vibrant campus life contributes to a positive and enriching experience for international students.

**Scholarship Opportunities:**

Okayama University offers several scholarship opportunities for international students. The most prestigious is the **Japanese Government (MEXT) Scholarship**, which covers tuition fees, provides a monthly stipend, and includes travel expenses. This scholarship is highly competitive and requires a separate application process through the Japanese embassy or consulate in the applicant's home country. The university also offers its own scholarships for international students, such as the **Okayama University International Student Scholarship**, which provides partial tuition fee waivers. Additionally, some private foundations offer scholarships to international students studying in Japan, such as the **Rotary Yoneyama Memorial Foundation Scholarship** and the **Inoue International Education Foundation Scholarship**. Scholarship eligibility criteria and application deadlines vary, so students should carefully check the details on the university's website and the respective scholarship organizations.

**Cost of Living:**

The cost of living in Okayama is relatively lower compared to major cities like Tokyo or Osaka. Rent for a single apartment near the university typically ranges from 40,000 to 60,000 JPY per month. Food expenses can be estimated at around 30,000 to 40,000 JPY per month, depending on dietary habits. Transportation costs, including train and bus fares, can range from 5,000 to 10,000 JPY per month. Other expenses, such as utilities, internet, and personal items, may amount to another 10,000 to 20,000 JPY per month. Overall, a rough monthly budget estimate for international students in Okayama is around 85,000 to 130,000 JPY.
