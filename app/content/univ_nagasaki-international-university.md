## 🏫 University Overview

Nagasaki International University (NIU), established in 2000 in Sasebo, Nagasaki Prefecture, is a private institution that has quickly earned a reputation as a prominent hub for international education, hospitality, and practical sciences. Built on the core philosophy of "Respect for Humanity" (Humana Communitas), NIU is dedicated to fostering global citizens who possess both high professional skills and a deep, empathetic understanding of others. 

One of the university's most distinguishing features is its physical and institutional connection to Huis Ten Bosch, Japan's largest European-themed resort. Located immediately adjacent to the campus, this resort serves as a living laboratory for students studying tourism, hospitality, and service management. Sasebo itself is a dynamic, historic port city with a rich heritage of foreign exchange, influenced historically by European traders and modern global naval ties, making it an ideal, welcoming environment for international scholars.

At NIU, education goes beyond traditional lecturing. The university actively integrates local community revitalization projects, advanced medical science programs, and traditional cultural training into its curriculum. For international students, NIU offers a safe, affordable, and incredibly supportive environment, facilitating a smooth transition into Japanese society and paving clear pathways to global careers.

## 🎓 Academic Offerings and Specializations

NIU is structured around three distinct faculties, each offering targeted curricula designed to prepare students for real-world professional challenges.

### 1. Faculty of Human Social Studies
This faculty is the primary academic destination for international students looking to enter the tourism, hospitality, and public service sectors in Japan and abroad.

*   **Department of International Tourism Management:** This department trains students to become leaders in the global hospitality and tourism industries. The curriculum covers tourism planning, airline operations, hotel management, and cross-cultural communication. Crucially, the department leverages its proximity to Huis Ten Bosch to offer structured, credit-bearing internship programs where students gain hands-on experience in luxury hotels, theme parks, and retail operations.
*   **Department of Social Work:** Focused on creating a welfare-oriented society, this department trains certified social workers, mental health professionals, and high school teachers. International students studying here gain deep insights into Japan's advanced healthcare and elderly care systems, which are increasingly relevant worldwide.

### 2. Faculty of Health Management
Addressing the critical fields of nutrition, sports, and health promotion, this faculty combines scientific theory with extensive laboratory and field training.

*   **Department of Health and Nutrition:** This program prepares students to become registered dietitians capable of managing nutrition in hospitals, schools, and corporate environments. 
*   **Department of Sports Health and Nutrition:** This specialized track merges sports science with nutrition education, training athletic coaches, personal trainers, and health educators who can support athletes and local communities in leading healthier lifestyles.

### 3. Faculty of Pharmacy
*   **Department of Pharmacy (6-Year Program):** Dedicated to training highly skilled, compassionate pharmacists. The program emphasizes clinical pharmacy practice, pharmacology, and drug development. With state-of-the-art laboratory facilities and strong partnerships with hospitals and medical centers throughout Kyushu, students receive comprehensive clinical practice training.

## 🍵 Cultivating "Omotenashi" through Tea Ceremony (Sado)

Perhaps the most unique aspect of NIU's educational philosophy is its compulsory instruction in traditional Japanese Tea Ceremony (*Sado*). The university believes that technical knowledge must be balanced with spiritual maturity and interpersonal grace. To facilitate this, NIU constructed the "Jyushinkan," an authentic tea ceremony house on campus built with traditional materials.

Under the guidance of masters from the Urasenke school, all undergraduate students, regardless of their major, learn the intricate arts of tea preparation, calligraphy, flower arrangement, and traditional etiquette. For international students, this represents a profound opportunity to experience the core of Japanese *Omotenashi* (selfless hospitality) firsthand. This cultural education not only enriches the students' personal lives but also acts as a powerful differentiator during job interviews in Japan, where employers highly value workers who understand and respect traditional Japanese customs and social harmony.

## 🌐 International Student Support and Community

NIU boasts a vibrant, cosmopolitan campus with a high proportion of international students, mainly hailing from countries across Asia, including China, Vietnam, Nepal, South Korea, and Southeast Asia. The university has built a robust support ecosystem to ensure that foreign students feel secure, valued, and integrated from day one.

*   **The International Affairs Center:** This dedicated office acts as the primary contact point for international students, assisting with visa extensions, health insurance paperwork, part-time job permits, and accommodation arrangements. Staff members are multilingual, ensuring that students can seek help in their native languages.
*   **Japanese Language Support:** Recognizing that language proficiency is key to academic success and employment, NIU offers specialized Japanese language courses tailored to different proficiency levels. From intensive prep classes for the Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) N2 and N1 levels to specialized business Japanese, the university helps students gain the linguistic confidence required to work in corporate Japan.
*   **Cultural Exchange Programs:** The university frequently organizes international food festivals, language tables, and homestays with local families. These activities allow international students to share their heritage with Japanese classmates and Sasebo residents, building lifelong friendships and cross-cultural bridges.

## 💰 Tuition Fees, Reductions, and Financial Planning

While studying abroad represents a major investment, NIU is highly regarded for its affordability compared to private universities in Tokyo or Osaka. The university offers a generous tuition reduction system specifically designed to ease the financial burden on international students.

### Estimated Academic Costs (Human Social Studies - After Reductions)
*   **Admission Fee:** 220,000 JPY (Paid only in the first year)
*   **Annual Tuition (Standard):** Approx. 810,000 JPY
*   **International Student Tuition Waiver:** NIU routinely applies a **30% to 50% tuition reduction** for privately financed international students who maintain satisfactory academic performance and attendance. With this waiver, the annual tuition can drop to around 560,000 JPY to 400,000 JPY, making it highly competitive.

### Scholarship Opportunities
In addition to tuition reductions, international students can apply for various external and internal scholarship programs:
1.  **JASSO Honors Scholarship:** Providing monthly stipends of 48,000 JPY to students with outstanding academic records.
2.  **Nagasaki Prefecture International Student Scholarships:** Targeted grants to support regional integration.
3.  **NIU Academic Excellence Scholarships:** Internal awards that recognize top-performing students each semester.

## 🏢 Campus Life, Housing, and the Huis Ten Bosch Advantage

Living in Sasebo offers a high quality of life with a low cost of living. Compared to major metropolitan areas like Tokyo, where rent and daily expenses can be prohibitive, Sasebo allows students to live comfortably on a modest budget.

### Student Accommodation
NIU provides safe, modern dormitory accommodations located close to campus. Double and single rooms are available, equipped with high-speed internet, study desks, laundry facilities, and common areas. Rent is exceptionally reasonable, typically ranging from **20,000 to 30,000 JPY per month**, allowing students to focus on their studies without housing stress.

### The Huis Ten Bosch Connection
The university’s proximity to Huis Ten Bosch is a game-changer for student life and financial sustainability. Under Japanese law, international students can work up to 28 hours per week. Huis Ten Bosch offers numerous part-time job (*arubaito*) opportunities in hospitality, retail, translation, and theme park operations. This connection allows students to earn money to support their living costs while simultaneously practicing their Japanese language skills and gaining valuable corporate hospitality experience in a world-class environment.

## 📈 Career Development and Placement Records

Nagasaki International University has an exceptional job placement rate, regularly exceeding **95% for international graduates** who wish to remain and work in Japan. The university’s Career Center works proactively with students from their second year onward to prepare them for the competitive Japanese job market.

### Core Support Services:
*   **One-on-One Career Counseling:** Experienced counselors assist students in mapping out career goals, finding job openings, and structuring their career paths.
*   **Resume and Entry Sheet Writing Workshops:** Guidance on how to present academic achievements, part-time work, and tea ceremony training in a format that appeals to Japanese corporate recruiters.
*   **Mock Interviews:** Comprehensive practice sessions to help students master Japanese business etiquette, speech patterns, and common interview questions.
*   **On-Campus Company Information Sessions:** NIU regularly invites recruiters from hotels, travel agencies, logistics firms, retail chains, and IT corporations across Japan to recruit directly from the campus.

Graduates frequently find employment in major hospitality groups, airport ground handling agencies, international trade companies, and healthcare facilities throughout Kyushu, Tokyo, and other metropolitan centers.

## 📝 Admissions Guide for International Students

NIU holds multiple application windows throughout the year to accommodate different academic cycles. The admissions process is structured to evaluate a student's potential, motivation, and linguistic competence fairly.

### Key Admission Requirements:
1.  **Academic Eligibility:** Completion of 12 years of formal education outside of Japan (or equivalent certification).
2.  **Language Proficiency:** Applicants must generally possess Japanese language proficiency equivalent to **JLPT N2 or higher**. However, candidates with strong N3 scores who demonstrate a commitment to intensive language acquisition during their initial terms are also considered for specific programs.
3.  **Application Materials:** Submission of high school or preparatory school transcripts, graduation certificates, a statement of purpose detailing why the applicant wants to study at NIU, and proof of financial sponsorship.
4.  **Selection Examination:** The entrance exam typically consists of a document screening, a written essay test (in Japanese), and an interview. The interview is highly weighted, as faculty members look for students who are enthusiastic, respectful, and eager to engage with the unique "Omotenashi" culture of the university.

By choosing Nagasaki International University, international students do not just get a degree; they embark on a holistic educational journey that beautifully blends advanced modern science and professional training with the timeless elegance of Japanese culture.
